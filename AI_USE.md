## What AI was used for

| Task | Tool | Artifact committed |
|---|---|---|
| Literature synthesis (initial survey of 2024–2026 post-DESI-DR2 NMC papers) | Claude (Anthropic) | `paper/eci.bib` (every entry cross-checked against Crossref / arXiv APIs before inclusion) |
| Bibliographic verification (DOI resolution, author attribution, journal/vol/page) | Crossref REST API + arXiv Atom API, called from Bash/Python | `notes/v3-to-v4-corrections.md` |
| Technical self-review of equations and axiom consistency | Claude Opus reviewer persona | `docs/REVIEW_NOTES.md` |
| LaTeX scaffolding (RevTeX 4-2 skeleton, `CITATION.cff`, `.zenodo.json`) | Claude | this repository |
| Symbolic re-derivation of Section 2 equations | Cadabra2 (planned, not yet run) | `derivations/` (see README) |
| Numerical sanity checks of predictions | SymPy + NumPy (planned) | `numerics/` (see README) |

## What AI was NOT used for

- Generating claims without a verifiable source. Every in-text citation in `paper/eci.tex` maps to a `.bib` entry that was independently re-fetched from Crossref or arXiv in April 2026.
- Fabricating numerical values. All constraints ($f_{\mathrm{EDE}}$, $N_{\mathrm{eff}}$, $\dot G/G$, DESI $w_0$ / $w_a$ centrals) are pulled directly from the cited primary sources, not synthesised by the model.
- Substantive physical reasoning beyond what the cited references establish. The axiom set is the author's assembly choice; the individual programs (A1–A6) are each sourced from peer-reviewed or preprint work.

## Known limitations of the AI-augmented workflow

- **The v3 draft contained four author-attribution errors** generated by the LLM when assembling the bibliography (documented in `notes/v3-to-v4-corrections.md`). These were caught at the v3→v4 verification stage by re-fetching every arXiv ID. This is the core reason the `.bib` entry list is now much shorter and every entry has been API-verified. **LLM-generated citations must be treated as unverified until cross-checked.**
- A Cadabra2 / xAct re-derivation of the NMC stress tensor is listed as future work (`derivations/README.md`). Until it exists as a committed artifact, the equations in Section 2 are vouched for by hand-comparison with Faraoni (2000, 2004) and Birrell–Davies (1982), plus the reviewer-persona audit in `docs/REVIEW_NOTES.md`.

## Reproducibility

- LaTeX source and `.bib` are versioned in git.
- Every release is archived on Zenodo with a DOI.
- The `derivations/` and `numerics/` folders are intended to ship Jupyter notebooks so that a referee can re-run every symbolic and numerical claim.
