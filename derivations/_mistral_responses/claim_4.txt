Let's tackle each part of the problem step-by-step.

### **(A) No-ghost condition in the Jordan-frame action**

The given Jordan-frame action is:
\[
S = \int d^4x \sqrt{-g} \left[ \frac{1}{2} M_P^2 R - \frac{1}{2} (\partial \chi)^2 - V(\chi) - \frac{1}{2} \xi R \chi^2 \right].
\]
This is a **non-minimally coupled scalar-tensor theory**, where the scalar field \(\chi\) couples to the Ricci scalar \(R\) via the term \(-\frac{1}{2} \xi R \chi^2\).

#### **Step 1: Rewrite the action in a more familiar form**
The action can be rewritten as:
\[
S = \int d^4x \sqrt{-g} \left[ \frac{1}{2} \left( M_P^2 - \xi \chi^2 \right) R - \frac{1}{2} (\partial \chi)^2 - V(\chi) \right].
\]
This resembles the **Brans-Dicke theory** or **general scalar-tensor gravity**, where the effective Planck mass is field-dependent:
\[
M_{\text{eff}}^2 = M_P^2 - \xi \chi^2.
\]

#### **Step 2: Ghost condition**
A **ghost** is a field with a wrong-sign kinetic term (negative kinetic energy). To avoid ghosts, the kinetic term of the **graviton** (which is encoded in the coefficient of \(R\)) must be positive. This requires:
\[
M_{\text{eff}}^2 = M_P^2 - \xi \chi^2 > 0.
\]
Thus, the **no-ghost condition** is:
\[
\boxed{M_P^2 - \xi \chi^2 > 0}.
\]
This ensures that the graviton has a healthy kinetic term.

### **(B) Late-time accelerating attractor for exponential quintessence**

We consider a **minimally coupled** scalar field with an exponential potential:
\[
V(\chi) = V_0 \exp\left( -\alpha \frac{\chi}{M_P} \right).
\]
We want to find the condition on \(\alpha\) for a **late-time accelerating scalar-dominated attractor** and compute the equation of state \(w_\phi\) at the fixed point.

#### **Step 1: Equations of motion**
The Friedmann equations for a flat FLRW universe with a scalar field are:
\[
H^2 = \frac{1}{3 M_P^2} \left( \frac{1}{2} \dot{\chi}^2 + V(\chi) \right),
\]
\[
\dot{H} = -\frac{1}{2 M_P^2} \dot{\chi}^2.
\]
The Klein-Gordon equation for \(\chi\) is:
\[
\ddot{\chi} + 3 H \dot{\chi} + V_{,\chi} = 0.
\]

#### **Step 2: Define dimensionless variables**
To analyze the attractor, we define:
\[
x = \frac{\dot{\chi}}{\sqrt{6} M_P H}, \quad y = \frac{\sqrt{V}}{\sqrt{3} M_P H}.
\]
The Friedmann equation becomes:
\[
x^2 + y^2 = 1 \quad (\text{since } \Omega_\phi = 1 \text{ for scalar domination}).
\]
The equation of state of the scalar field is:
\[
w_\phi = \frac{\frac{1}{2} \dot{\chi}^2 - V}{\frac{1}{2} \dot{\chi}^2 + V} = x^2 - y^2.
\]

#### **Step 3: Autonomous system**
The evolution equations for \(x\) and \(y\) are:
\[
x' = -3x + \lambda \sqrt{\frac{3}{2}} y^2 + \frac{3}{2} x (2x^2 + \gamma (1 - x^2 - y^2)),
\]
\[
y' = -\lambda \sqrt{\frac{3}{2}} x y + \frac{3}{2} y (2x^2 + \gamma (1 - x^2 - y^2)),
\]
where \(\lambda = -M_P \frac{V_{,\chi}}{V} = \alpha\) (for the exponential potential), and \(\gamma = 1\) for matter domination (but here we assume scalar domination, so \(\gamma\) drops out).

For a **scalar-dominated attractor**, we set \(x' = y' = 0\) and \(x^2 + y^2 = 1\). Solving the fixed-point equations gives:
\[
x = \frac{\lambda}{\sqrt{6}}, \quad y = \sqrt{1 - \frac{\lambda^2}{6}}.
\]

#### **Step 4: Acceleration condition**
For acceleration, we require \(w_\phi < -1/3\). Using \(w_\phi = x^2 - y^2\):
\[
w_\phi = \frac{\lambda^2}{6} - \left(1 - \frac{\lambda^2}{6}\right) = \frac{\lambda^2}{3} - 1.
\]
Thus, the condition for acceleration is:
\[
\frac{\lambda^2}{3} - 1 < -\frac{1}{3} \implies \lambda^2 < 2.
\]
Since \(\lambda = \alpha\), the condition is:
\[
\boxed{\alpha < \sqrt{2}}.
\]

#### **Step 5: Equation of state at the fixed point**
From above, the equation of state at the fixed point is:
\[
w_\phi = \frac{\alpha^2}{3} - 1.
\]
Thus:
\[
\boxed{w_\phi = \frac{\alpha^2}{3} - 1}.
\]

---

### **Summary of Results**
- **(A)** No-ghost condition: \(\boxed{M_P^2 - \xi \chi^2 > 0}\).
- **(B)** For a late-time accelerating attractor: \(\boxed{\alpha < \sqrt{2}}\), with \(w_\phi = \boxed{\frac{\alpha^2}{3} - 1}\).
