The evaluation of the five strategies for extending the T2 proof (past Big Bang non-cyclic-separating) to Bianchi cosmologies is summarized below.

### 1. Strategy Analysis

| Strategy | Plausibility | Obstruction | Bianchi Types | Tractability |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

### 2. Strategy Breakdown

*   **(S1) Volume-element divergence:** In FRW radiation, $\sqrt{-g} \propto t^{3/2}$. In Kasner, $\sqrt{-g} \propto t^1$. Since $t^1$ is *more* singular than $t^{3/2}$, the IR divergence of the smeared two-point function $W(f, f)$—which is the root cause of cyclic-separating failure in FRW—is strictly more severe in Bianchi. This is the most direct physical extension.
*   **(S2) E10/BKL Framework:** While the $E_{10}$ symmetry of BKL Bianchi IX is mathematically profound, recent results (Hartnoll-Yang 2025) suggest the resulting "primon gas" wavefunctions are associated with abelian Hecke algebras. This creates a "Type I" bottleneck that is incompatible with the crossed-product (Type II) framework of AWCH.
*   **(S3) Direct Wightman Failure:** This is the "brute force" path. By constructing a test function $f$ supported near the $t=0$ singularity in a Kasner metric, one can show $\langle f | W | f \rangle \to \infty$. If the state is not well-defined on the boundary, it cannot be cyclic-separating. This is the most rigorous path for Bianchi I.
*   **(S4) Tod Isotropic Singularity:** Tod (1987) proved that Isotropic $\iff$ Conformal Flatness (Weyl $\to 0$). The contrapositive is powerful: if a singularity is anisotropic (generic Bianchi), then Weyl is non-zero and divergent. One then argues that this "Weyl divergence" prevents the existence of any Hadamard-equivalent state on the boundary, thus forbidding cyclic-separating.
*   **(S5) Holographic Bound:** $S \leq A/4G \to 0$ at the past horizon is a strong physical motivator. A state with zero entropy (or zero entanglement) cannot be cyclic-separating for the local algebra of a causal diamond. However, this relies on the validity of the GSL near a singularity, which is less certain than pure QFT.

---

### 3. Overall Verdict & Recommended Path

**The best result is achieved by combining (S3) and (S4).**

1.  **Use (S3)** to prove the failure for **Bianchi I (Kasner)** via direct IR divergence. This is tractable because the mode-decomposition for Kasner is well-known (Pereira et al. 2018), and the SymPy-verified divergence of $C_{abcd} \propto t^{-8/3}$ provides the necessary "singular pressure."
2.  **Use (S4) (Tod's Theorem)** to generalize. Since any non-FRW Bianchi singularity is non-isotropic, it must be Weyl-divergent. By the **local quasi-equivalence principle** (Brunetti-Fredenhagen-Verch), any such singularity is locally modeled by a Kasner regime (BKL phase). The failure proven in (S3) then propagates to all Bianchi types.

This combination avoids the "BKL chaos" trap by focusing on the **geometric boundary** (Tod) and the **local operator algebra** (Kasner/S3), rather than the chaotic trajectory between them.
