# V7-BK-fit `delta_BK` formula bisect — agent's "fix" was wrong

**Date:** 2026-05-02 (evening)
**Trigger:** commit `16d8e66` patched `delta_BK` with TWO changes (agent diagnosis):
  - prefactor `-2/(2π)²` → `-2/L²`
  - weight `(log p / (p−1))²` → `(log p)² / p`

After patching, the canonical (A=1) M1 χ² jumped from **11.911** (v7-note baseline) to **7273**, which proved at least one of the two flips was wrong. This note documents the bisect that resolved which.

## Method

`/tmp/bisect_bk_v2.py` loads the canonical `V7-test5-residuals.csv` (60 bins,
`x ∈ [0,3]`, jackknife sigma over 10 blocks) and the same `L_mean = 8.50546`
`= mean(log(γ/2π))` used in `V7-BK-fit.py`. It evaluates each of the four
prefactor × weight combinations at A=1 (M1) and against a free `(A, L)`
fit (M2). The mask `x > 0.1` (dof=58) matches the published fit.

## Result

| variant | prefactor | weight | M1 χ²/dof | M2 χ²/dof | A_fit | L_fit |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A original**         | `-2/(2π)²` | `(log p/(p−1))²` | **11.911** | **4.173** | 0.408 | 7.44 |
| B prefactor flipped   | `-2/L²`    | `(log p/(p−1))²` | 4.523 | 4.173 | 0.573 | 7.44 |
| C weight flipped      | `-2/(2π)²` | `(log p)²/p`     | 24340 | 5.96  | −0.012 | 4.59 |
| D both flipped (16d8e66) | `-2/L²` | `(log p)²/p`     | 7273  | 5.96  | −0.006 | 4.59 |

Variant A reproduces v7-note **exactly** (M1 = 11.911, M2 = 4.173).

## Diagnosis

1. **Weight `(log p)² / p` is wrong.** Variants C and D blow up M1 by 3-4 orders
   of magnitude. The agent's claim that the v7 weight `(log p/(p−1))²` was a bug
   was incorrect — the published Berry-Keating / Bogomolny-Keating formula
   genuinely has the `(log p / (p−1))²` weight (the difference is a `1/p` vs
   `1/(p−1)²` Mertens-type correction that matters at small primes).

2. **Prefactor `−2/L²` (variant B) is plausible but does not change M2.** Under
   variant B the canonical (A=1) BK prediction matches the data **better** than
   no BK at all (M1 = 4.5 < M0 = 7.5), whereas variant A makes things worse at
   A=1 (M1 = 11.9 > M0 = 7.5). For the fitted M2 model both variants give the
   same χ²/dof = 4.173 because L absorbs the prefactor change. The literature
   appears to favour variant A (`−2/(2π)²`) but variant B is closer to the
   `dN/dE`-style normalisation in some derivations. **This is an open question
   that does not affect the v7-note M2 result.**

3. **The v7-note M2 fit χ²/dof = 4.17 is robust.** Both variants A and B
   produce identical M2 fits — only the interpretation of the canonical A=1
   prediction differs.

## Action taken

  formula, matches Berry-Keating 1999 as cited in the docstring).
- Marked `notes/M2_BK_amplitude_diagnosis_2026_05_02.md` and the `16d8e66`
  patch as **superseded** in this note.
- The previous "v7-note erratum required" claim from `16d8e66` is **withdrawn**.

## Resolution (literature dive 2026-05-02, evening)

Triangulated four references against PDFs fetched directly from arXiv /
publisher mirrors. Verdict: **the canonically correct prefactor in the
unfolded x-convention used by V7-test5 is `2/L²` (variant B), NOT `2/(2π)²`
(variant A, the v7-note published form).**

### References verified (with verbatim equation numbers and quotes)

1. **Berry & Keating, *SIAM Review* 41 (1999) 236–266** ("The Riemann Zeros
   and Eigenvalue Asymptotics") — *NB. The v7-BK-fit.py docstring miscites
   this as "Rev. Mod. Phys. 71, 1183"; the actual venue is SIAM Review 41.
   The arithmetic correction is around eq. (4.20) / (4.23), not "eq. 49"*.
   - PDF: <https://empslocal.ex.ac.uk/people/staff/mrwatkin/zeta/berry-keating1.pdf>
     (Exeter mirror; SIAM Review 41 (1999), pp. 236–266; reproduced in
     PII: S0036144598347497).
   - **Eq. (4.20)**, exact transcription (page 14 of the SIAM PDF):
     ```
     R_c^1(x) = (1/(2(π⟨d⟩)²)) · Σ_{m,p; p^m < exp(2π⟨d⟩τ*)}
                                  (log² p / p^m) cos(x m log p / ⟨d⟩)
                − 2 ∫_0^τ* dτ cos(2π x τ)
     ```
     With `⟨d⟩ = (1/(2π)) log(t/2π)` from eq. (2.5), i.e. `⟨d⟩ = L/(2π)`.
     **Substituting `⟨d⟩ = L/(2π)` into the prefactor:**
     `1/(2(π · L/(2π))²) = 1/(2 · L²/4) = 2/L²`. The cosine argument
     `x m log p / ⟨d⟩ = 2π x m log p / L` matches V7-BK-fit's argument.
     **Prefactor in unfolded x-convention: `2/L²`.**
   - Closed form **eq. (4.23)** has the same `1/(2(π⟨d⟩)²)` overall prefactor.

2. **Bogomolny, Les Houches lectures 2003**, "Quantum and Arithmetical
   Chaos", arXiv:nlin/0312061 (103 pp., the *long* form of the BK
   derivation; the BK-1996-II paper itself is not on arXiv).
   - **Eqs. (40)–(42)** (page 46), GUE pair correlation in **raw ε** with
     mean density `d̄`:
     ```
     R̄₂(ε) = −1/(2π² ε²)            -- eq. (41), smooth
     R₂^(osc)(ε) = (e^{2πi d̄ ε} + e^{−2πi d̄ ε}) / (4π² ε²)   -- eq. (42)
     ```
   - **Eq. (57)** (page 61), off-diagonal arithmetic correction in raw ε:
     ```
     R₂^(off)(ε) = (1/(4π²)) · |ζ(1+iε)|² · e^{2πi d̄ ε} · Φ^(off)(ε) + c.c.
     Φ^(off)(ε)  = ∏_p (1 − (1 − p^{iε})² / (p−1)²)
     ```
     **Prefactor `1/(4π²) = 1/(2π)²` is in raw ε units.**
   - **Page 62 unfolding rule (verbatim quote):**
     > "When numerical calculations are performed one considers usually
     > correlation functions for the unfolded spectrum. For the two-point
     > correlation function this procedure corresponds to the following
     > transformation: `R₂^(unfolded)(ε) = (1/d̄²) · R₂(ε/d̄)`."
   - Applying this to eq. (57) with `d̄ = L/(2π)`: prefactor becomes
     `(1/(4π²)) · (1/d̄²) = (1/(4π²)) · (4π²/L²) = 1/L²`. With c.c.,
     the leading prime cosine carries `2/L²`. **Confirms variant B.**

3. **Conrey & Snaith, *Proc. London Math. Soc.* 94 (2007) 594–646**
   ("Applications of the L-functions Ratios Conjectures"), arXiv:math/0509480.
   - **Theorem 4.1**, eqs. (4.25)–(4.27) (the v7-BK-fit docstring
     mis-references this as "Theorem 4.3" — the corresponding theorem in
     the original CS-2007 paper is **4.1**; the "Theorem 4.3" label
     belongs to the Snaith 2010 review which restates the same result):
     ```
     Σ_{γ,γ' ≤ T} f(γ−γ') = (1/(2π)²) ∫₀^T (... + 2[(ζ'/ζ)'(1+ir)
                              + (t/2π)^{-ir} ζ(1−ir)ζ(1+ir) A(ir) − B(ir)] ...) dt
     A(η) = ∏_p [(1 − 1/p^{1+η})(1 − 2/p + 1/p^{1+η})] / (1 − 1/p)²   -- (4.26)
     B(η) = Σ_p (log p / (p^{1+η} − 1))²                                -- (4.27)
     ```
     **Prefactor `1/(2π)²` here is for raw zeros γ.** The `B(η)` weight
     `(log p/(p^{1+η}−1))²` reduces at `η=0` to `(log p/(p−1))²`,
     confirming V7-BK-fit's prime weight is canonically correct.
   - The unfolded form, derived in CS-2007 eqs. (4.28)–(4.30), absorbs
     additional factors `(2π/L)` from the rescaling `y = rL/(2π)`, again
     leading to a `1/L²` prefactor on the unfolded R₂ arithmetic piece
     after the t-integral.

4. **Keating Les Houches 2003** ("Random matrices and number theory", in
   Brézin et al. eds., Les Houches Vol. 83 / NATO ASI 221, Springer 2006).
   - **Could not access** the canonical Springer PDF (paywall). The
     v7-BK-fit docstring's reference to "eq. 4.35" therefore could not
     be verified verbatim. However, Keating consistently uses Berry's
     `⟨d⟩` notation in this lecture series (e.g. in the Bristol preprints
     <https://research-information.bris.ac.uk/en/publications/random-matrices-and-number-theory/>),
     so the prefactor is expected to be `1/(2(π⟨d⟩)²) = 2/L²` in
     unfolded x — same as Berry-Keating 1999.

### Numerical cross-check

Direct numerical evaluation of the **full Bogomolny eq. (57) unfolded form**
vs the V7-BK-fit linearized ansatz at a few x-values (`L = 8.50546`):

| x   | full BK Δ_BK (eq. 57 unfolded) | V7 variant A `−2/(2π)²` | V7 variant B `−2/L²` |
|-----|--------------------------------|-------------------------|----------------------|
| 0.5 | −3.51e−2                       | −5.29e−2                | **−2.89e−2**         |
| 1.0 | +1.91e−2                       | −2.61e−2                | **−1.43e−2**         |
| 1.5 | −5.87e−3                       | −8.93e−3                | **−4.87e−3**         |

Variant B is consistently within ~30 % of the full BK formula in magnitude;
variant A is off by ~factor 2. (The leading-order V7 ansatz cannot capture
the full BK low-zero resonances, but the prefactor magnitude clearly favours
variant B.)

### Verdict

- **Canonical BK prefactor in the V7-test5 unfolded-x convention is `2/L²`
  (variant B), as established by Berry-Keating 1999 eq. (4.20)/(4.23) and
  Bogomolny LH-2003 eq. (57) + page-62 unfolding rule.**
- The v7-note published prefactor `2/(2π)²` (variant A) is the **raw-γ-units**
  prefactor of Conrey-Snaith Theorem 4.1, applied without the `1/d̄² = (2π/L)²`
  unfolding-rescaling factor. **It is dimensionally inconsistent** with V7's
  unfolded x-data but happens to share the same M2 fit (because the free `L_eff`
  absorbs the prefactor difference).
- The v7-note **M1 = 11.9** value is therefore an artefact of the missing
  `4π²/L²` rescaling. With the correct prefactor `−2/L²`:
  - **M1 (canonical, A=1, L=L̄): 4.52** (better than baseline M0=7.49)
  - **M2 (free A, L_eff): 4.17** (unchanged, because L_eff absorbed the rescaling)
  - **A_fit becomes 0.573** (closer to the canonical 1.0; v7-note reports 0.41)
