\paragraph{Wolf 2025 NMC vanilla action.}
\citet{wolf2025nmc} fit the Jordan-frame scalar-tensor action
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{S}_{\rm Wolf} = \int d^4x\,\sqrt{-g}\left[
   \frac{M_{\rm Pl}^2}{2}\,F(\varphi)\,R
   - \tfrac{1}{2}\,G(\varphi)\,X
   - V(\varphi)
   - J(\varphi)\,X^2
   + \mathcal{L}_m
\right],
\qquad X \equiv \partial_\mu\varphi\,\partial^\mu\varphi,
\label{eq:wolf-action-general}
\end{equation}
with the explicit functional choices
\begin{align}
F(\varphi) &\simeq 1 - \xi\,\frac{\varphi^2}{M_{\rm Pl}^2}, &
V(\varphi) &\simeq V_0 + \beta\,\varphi + \tfrac{1}{2}\,m^2\,\varphi^2, \\
G(\varphi) &= 1, &
J(\varphi) &= 0.
\label{eq:wolf-functionals}
\end{align}
The constant $V_0$ is not a free parameter; it is tuned to close the Friedmann
equation today. The kinetic-functional choice $G=1$ and $J=0$ removes both
$k$-mouflage and the $X^2$ Galileon term from the analysed model — Vainshtein
screening via the $X^2$ term is mentioned in their discussion only as a possible
UV completion. The non-minimal coupling enters exclusively through the
Brans-Dicke / Horndeski-$G_4$ branch.
