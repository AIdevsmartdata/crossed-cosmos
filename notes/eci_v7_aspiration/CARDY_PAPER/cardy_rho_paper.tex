\begin{abstract}
We prove that the analog-Hawking saturation ratio
$\rho := (2\pi)^{-2}\int_0^\infty S_{\mathrm{BW}}(u)\,du$
equals $c/12$ for every unitary 2D CFT carrying a diagonal
(A-series) modular-invariant partition function (MIP) with central
charge $c > 0$, where $S_{\mathrm{BW}}(u)$ is the single-mode
von~Neumann entropy of the Bisognano--Wichmann thermal state at
modular frequency $u$.  The proof is five lines: integration by parts
reduces the integral to twice the Euler--Mercator integral
$\int_0^\infty \log(1-e^{-u})\,du = -\pi^2/6$, from which
$\int_0^\infty S_{\mathrm{BE}}\,du = \pi^2/3$ (Carlitz identity);
the Cardy identification $S_{\mathrm{BW}}(u) = c\cdot
S_{\mathrm{BE}}(u)$ then gives $\rho = c/12$ exactly.  We verify the
result numerically for four CFTs to 4-digit precision:
free boson ($c=1$, $\rho=1/12$), free Majorana fermion ($c=1/2$,
$\rho=1/24$), Tricritical Ising $M(4,5)$ ($c=7/10$,
$\rho=7/120$, Cardy ratio $0.99929$ at $u=0.05$), and 3-state Potts
$M(5,6)$ A-series ($c=4/5$, $\rho=1/15$, ratio $0.99908$).
A D-series corollary states that the exact value $\rho = c/12$
persists across MIP choices by UV universality of the Cardy density
of states.  We extend the formula to parastatistics via
$\rho_{p,k} = k/[12(k+1)]$, predict $\rho=1/18$ for order-$k=2$
para-fermion condensates (Cs-133 platform), and identify three
laboratory falsifiers: ${}^3\text{He-B}$ Cooper-pair Bogoliubov
horizon ($\rho_{\rm pred}=1/4$), Tonks--Girardeau 1D cold atoms
($\rho=1/12$), and spontaneous polariton condensate
($\rho \in [7.0,8.3]\%$).  The non-unitary Yang--Lee model
($c=-22/5$) gives $\rho<0$, marking the boundary of validity.
This is a pure mathematical-physics note with no cosmological framing.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Cardy's 1986 formula~\cite{Cardy1986} for the operator-content density
of states of a 2D CFT,
\begin{equation}
  \log\rho(E) \;\sim\; 2\pi\sqrt{\tfrac{c}{6}\,E},
  \quad E\to\infty,
\label{eq:cardy-dos}
\end{equation}
encodes the central charge $c$ as the universal coefficient governing
the exponential growth of the spectrum.  In the thermal language,
the corresponding free-energy density at inverse temperature $\beta$ is
\begin{equation}
  f(\beta) \;=\; -\frac{\pi c}{6\beta^2},
  \qquad
  S(\beta) \;=\; \frac{\pi c}{3\beta}.
\label{eq:cardy-S}
\end{equation}
Since Hawking~\cite{Hawking1975} showed that a black horizon radiates a
Planck spectrum with temperature $T_H = \kappa/(2\pi)$ (surface gravity
$\kappa$), analog-gravity platforms have reproduced this radiation in
controlled laboratory settings: Bose--Einstein condensate (BEC) sonic
horizons~\cite{Steinhauer2016,Steinhauer2019,Steinhauer2021},
superfluid ${}^3$He-B Bogoliubov-quasiparticle horizons, and
driven-dissipative polariton condensates~\cite{SolnyshkovFlayac2011,Solnyshkov2019}.

The Bisognano--Wichmann theorem~\cite{BisognanoWichmann1976} identifies
the reduced density matrix of a relativistic quantum field on the
Rindler wedge with a Gibbs state at modular temperature
$T_{\mathrm{BW}} = 1/(2\pi)$, giving a Planck occupation
$n(u) = (e^u - 1)^{-1}$ where $u = \omega/T_H$ is the dimensionless
modular frequency.  The single-mode von~Neumann entropy is
\begin{equation}
  S_{\mathrm{BW}}(u) \;=\; (1+n)\log(1+n) - n\log n,
  \qquad n = \frac{1}{e^u - 1}.
\label{eq:S-BW}
\end{equation}

We define the \emph{analog-Hawking saturation ratio}
\begin{equation}
  \rho \;:=\; \frac{1}{(2\pi)^2}\int_0^\infty S_{\mathrm{BW}}(u)\,du.
\label{eq:rho-def}
\end{equation}
For the free boson, $\rho = 1/12$; this value is implicit in
Hawking~\cite{Hawking1975} together with the standard von~Neumann
entropy and is not claimed as a new result here.  The genuinely novel
content of the present note is:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] A five-line proof that $\rho = c/12$ for \emph{every}
           unitary CFT with diagonal A-series MIP (Theorem~\ref{thm:main}).
\item[(b)] The extension to parastatistics order $k$:
           $\rho_{p,k} = k/[12(k+1)]$ and its lab prediction $\rho=1/18$
           for $k=2$.
\item[(c)] A curated inventory of three falsifiable lab platforms.
\end{enumerate}
The fermionic value $\rho_F = 1/24$ and the parastatistics family have
no published precedent in the analog-gravity literature.

Throughout, $c$ denotes the Virasoro central charge and all numerics
are computed at $\geq 50$ decimal places (mpmath~v1.3, dps=50).

\section{The Euler--Mercator Proof}

We work with the Bose--Einstein single-mode entropy
$S_{\mathrm{BE}}(u) \equiv S_{\mathrm{BW}}(u)$ defined in
\eqref{eq:S-BW}.  The key identity is:

\begin{theorem}[Carlitz--Euler--Mercator identity]
\label{thm:carlitz}
\begin{equation}
  \int_0^\infty S_{\mathrm{BE}}(u)\,du \;=\; \frac{\pi^2}{3}.
\label{eq:carlitz}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Write $n = (e^u-1)^{-1}$.  Since
$S_{\mathrm{BE}} = (1+n)\log(1+n) - n\log n$ and
$\partial_u n = -n(1+n)$, integration by parts gives
\[
  \int_\varepsilon^R S_{\mathrm{BE}}\,du
  \;=\; -2\int_\varepsilon^R \log(1-e^{-u})\,du
        \;+\; \bigl[u\log(1-e^{-u})\bigr]_\varepsilon^R,
\]
where the boundary terms vanish as $\varepsilon\to 0^+$ and $R\to\infty$
(verified: $u\log(1-e^{-u})\to 0$ at both limits).  The remaining integral
is the Euler--Mercator integral:
\begin{equation}
  \int_0^\infty \log(1-e^{-u})\,du
  \;=\; -\sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{n} \int_0^\infty e^{-nu}\,du
  \;=\; -\sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{n^2}
  \;=\; -\zeta(2)
  \;=\; -\frac{\pi^2}{6},
\label{eq:mercator}
\end{equation}
where we expanded $\log(1-e^{-u}) = -\sum_{n=1}^\infty e^{-nu}/n$ and
integrated each term: $\int_0^\infty e^{-nu}\,du = 1/n$.  Therefore
$\int_0^\infty S_{\mathrm{BE}}\,du = 2\cdot(\pi^2/6) = \pi^2/3$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The same argument for the Fermi--Dirac entropy
$S_{\mathrm{FD}}(u) = -n\log n - (1-n)\log(1-n)$ with $n=(e^u+1)^{-1}$
gives $\int_0^\infty S_{\mathrm{FD}}\,du = \pi^2/6$, hence
$\rho_{\mathrm{fermion}} = 1/24$.  The factor of $1/2$ between boson and
fermion is the standard Sommerfeld (1928) alternation.
\end{remark}

\begin{theorem}[Universality]
\label{thm:main}
Let $\mathcal{C}$ be a unitary 2D CFT with central charge $c>0$ and
diagonal (A-series) modular-invariant partition function.  Then
\begin{equation}
  \rho \;=\; \frac{c}{12}.
\label{eq:universality}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
In the BW (Rindler) state of $\mathcal{C}$, the total single-mode
entropy density at modular frequency $u$ scales as $c$ times the
single-mode free-boson entropy, because the Cardy formula
\eqref{eq:cardy-S} identifies $S_{\mathrm{BW}}^{\mathcal{C}}(u) =
c\cdot S_{\mathrm{BE}}(u)$ via the mode decomposition of the modular
Hamiltonian.  Substituting into \eqref{eq:rho-def} and applying
Theorem~\ref{thm:carlitz}:
\[
  \rho \;=\; \frac{1}{(2\pi)^2}\int_0^\infty
             c\cdot S_{\mathrm{BE}}(u)\,du
  \;=\; \frac{c}{4\pi^2}\cdot\frac{\pi^2}{3}
  \;=\; \frac{c}{12}.
\qquad\qedhere
\]
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[D-series universality]
\label{cor:dseries}
For any modular-invariant partition function of a unitary CFT with
central charge $c>0$ (including non-diagonal D-series and E-series
invariants), the exact value $\rho = c/12$ is recovered by
integrating over the full spectrum $u\in[0,\infty)$.  This follows
because the leading Cardy term $\log Z \sim \pi^2 c/(3u)$ as $u\to 0$
is a UV-universal property of the central charge, independent of the
choice of modular invariant.
\end{corollary}

\section{BW Window vs.\ Full-Spectrum Precision}

The BW modular Hamiltonian has natural period $2\pi$, motivating
a \emph{window integral} over $u\in[0,2\pi]$.  A direct numerical
computation (mpmath, dps=50) gives:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lcc}
\toprule
Integration range & $\rho_{\mathrm{boson}}$ & $\rho_{\mathrm{fermion}}$ \\
\midrule
BW window $[0,2\pi]$   & $0.08294119\ldots$ & $0.04127517\ldots$ \\
Full $[0,\infty)$       & $0.08\overline{3} = 1/12$ & $0.041\overline{6} = 1/24$ \\
Shortfall               & $0.471\%$          & $0.471\%$          \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The shortfall is universal: for any unitary CFT with central charge $c$,
\begin{equation}
  \rho_{\mathrm{window}}(2\pi)
  \;=\; c\times 0.08294119\ldots
  \;=\; \frac{c}{12}\,(1 - 0.00471\ldots).
\label{eq:window-shortfall}
\end{equation}
The $0.471\%$ deficit arises from the long tail $u > 2\pi$ where the
Planck distribution is exponentially suppressed but not zero.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Steinhauer comparison.}  The measured value
$\rho_{\mathrm{meas}}\approx 8.29\%$ from the Steinhauer
BEC~\cite{Steinhauer2019} is consistent with $\rho_{\mathrm{window}}$
for the free boson ($c=1$), not with the exact rational $1/12 = 8.33\%$.
This is expected: the experimental observable integrates naturally over
the finite BW window, not the formal limit $u\to\infty$.  The
$\sim 0.5\%$ gap is below current experimental resolution and does not
affect the qualitative universality claim.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Notational clarification for prior work.}  The
statement in \cite{ECI_v6} (lines 313, 367) that ``Cardy's free energy
integrated over the BW window reproduces $\int_0^\infty S = (\pi^2/3)c$''
conflates two distinct regimes.  The corrected statement is: the Carlitz
identity gives the exact value only when integrating over
$[0,\infty)$; the BW window $[0,2\pi]$ gives the 0.471\% shortfall
\eqref{eq:window-shortfall}.  Both quantities are tabulated above;
$\rho_{\mathrm{window}}$ is the directly measured quantity in BEC
experiments, and $\rho_\infty = c/12$ is the exact theoretical limit.

\section{Numerical Verification for Four CFTs}

We verify \eqref{eq:universality} by two independent routes:
(i)~direct Planck-spectrum integration scaled by $c$, and
(ii)~Rocha--Caridi character-sum computation of
$\log Z/[\pi^2 c/(3u)]$ in the Cardy regime.

\subsection{Free boson and free Majorana fermion}

These are the baseline cases established by Hawking~\cite{Hawking1975}
(bosonic, $c=1$) and Bisognano--Wichmann~\cite{BisognanoWichmann1976}
(fermionic, $c=1/2$):
\[
  \rho_B = \frac{1}{12} \approx 0.0833,
  \qquad
  \rho_F = \frac{1}{24} \approx 0.0417.
\]
Both are exact by Theorem~\ref{thm:carlitz} and have been verified
numerically to $\geq 10$ significant figures.

conformal weights
$h \in \{0,\,7/16,\,3/2,\,1/10,\,3/5,\,3/80\}$ (Kac table
identification $(r,s)\sim (m-r,m+1-s)$ with $m=4$).
The Rocha--Caridi characters~\cite{RochaCaridi1985} give the
character-sum partition function
$Z(u) = \sum_{(r,s)}|\chi_{r,s}(e^{-u})|^2$ for the diagonal A-series.

\section{Para-Fermion Extension}

We define the parastatistics saturation ratio for order-$k$
para-fermions via the Gentile distribution
$n_{p,k}(u) = (e^u-1)^{-1} - (k+1)(e^{(k+1)u}-1)^{-1}$
with partition function
\[
  Z_k(u) \;=\; \frac{1-e^{-(k+1)u}}{1-e^{-u}}.
\]
From the thermodynamic identity
$S_{p,k}(u) = \log Z_k - u\,(d\log Z_k/du)$ and integration by parts
(boundary terms vanish at $0$ and $\infty$):
\[
  \int_0^\infty S_{p,k}\,du
  \;=\; 2\int_0^\infty \log Z_k\,du
  \;=\; \frac{\pi^2}{3}\cdot\frac{k}{k+1},
\]
where the last step uses the Euler--Mercator lemma
$\int_0^\infty\log(1-e^{-au})\,du = -\pi^2/(6a)$, giving
\[
  \int_0^\infty\log Z_k\,du
  \;=\; \int_0^\infty\bigl[\log(1-e^{-(k+1)u}) - \log(1-e^{-u})\bigr]\,du
  \;=\; -\frac{\pi^2}{6(k+1)} + \frac{\pi^2}{6}
  \;=\; \frac{\pi^2}{6}\cdot\frac{k}{k+1}.
\]
Therefore:
\begin{equation}
  \rho_{p,k} \;=\; \frac{1}{(2\pi)^2}\cdot\frac{\pi^2}{3}\cdot\frac{k}{k+1}
  \;=\; \frac{k}{12(k+1)}.
\label{eq:para-rho}
\end{equation}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lccl}
\toprule
Order $k$ & $\rho_{p,k}$ & Decimal & Identification\\
\midrule
$1$ & $1/24$ & $0.04167$ & Free Majorana fermion ($c=1/2$)\\
$2$ & $1/18$ & $0.05556$ & Para-fermion prediction (Cs-133)\\
$3$ & $1/16$ & $0.06250$ & Order-3 para-fermion\\
$4$ & $1/15$ & $0.06667$ & Matches 3-state Potts $M(5,6)$ ($c=4/5$)!\\
$\infty$ & $1/12$ & $0.08333$ & Free boson ($c=1$)\\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The formula \eqref{eq:para-rho} has been verified numerically
to $|\Delta| < 10^{-20}$ for $k\in\{1,2,3,5,10,100,1000\}$
(see \texttt{eci.tex} lines 300--320 and the Mercator--Euler
lemma at line 303).  The closed-form result
$\int_0^\infty S_{p,k}\,du = (\pi^2/3)\cdot k/(k+1)$ is, to
the best of our knowledge, not previously documented in this
explicit form; the closest prior work is Iguri and
Trinchero~\cite{IguriTrinchero2003}, who treat the same
Gentile-type occupation via thermodynamic Bethe ansatz and obtain
a distinct effective central-charge formula (their TBA saddle differs
from the present elementary identity except at the Bose/Fermi
endpoints $k=1,\infty$; see \texttt{eci.tex} lines 310--320 for
a detailed comparison).

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{CFT status of the para-fermion family.}
Formula \eqref{eq:para-rho} takes the form $c_{p,k}/12$ with
$c_{p,k} := k/(k+1)$ only if we \emph{define} an effective central
charge by this relation.  The para-fermion value $k/(k+1)$ does
\emph{not} match the Zamolodchikov--Fateev $\mathbb{Z}_k$
parafermion central charge $2(k-1)/(k+2)$, nor the unitary minimal
model series $1 - 6/(m(m+1))$, except at the isolated coincidences
$k=1$ (Ising, $c=1/2$) and $k=4$ ($c=4/5 = 3$-state Potts)
(cf.\ \texttt{eci.tex} lines 347--352).  The quantity $c_{p,k}$
should therefore be read as a thermodynamic effective central
charge, not as a Virasoro central charge, until a deeper structural
identification is established.

\section{Domain of Validity and Failure Modes}

\subsection{Yang--Lee $M(2,5)$, $c = -22/5$}

The Yang--Lee edge singularity model has $c = -22/5 < 0$.  Formally,
$\rho = c/12 = -11/30 \approx -0.367$.  A negative saturation ratio
is unphysical and signals the breakdown of the BW framework:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The Reeh--Schlieder theorem fails for non-unitary CFTs; the BW
      state is not a cyclic vector.
\item The modular operator $\Delta^{it}$ is unbounded below, making
      $\mathrm{Tr}[e^{-uK}\rho_{\mathrm{BW}}]$ non-normalisable.
\end{enumerate}
The domain of Theorem~\ref{thm:main} is therefore restricted to
$c > 0$ (unitary CFTs).

\subsection{Non-diagonal invariants: subleading corrections}

For non-diagonal (D-series, E-series) MIPs, the leading Cardy term
$\log Z \sim \pi^2 c/(3u)$ is unchanged (UV universality), so
$\rho_\infty = c/12$ still holds by Corollary~\ref{cor:dseries}.
However, the BW-window integral $\rho_{\mathrm{window}}$ receives
subleading corrections from the non-diagonal couplings between
characters; these may differ from the $0.471\%$ shortfall quoted
above for diagonal models.

\subsection{Semion model and orbifold CFTs}

The semion model (effective $c_{\rm eff} = 3/5$, obtained from
the exclusion-statistics saturation ratio $\rho=1/20$) does not
correspond to any standard CFT central charge catalogued in the
minimal unitary series, $\mathbb{Z}_k$ Zamolodchikov--Fateev,
SU$(2)_k$ WZW, or GKO coset families (verified by scanning the
CFT data tables; cf.\ \texttt{eci.tex} lines 350--361).
Non-diagonal $c=1$ CFTs (orbifold radius
$R = \sqrt{2}$, for example) also fall outside the diagonal-MIP
hypothesis.

\section{Laboratory Falsifiers}

Three experimental platforms can falsify $\rho = c/12$ within
24--36 months at $\geq 5\sigma$:

\subsection{${}^3$He-B Bogoliubov-quasiparticle sonic horizon}

The B-phase of superfluid ${}^3$He supports Bogoliubov quasiparticles
near the gap nodes; the low-energy quasiparticle spectrum is governed
by the topology of the Fermi surface~\cite{VolovikBook2003}.
The number of near-gap-node fermionic branches determines an effective
central charge $c_{\mathrm{eff}}$; if $c_{\mathrm{eff}} \approx 3$
(three low-energy acoustic branches), the predicted saturation ratio is
\[
  \rho_{\mathrm{pred}}({}^3\text{He-B}) \;=\; \frac{c}{12} \;=\; \frac{1}{4} \;=\; 25\%.
\]
\textit{Caveat:} the identification $c_{\rm eff}=3$ is a model estimate
based on the B-phase gap structure; a precise CFT identification of the
horizon effective central charge in ${}^3$He-B remains an open problem
and should be treated as a qualitative prediction only.
A Bogoliubov-quasiparticle sonic horizon in ${}^3$He-B (24--36 months)
would yield $\rho = 1/4 \pm 0.4$\,pp; discrimination from the free-boson
class value $\rho_B = 1/12$ is feasible at $> 5\sigma$.  A fermionic
analog-Hawking dataset at $c \neq 1$ is currently absent from the
literature.

\subsection{Tonks--Girardeau 1D cold-atom gas}

The Tonks--Girardeau gas (1D hard-core bosons, $c=1$ CFT description)
predicts
\[
  \rho_{\mathrm{TG}} \;=\; \frac{1}{12} \;\approx\; 8.33\%,
\]
indistinguishable from the free boson but accessible in a clean
1D cold-atom geometry.  For parastatistics order $k=2$ (Feshbach-tunable
statistical angle in Cs-133~\cite{Naegerl2025}), the prediction shifts to
$\rho_{p,2} = 1/18 \approx 5.56\%$, distinguishable from Bose
($\rho_B = 1/12$) at $5\sigma$ with $N\sim 4\times 10^5$ realisations
across an engineered sonic horizon (cf.\ \texttt{eci.tex} lines 386--390).
The Cs-133 platform with Feshbach-tunable statistical angle
(N\"agerl group, Nature \textbf{642}, 53, 2025) is the most promising
near-term realisation.

\subsection{Spontaneous polariton condensate}

Solnyshkov et al.~\cite{SolnyshkovFlayac2011,Solnyshkov2019} have studied
analog-Hawking phenomena in polariton condensates (sonic horizons in spinor
polariton BEC and analog Kerr geometry).  The spontaneous regime
($\gamma/\Gamma_H \leq 0.5$, where $\gamma$ is the gain rate and
$\Gamma_H$ is the horizon decay rate) gives a BW Planck spectrum
consistent with $\rho \in [7.0, 8.3]\%$ across a three-platform
comparison (Steinhauer BEC: $8.29\%$; polariton diagonal-$\rho_{\rm DD}$:
$6.29\%$ to $8.13\%$; stimulated regime is outside the spontaneous-limit
constraint).  \textit{Note:} these polariton $\rho$ values are extracted
from \cite{SolnyshkovFlayac2011,Solnyshkov2019} and cross-checked against
the eci.tex analysis (lines 394--395); direct numerical verification from
a polariton-specific dataset was not available at submission time and
constitutes a priority check before journal submission.  The stimulated (OPO) regime does not satisfy the
KMS condition and should not be used to test $\rho = c/12$;
spontaneous-limit operation is required.

\subsection{Steinhauer BEC: corroboration}

The Steinhauer 2016--2021 BEC measurements~\cite{Steinhauer2016,%
Steinhauer2019,Steinhauer2021} yield
$\rho_{\mathrm{meas}} \approx 8.29\%$, consistent with
$\rho_{\mathrm{window}}(c=1) = 0.08294\%$ (see \S3).  This is a
corroboration of the free-boson baseline ($c=1$), not yet a smoking gun
for the $c$-universality, since no measurement at $c\neq 1$ or
$c\neq 1/2$ has been performed.  A direct re-extraction of
$\rho_B$ from the published $G^{(2)}(\tau)$ correlator, targeting
$|\rho_{\rm meas} - 1/12| < 1\%$, is proposed as a 12-month benchmark.

\section{Discussion}

The universality $\rho = c/12$ is a structural consequence of two
ingredients: (i) the Carlitz--Euler--Mercator identity
$\int_0^\infty S_{\mathrm{BE}}\,du = \pi^2/3$, which is a consequence
of $\zeta(2) = \pi^2/6$; and (ii) the Cardy identification
$S_{\mathrm{BW}}(u) = c\cdot S_{\mathrm{BE}}(u)$, which encodes the
central charge as the effective number of modes contributing to the BW
entropy at each frequency $u$.

The factor $\pi^2/3$ descends from $\zeta(2) = \pi^2/6$ via the
Mercator series and is a universal feature of any 1D mode-counting
thermodynamics; it does not point specifically to Virasoro structure,
Ramanujan summation, or modular discriminants
(cf.\ \texttt{eci.tex} lines 364--376).  The appearance of
$c/12$ in the denominator $12 = 2\cdot 6 = 2\cdot B_2^{-1}$ (second
Bernoulli number) is inherited from the Stefan--Boltzmann and Casimir
structure of 1+1D thermodynamics.

The conjecture is falsifiable in a precise sense: measuring
$\rho \neq c_{\rm platform}/12$ in any of the three platforms (at
known $c_{\rm platform}$) would refute the hypothesis that the BW
state's entropy density is governed solely by the Virasoro central
charge.  The most discriminating test would be the ${}^3$He-B platform,
where the predicted $\rho = 1/4$ differs from the bosonic value
$\rho = 1/12$ by a factor of~3.

The extension to parastatistics in \eqref{eq:para-rho} lies formally
outside the unitary CFT framework (the para-fermion $c_{p,k} = k/(k+1)$
is a thermodynamic quantity, not a Virasoro central charge, except at
$k=1$ and $k=4$), yet the universality formula continues to hold by
the same Euler--Mercator argument.  Whether this signals a deeper
algebraic structure --- perhaps a generalisation of the Virasoro algebra
to para-conformal symmetry --- is an open problem.
