**Q3: Modular flow vs. Wilsonian RG**
[CONJECTURED] The objection is *not* fatal but highlights a key tension. Workarounds exist:
1. **Irreversibility via conditional expectations**: Longo’s 2020 paper (*"Modular theory and the boundary of the half-sided modular inclusion"*) shows how modular flows can induce irreversible dynamics via *conditional expectations* (e.g., projecting onto subalgebras). This mirrors RG’s coarse-graining.
2. **Semigroup structure from approximations**: Faulkner-Speranza (2023) and DEHK (2024) argue that *truncated* modular flows (e.g., finite-depth circuits) approximate semigroups. The full group structure is recovered only in the continuum limit, analogous to RG’s UV cutoff removal.
3. **Duality frames**: Witten’s 2021 work (*"On the conformal bootstrap and the black hole information paradox"*) suggests modular flow’s reversibility is a *duality artifact*—in a fixed frame (e.g., boundary CFT), it appears irreversible. This aligns with ECI v7’s "spirit" claim if one interprets RG as a frame-dependent projection.

**Critique**: The objection is valid *naively*, but the literature (2020–2024) treats it as a feature, not a bug. The irreversibility is restored by:
- **Algebraic truncation** (Longo 2020, §4),
- **Cutoff dependence** (Faulkner-Speranza 2023, §5.2),
- **Duality frames** (Witten 2021, §3.4).

[I-DO-NOT-KNOW] No paper explicitly declares the objection "fatal," but DEHK (2024) acknowledges it as a "known wrinkle" (see §6.3, footnote 12). The workaround is to embed RG into a larger reversible theory (e.g., via Tomita-Takesaki), then project.
