## §G2.A: KMS Condition Identification

### What DEHK 2412.15502 actually says

The paper by De Vuyst, Eccles, Hoehn, and Kirklin (arXiv:2412.15502, JHEP 2025, 103 pp.) concerns **observer-dependent gravitational entropy** via quantum reference frames (QRFs). The core construction is:

1. A type-III₁ von Neumann algebra A_S of QFT observables in a subregion is crossed with the modular flow of a cyclic separating state |Ψ⟩. This produces a **type-II∞** algebra (CLPW construction, citing arXiv:2206.10780).

2. If additionally the clock energy spectrum is **bounded below** (i.e., the clock Hamiltonian H_i is bounded below), the type conversion goes further to a **type-II₁** factor, "which can be understood as the condition that the algebra admits a maximally entangled state" (DEHK §3, p. 3).

3. The **one-parameter family of observer states** is parameterized by the **clock Hamiltonian H_i** spectrum. Different clock choices correspond to different physical observers and yield different density operators and entropies (Sections 4–8 of DEHK).

**What DEHK explicitly states about KMS:**

The paper contains exactly two mentions of KMS states (lines 2161 and 2380 of the extracted text):

> "...it is known that the modular flow of a **KMS state** of the QFT has a geometric interpretation as a spacetime diffeomorphism, addressing (b) above, namely the one corresponding to the boost Killing vector field generating the horizon." (§3.1, line 2161)

> "...there is a 'geometric modular flow conjecture' asserting that the diffeomorphism generated by a vector field that appears boost-like near the boundary of a general subregion... will agree infinitesimally with the modular flow of **some KMS state** and so can be used for building crossed products." (§3.2, line 2380)

**What DEHK does NOT state:**

- DEHK **does not** explicitly state a KMS condition at β = 2π.
- DEHK **does not** state that β = 2π selects a unique state in the one-parameter family.
- DEHK **does not** mention modular forms, Maass forms, or the upper half-plane H.
- The one-parameter family of states is parameterized by the clock energy spectrum and entanglement with fields — it is a **functional** space, not a one-parameter family in any τ.

### The standard KMS/β = 2π fact (not from DEHK, from background literature)

The β = 2π normalization is a standard fact of Tomita-Takesaki modular theory:

- The **Bisognano-Wichmann theorem** (1975) establishes that the modular flow of the Minkowski vacuum restricted to a Rindler wedge is the Lorentz boost, and the vacuum is a **KMS state at β = 2π** with respect to this flow.
- More generally, for a QFT in a spacetime with a Killing horizon (Schwarzschild, de Sitter), the natural horizon KMS temperature is T = κ/2π where κ is the surface gravity, giving β = 2π/κ. For unit surface gravity κ = 1, β = 2π.
- In the de Sitter context of CLPW (2206.10780), the de Sitter vacuum is indeed a KMS state with respect to the boost Killing flow, at the Gibbons-Hawking temperature T_dS = H/2π (equivalently β = 2π in units of the Hubble radius).

**The parameter β = 2π therefore does appear in the crossed-product gravitational algebra construction — but as the normalization convention of the modular Hamiltonian H_S = H_ξ, not as a free parameter being tuned.** The KMS condition is already baked in by the choice of vacuum state |Ψ⟩ used to define the modular flow. It does not freely select among a family of τ values.

### Faulkner-Speranza (arXiv:2405.00847) correction

The correct arXiv ID for the Faulkner-Speranza "Gravitational algebras and the generalized second law" paper is **2405.00847**, not 2305.01696 as stated in the task prompt. The abstract (verified live) confirms: this paper derives the GSL for Killing horizon cuts using crossed-product algebras. It invokes a "state whose modular flow is geometric on the horizon" but does not state a β = 2π condition explicitly, nor does it mention modular forms or the upper half-plane.

### Verdict for G2.A

**The β = 2π KMS condition is real** as a background fact of the Tomita-Takesaki/Bisognano-Wichmann framework that underlies these papers, but **DEHK and Faulkner-Speranza do not state it as a condition that selects a unique state from a family parameterized by τ.** The KMS state in these papers is the pre-existing vacuum/horizon state; τ is not one of its parameters.

## §G2.B: Polyharmonic Order from Algebra Type

### Critical terminological finding

The Qu-Ding paper (arXiv:2406.02527) uses the term "polyharmonic Maaß forms" in a **non-standard sense** relative to the mathematical literature. In their paper:

- "Polyharmonic Maaß forms" satisfy **Δ_k Y(τ) = 0** (eq. 2.6b of their paper — depth 1 in the mathematical sense).
- The "poly" refers to the fact that the full set of Yukawa couplings includes forms of multiple weights (positive, zero, and negative), connected by the raising/lowering operators D and ξ.
- This is **not** Δ_k^r Y = 0 with r > 1.

In the **mathematical literature** (Lagarias-Rhoades 2015, arXiv:1508.02652; Alfes-Neumann et al. 2022, arXiv:2207.02278), "polyharmonic Maass form of depth m" means a real-analytic modular form of weight k annihilated by (Δ_k)^m but not (Δ_k)^{m-1}. Depth 1 = harmonic Maass form; depth m ≥ 2 is the genuine polyharmonic generalization.

**The question posed in G2.B assumed Qu-Ding uses Δ_k^r Y = 0 with r dictated by algebra type. This assumption is false**: Qu-Ding uses r = 1 only (harmonic condition), and **no connection between the polyharmonic depth r and the Murray-von Neumann type classification (I, II, III) appears in Qu-Ding 2406.02527 or in any related paper found.**

### What the Radulescu paper actually says

The paper most directly linking Maass forms to von Neumann algebras is Radulescu (arXiv:0802.3548), which shows that Hecke operators on **classical Maass forms** (harmonic, depth 1) are unitarily equivalent to completely positive maps on **type-II₁ factors**. Key points:

- This constructs a representation of Hecke operators as maps on a type-II₁ factor, not the other way around.
- It does not define "higher depth" Maass forms and does not claim that depth m corresponds to type-II factor with some subscript related to m.
- The connection is between the **harmonic** Maass form theory (depth 1) and type-II₁; there is no r → type correspondence in this paper.

### On a potential r ↔ algebra-type correspondence

No paper found makes the claim "depth r of a polyharmonic Maass form corresponds to some number associated with the type-I/II/III classification." There is also a critical notational collision: "Δ" in operator algebra theory (Tomita-Takesaki) is the modular operator on the Hilbert space of the algebra; "Δ_k" in number theory is the weight-k hyperbolic Laplacian on H. These are completely different objects. The crossed product construction (type-III × modular flow → type-II∞) corresponds to r = 1 in the Laplacian sense throughout — the depth-1 harmonic forms are precisely those arising in existing Maass/Hecke/type-II₁ connections (Radulescu 0802.3548). No extension to r ≥ 2 has been constructed or motivated.

**Verdict for G2.B:** The claim "r = 2 is naturally selected for type-II∞" is **not supported** by any available source. Qu-Ding 2406.02527 uses only r = 1 (depth-1 harmonic Maass forms despite the "polyharmonic" label). The mathematical literature on genuine depth-m polyharmonic Maass forms (m ≥ 2) makes no connection to operator algebra types.

## §G2.C: Fixed-Point Analysis on H/Γ(4)

### Three-level obstruction

**Obstruction 1 (upstream):** DEHK does not impose any condition that pins τ. Without a specific KMS-selected form Y(τ) identified by G2.A, the question of its critical points is underdetermined.

**Obstruction 2 (Qu-Ding forms are depth-1, not depth-2):** Qu-Ding's forms at level N = 4, weight k = 5 satisfy Δ_5 Y = 0 (depth 1). Their explicit Fourier expansions follow from eq. (2.47) of arXiv:2406.02527, lifted from weight-(2-5) = weight-(-3) modular forms at level 4. The space PH_5(Γ(4)) is finite-dimensional but multi-dimensional. No physical principle from the KMS/DEHK side selects a particular basis element, so critical-point analysis is underdetermined.

**Obstruction 3 (symmetry alone is insufficient):** The points τ = i and τ = ω = e^{iπ/3} are SL₂(ℤ) fixed points (orders 2 and 3 respectively). For holomorphic weight-k forms, vanishing conditions at these points follow from f(Sτ) = τ^k f(τ). For non-holomorphic harmonic forms the constraint is weaker and does not automatically produce critical points. These are candidate critical points, not guaranteed ones.

## §G2.D: Verdict — Does the Chain Close?

### Chain assessment: step by step

**G2.A → G2.B link (KMS condition constrains algebra type → sets depth r):**

STATUS: **DOES NOT CLOSE.**

The KMS condition at β = 2π is a background fact of the Tomita-Takesaki formalism for Killing horizons (Bisognano-Wichmann). It selects the horizon vacuum state, not a τ value. The type-II∞ algebra that results from the crossed product construction has no natural association with polyharmonic depth r ≥ 2. The connection would require a new theorem of the form:

> "For a type-II∞ crossed product algebra A, the natural modular object associated with A on the upper half-plane H is a polyharmonic Maass form of depth 2."

No such theorem exists in the literature as of May 2026. This is the **primary missing lemma.**

**G2.B → G2.C link (depth r = 2 → specific form → critical point at τ = i):**

STATUS: **BLOCKED UPSTREAM.**

Even if depth r = 2 were established (G2.B fails to establish it), the mapping from a depth-2 Maass form on H to a specific element of PH_5(Γ(4)) would require identifying which representation of Γ'_4 ≅ S₄' the physical Yukawa matrix transforms in — a separate non-trivial input from the LYD20 model.

**G2.C standalone (does any weight-5 harmonic Maass form on Γ(4) have its unique critical point at τ = i?):**

STATUS: **UNDETERMINED** — requires Sage computation.

The question is well-posed but not answered by analytical means in this session. The symmetry argument (τ = i is a fixed point of SL₂(ℤ)) is suggestive but not sufficient to establish it as a critical point of a generic harmonic Maass form.

### Three missing lemmas

**Lemma 1 (algebraic — primary gap):** There must exist a natural map Φ from the state space of a type-II∞ gravitational crossed product algebra to functions on H = {τ : Im τ > 0}, with the KMS state at β = 2π mapping to a Maass form of specified depth, weight, and level.

- **Missing:** No construction of such Φ exists. The upper half-plane H does not appear in DEHK-type constructions. The parameter τ in QRF/clock papers is clock time, not the modular form modulus.

**Lemma 2 (physical — bridge gap):** The Maass form produced by Lemma 1 must live in PH_k(Γ(N)) for k = 5 (Yukawa weight in S'₄ models) and N = 4 (level of S'₄).

- **Missing:** No derivation connects β = 2π KMS states to the specific flavor-symmetry parameters (k=5, N=4). This requires an explicit embedding of S'₄ symmetry into the gravitational algebra structure.

**Lemma 3 (analytic — downstream gap):** The specific form from Lemma 2 has its unique critical point on H/Γ(4) at τ = i or τ = ω.

- **Missing:** Not proved. Multi-dimensionality of PH₅(Γ(4)) prevents a generic statement. Requires Sage computation for each potential physical form once Lemmas 1 and 2 are established.

### Overall verdict

**[CLOSURE BLOCKED]** — the chain G2.A → G2.B → G2.C does not close as a logical argument:

1. **G2.A is correct as background physics** (β = 2π KMS is a real fact of the modular flow framework) but **wrong as a τ-fixing mechanism**: the KMS condition fixes a state in the QFT Hilbert space, not a point in the modular parameter space H.

2. **G2.B is false as stated**: Qu-Ding 2406.02527 does not use iterated Laplacians (depth r ≥ 2), and no connection between polyharmonic depth r and von Neumann algebra type (I/II/III) exists in the literature.

4. **The "Nobel-level hypothesis" of G2.D** — that KMS at β = 2π picks out a depth-2 form on Γ(4) with unique critical point at τ = i, yielding m_c/m_t = 2.7×10⁻³ as a structural prediction — **is not supported by the current state of the literature.** It remains a speculative hope, not an argument.
