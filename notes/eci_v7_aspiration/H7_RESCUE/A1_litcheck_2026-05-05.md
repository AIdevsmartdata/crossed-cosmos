## Standard formula (found)

   Σ' 1/(a+bi)^{4ν} = (2ϖ)^{4ν} · H_{4ν} / (4ν)!

**Shimura 1976 [Sh3, "On some arithmetic properties of modular forms…", Ann. Math. 102]:**

## Cardy hit explanation

The "parameter-free Cardy hits" α_2 = 1/12 = c/12 (c=1, free boson) and α_3 = 1/24 = c/12 (c=1/2, Ising) are **NOT independent** — α_3 = α_2/2 is forced by Γ(3)/Γ(2) = 2. So there is exactly ONE non-trivial coincidence to explain: **why does α_2 = 1/12 ?**

α_2 corresponds to L(ψ⁴, 1) (the central-ish point), which is computed by Damerell-Shimura via a holomorphic Eisenstein-Kronecker series E*₄(z, 1; i) at z = i. Its rational part is, by [WEK Weil 1976, Ch. VIII / Katz 1976 §5], a specific Bernoulli-Hurwitz number. The value 1/12 is the second Bernoulli-Hurwitz coefficient and equals B_2/2 = 1/12 — i.e., the **classical Bernoulli number contribution**. This is the same 1/12 as in ζ(−1) = −1/12, transported to the Q(i) CM motive via Chowla-Selberg.

**Therefore the c=1 hit is structural** (Bernoulli B_2 = 1/6 → 1/12), but the **c=1/2 hit is mathematical doubling** (functional equation Γ(3)/Γ(2)=2). The genuinely new physics observation is the SINGLE coincidence α_2 = B_2/2 = 1/12 = c=1 Cardy. The Ising c=1/2 hit is automatic, not independent confirmation.

This **DOWN-WEIGHTS the empirical "two-Cardy hit" claim by half** — it's one hit + one Γ-shadow, not two independent hits.
