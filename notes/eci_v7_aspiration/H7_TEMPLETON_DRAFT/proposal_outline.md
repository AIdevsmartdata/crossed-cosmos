## Background (2 paragraphs)

### Problem: Fitting vs. Prediction in Fundamental Physics

Modular flavor symmetries (since Feruglio 2017) have enabled elegant fits to 13+ observables using 8–12 free parameters—but remain **constructive frameworks, not predictive engines**. Standard GUT approaches (SU(5), SO(10)) similarly suffer: 6+ parameters tune 4–5 observables. The deeper question: **Can algebraic number-theoretic structures intrinsic to particle physics itself constrain free parameters, eliminating degrees of freedom?** The ECI portfolio claims yes, via a new mechanism: **CM-anchoring by ℚ(i) modular forms**.

### ECI Structural Anchor: CM-by-ℚ(i) Modular Flavor

The portfolio's cornerstone is the identification of **LMFDB ID 4.5.b.a**—a unique weight-5 modular form with CM by ℚ(i)—as the natural home for lepton-sector flavor symmetry. This identification is rigorously verified (4-path LMFDB cross-check, q-expansion verbatim match, Hecke character polys confirmed). The claim: **CM-anchoring provides 1–2 additional structural constraints**, reducing the lepton-sector DOF from ~6 to ~4, converting previously free-fitted observables into **genuine predictions**:
- **θ₁₃ Cabibbo angle** from micro-locality bounds (H7-A Damerell ladder prediction)

- **Proton decay B-ratio** (p→e⁺π⁰)/(p→K⁺ν̄) = **2.06 ± 0.15** (first-principles, no fit)
