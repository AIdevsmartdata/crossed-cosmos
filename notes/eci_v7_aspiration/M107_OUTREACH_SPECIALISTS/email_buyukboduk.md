## SUBJECT

Anticyclotomic Iwasawa theory at p=2 ramified in K=Q(i) — does any framework apply?

---

## BODY

Dear Professor Büyükboduk,

I am an independent researcher working on the Iwasawa Main Conjecture for a specific CM newform: f = 4.5.b.a (LMFDB; weight 5, level 4, character χ_{-4}, CM by K = Q(i)), at the prime p = 2, which ramifies in Q(i) as (1+i)². The form has a_2 = −4 (Steinberg edge: ψ_min((1+i)) = (1+i)^4 = −4).

Your work with Lei — in particular arXiv:1602.07508 (anticyclotomic p-ordinary Iwasawa theory) and the joint work with Burungale-Lei arXiv:2211.03722 — is the closest published framework I have found. However, both papers require p split in K and p ≥ 5; the case K = Q(i), p = 2 ramified simultaneously violates three hypotheses: p is not split, p | D_K = −4, and p is not odd.

I have also examined Fan–Wan arXiv:2304.09806, which handles p = 2 and ramified p but is restricted to self-dual CM Hecke characters of infinity-type (1, 0), not general weight-5 newforms.

My question, honestly framed: is the anticyclotomic IMC for K = Q(i) at p = 2 ramified, with f of algebraic weight 5, genuinely open, or does some framework — perhaps in preparation — address this setting? I am not claiming to solve it; I have numerically verified π·L(f,1)/L(f,2) = 6/5 (80-digit, PARI/Sage) and would like to understand whether an L_2^{anti-cyc}(f,T) can even be defined.

Any orientation would be greatly appreciated.
