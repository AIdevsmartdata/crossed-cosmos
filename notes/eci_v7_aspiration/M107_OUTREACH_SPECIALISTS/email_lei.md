## BODY

Dear Professor Lei,

I am an independent researcher working on the anticyclotomic Iwasawa Main Conjecture for f = 4.5.b.a (LMFDB; weight 5, level 4, CM by K = Q(i)), at p = 2 (ramified in Q(i) as (1+i)², with a_2 = −4 at the Steinberg edge).

The recent paper of Longo, Vigni and Wang (arXiv:2501.03673, "A generalized Rubin formula for Hecke characters") comes very close to what I need but explicitly excludes my setting via Assumption 1.1: their framework requires disc(K) odd, p splits in K, and infinity-type (1+ℓ, −ℓ) with ℓ ∈ ℤ. For f = 4.5.b.a with K = Q(i), all three conditions fail simultaneously: disc = −4 (even), p = 2 ramifies in K, and weight k = 5 gives ℓ = 3/2 ∉ ℤ.

Your joint papers with Büyükboduk and Burungale (arXiv:2211.03722, arXiv:2310.06813) address non-ordinary primes but again require p ≥ 5 and p split or inert in K. Fan–Wan arXiv:2304.09806 handles p = 2 ramified but only for self-dual weight-2 CM characters.

The question I am trying to answer honestly: is the anticyclotomic IMC for (K = Q(i), p = 2 ramified, weight 5 newform) genuinely open in the current literature, or does some framework — including work in preparation — address this triple of obstructions? I have numerically verified π·L(f,1)/L(f,2) = 6/5 (80-digit, PARI) and would gratefully welcome any orientation on whether an L_2^{anti-cyc}(f,T) can even be constructed here.
