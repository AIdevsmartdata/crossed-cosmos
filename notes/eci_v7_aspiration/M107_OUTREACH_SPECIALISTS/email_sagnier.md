## BODY

Dear Dr. Sagnier,

I am an independent researcher working on a conjecture that an explicit CM newform, f = 4.5.b.a in LMFDB notation (weight 5, level 4, character χ_{-4}, CM by K = Q(i), with ψ_min of ∞-type (4, 0)), might be accessible via a spectral-realisation approach in the spirit of your arXiv:1703.10521 (J. Number Theory 2019).

A specific question concerns Theorem 7.2 of that paper. As I understand it, your framework constructs an arithmetic site for imaginary quadratic fields K with class number 1, and achieves a spectral interpretation of zeros of Dedekind ζ_K and of Hecke L-functions — but for characters factoring through the compact S^1 ≅ K_∞^1 component of the adèle class space. My question is whether Theorem 7.2 (or the construction preceding it) is intended to cover only finite-order angular characters χ(e^{iθ}) = e^{inθ}, or whether it also applies to algebraic weight characters ψ of ∞-type (k-1, 0) (equivalently, ψ: z ↦ (z/|z|)^{k-1} · |z|^{k-1} on C^×). For the k=5 case this is ∞-type (4, 0).

The obstruction I suspect is representation-theoretic: the factor |z|^{k-1} is non-compact (from C^×/S^1 ≅ ℝ_{>0}^×) and would not lie in L^2(A_K^×/K^××K_∞^1, d^×x) without modifying the ambient Hilbert space. I am curious whether you have explored a weight-k extension, or whether — as my survey of the 2018–2026 literature suggests — this remains genuinely open.

I would be very grateful for any clarification, including pointers to unpublished work or work in preparation. I am happy to share project context if it might be of interest.
