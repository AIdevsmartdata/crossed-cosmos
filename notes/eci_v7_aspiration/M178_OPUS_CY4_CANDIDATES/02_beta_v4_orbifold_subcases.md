## (β1) Two-step orbifold (X^L × X^Q) / V_4 with Galois action

**Construction.** Take the same X_a^L = Km(E_a^L × E_a^L) and X_b^Q = Shioda-Inose K3 over H(-88) as in (α). Form:

$$Y_{\beta_1} = (X_a^L \times X_b^Q) / V_4$$

with V_4 = ⟨g_1, g_2⟩ where:
- g_1 = σ_L × σ_Q (the Borcea-Voisin Z_2 action of (α))
- g_2 = id × ρ_2|_{X_b^Q} (Galois-conjugation of X_b^Q sending j(E_b^Q) ↦ j(E_a^Q))

**CRITICAL OBSTRUCTION.** ρ_2 is a **Galois automorphism**, NOT a regular automorphism (algebraic automorphism over the base field). It acts on X_b^Q^{Gal} viewed as a scheme over Q, **descending** the Q(√2)-coordinates to Q. It is **not** a self-map of X_b^Q over its base field H(-88). 

So V_4 is not literally a group of automorphisms of X_a^L × X_b^Q over a single field — it mixes two operations:
- σ_L × σ_Q is a regular automorphism over H(-88) (or any common field of definition)
- ρ_2 is a Galois descent operation that exchanges X_a^Q ↔ X_b^Q (the two Shioda-Inose K3s for class [1] and class [2])

**Reformulation (β1'):** Use both X_a^Q and X_b^Q (the two Galois-conjugate Shioda-Inose K3s for disc -88), and form:

$$Y_{\beta_1'} = (X_a^L \times X_a^Q \times X_b^Q) / G$$

with G = ⟨σ_L × σ_a^Q × σ_b^Q, id × \text{swap}_{a \leftrightarrow b}⟩

This is a CY 6-fold (3·2 = 6 dimensions), NOT a CY 4-fold. **Wrong dimension.**

**Alternative (β1''):** Form (X_a^L × X_b^Q) but quotient by the **Galois group V_4 acting only diagonally on field of definition**. This is just the Galois descent to Q of the variety Y_α. Y_α is defined over H(-88) ⊂ M; its Galois descent Y_α/Q is well-defined.

Y_α/Q is a CY 4-fold over Q (after étale Galois quotient + smooth resolution). **This is well-defined and is a "(β1) interpretation" of V_4-orbifold descent.**

**KW status of Y_α/Q.** Same period structure as Y_α/H(-88) up to Galois action; KW Case A/B analysis applies on the geometric generic fibre = Y_α ⊗_Q Q̄ ≃ Y_α/M, giving K_L = Q(i), K_Q = Q(√-22), Case B FAILS.

## (β3) Galois descent of Y_α from H(-88) (or M) to Q

This is the CLEANEST V_4-orbifold interpretation. Let me make it rigorous.

**Setup.** Y_α was constructed over H(-88) = Q(√-22, √2). Gal(H(-88)/Q) = V_4 = ⟨ρ_1, ρ_2⟩. The Galois group acts on Y_α(C) = Y_α ⊗_{H(-88)} C by base-change, permuting the H(-88)-rational points.

**Galois descent theorem.** A scheme Y_α defined over H(-88) descends to a scheme Y_α^{Q} over Q if and only if there exist isomorphisms φ_g : Y_α → g·Y_α for each g ∈ Gal(H(-88)/Q) satisfying the cocycle condition φ_{gh} = (g·φ_h) ∘ φ_g.

For our Y_α = (X_a^L × X_b^Q)/⟨σ_L × σ_Q⟩:
- X_a^L is defined over Q (Kummer of CM elliptic curve E_a^L : y² = x³ - x defined over Q)
- X_b^Q is defined over H(-88) but its Galois conjugate X_a^Q (the other class [1] singular K3, with τ_a = i√22) is also defined over H(-88), and ρ_2 : X_b^Q → X_a^Q is an isomorphism over H(-88)

**Issue.** ρ_2(X_b^Q) = X_a^Q ≠ X_b^Q (they are NOT isomorphic as K3 surfaces over H(-88) — they are Galois conjugates but represent **distinct** elements of the moduli of singular K3 with disc -88, namely classes [1] vs [2] of Cl(-88) = Z/2).

So Y_α does NOT descend to Q as Y_α — instead, the Q-form is:

$$Y_\alpha^Q := \text{Gal-orbit of } Y_\alpha = (X_a^L \times (X_a^Q \sqcup X_b^Q)) / \text{wider group}$$

This is **two copies** of Y_α glued by Galois descent.

**Cleaner formulation.** Form the abelian variety / CY pair:

$$Z_\alpha^Q := \text{Res}_{H(-88)/Q}(Y_\alpha)$$

the Weil restriction of scalars from H(-88) to Q. This is a 4·[H(-88):Q] = 16-dim variety over Q. **Wrong dimension** (we want CY 4-fold over Q, not over H(-88)).

**Correct formulation (β3):** "CY 4-fold Y over Q whose base-change to H(-88) is Y_α." Such a Y exists iff Y_α has a Q-form, which requires Galois cocycle data.

**Cocycle obstruction.** ρ_2 : Y_α → ρ_2(Y_α) where ρ_2(Y_α) = (X_a^L × X_a^Q)/⟨σ_L × σ_a^Q⟩. **This is a different CY 4-fold** (uses X_a^Q with τ = i√22 instead of X_b^Q with τ = i√(11/2)).

So Y_α (with τ_Q = i√(11/2)) is **not Galois-stable**. Its Galois orbit is {Y_α, ρ_2 Y_α} = {two CY 4-folds}, related by τ_Q = i√(11/2) ↔ τ_Q' = i√22.

**Conclusion (β3):** The Galois descent of Y_α to Q is a CY 4-fold-pair (twisted form), NOT a single CY 4-fold. ECI v9 distinguishes class [2] anchor from class [1] (only τ_Q = i√(11/2) gives ECI v9 quark sector). **Galois symmetry is BROKEN by ECI v9 anchor selection.**

**Verdict (β3):** **(C) NEGATIVE for descent to Q with single τ_Q anchor.** ECI v9 selects a Galois-asymmetric CY 4-fold by construction.
