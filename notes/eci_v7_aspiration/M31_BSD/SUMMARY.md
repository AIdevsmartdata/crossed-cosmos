## Sole salvage: 1-paragraph footnote for M13.1 paper-2 §6

> *Relation to BSD.* The Birch-Swinnerton-Dyer conjecture is a statement
> about elliptic curves over ℚ, equivalently weight-2 newforms via the
> modularity theorem (Wiles 1995; BCDT 2001). The newform $f$ here has
> weight 5 and is not associated to any elliptic curve, so BSD itself
> does not apply. The natural generalisation to the weight-5 motive $M(f)$
> is the Bloch-Kato Tamagawa number conjecture (Bloch-Kato 1990) at
> non-critical integer points, equivalently the Beilinson regulator pairing
> on $H^2_\mathcal{M}(K_3, \mathbb{Q}(j))$ at $s=k=5$ (Beilinson 1984;
> Deninger-Scholl 1991). Conjecture M13.1, if proven, supplies 2-adic
> information at $\ell=2$ entering the Tamagawa factor.
