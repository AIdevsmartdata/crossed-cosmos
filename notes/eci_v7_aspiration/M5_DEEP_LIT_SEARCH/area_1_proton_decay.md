## P1-A1: SUPPORTER/ADJACENT

**ID:** 2604.24613v1
**DATE:** 2026-04-27
**AUTHORS:** Wei-Qi Fan, Yi Liao, Xiao-Dong Ma
**TITLE:** Nucleon decays into one lepton plus two non-strange mesons
**ABSTRACT (verified):** Studies nucleon decay into lepton + two pseudoscalar mesons using low-energy EFT, without single-operator-dominance assumption. Model-independent global analysis correlates two- and three-body processes. Improves bounds on 15 three-body decay modes and improves two-body process constraints by ~factor 2.
**CLASSIFICATION: ADJACENT**
**ECI RELEVANCE:** Three-body modes (p → e+ π⁰ π⁰ etc.) are sub-leading relative to ECI's two-body branching ratio B(p→e+π⁰)/B(p→K+ν̄) = 2.06. This paper is complementary: it tightens experimental handles on the full two-body + three-body operator space. No direct challenge to the 2.06 ratio (which comes from the operator coefficient ratio, not from this EFT global fit). Does improve constraints on the dimension-6 operators that also feed into two-body modes — worth citing in the proton-decay PRD paper as context.
**NOTE:** Live-verified at https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24613. All 3 authors and title confirmed.

## P1-A2: CHALLENGER (partial)

**ID:** 2604.08237v1
**DATE:** 2026-04-09
**AUTHORS:** Akifumi Chitose, Ko Hirooka, Masahiro Ibe, Satoshi Shirai
**TITLE:** Fermion Multiplicities at the GUT Scale: A Statistical Study of Unification and Proton Decay
**ABSTRACT (verified):** Examines vector-like fermion multiplets in SU(5) GUT. Threshold effects raise M_GUT to ~10^15.5 GeV. Multiple GUT multiplet admixtures suppress nucleon decay operator coefficients, leading to **longer proton lifetimes** than conventional GUTs. Relaxes rigid Yukawa relations and alleviates bottom-tau unification problem. Recommends probing multiple proton-decay channels at Hyper-Kamiokande.
**CLASSIFICATION: CHALLENGER (partial)**
**ECI RELEVANCE:** This paper introduces a mechanism (multi-fermion SU(5) with threshold effects) that generically **suppresses** proton decay operators and extends proton lifetime beyond conventional non-SUSY SU(5) predictions. If this mechanism applies to ECI's 5_H+45_H scenario, it could shift the partial lifetime predictions upward (away from current experimental reach), but it would not necessarily alter the **branching ratio** B(e+π⁰)/B(K+ν̄) = 2.06 which is set by the operator structure. The bottom-tau unification discussion is relevant to ECI's M5→M6 redo with full modular f^{ij}(τ=i).
**CHALLENGER STATUS:** Challenges the assumption of unmodified GUT-scale operator coefficients. Non-threatening to B-ratio prediction but potentially lengthens predicted lifetime — need to check if ECI's M5→M6 redo must account for multi-fermion threshold corrections.
**NOTE:** Live-verified at https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08237. All 4 authors and title confirmed.

## P1-A3: SUPPORTER

**ID:** 2512.14813v2
**DATE:** 2026-04-02 (revised version; originally submitted 2025-12)
**AUTHORS:** Arnau Bas i Beneito, Ajdin Palavrić, Andrea Sainaghi
**TITLE:** Implications of Flavor Symmetries for Baryon Number Violation
**ABSTRACT (verified):** Systematic classification of dimension-6 baryon-number-violating SMEFT operators under various flavor-symmetry assumptions. In certain flavor scenarios, non-trivial interplay with neutrino masses leads to proton-decay constraints compatible with BNV scales in multi-TeV range. Identifies one-particle UV completions.
**CLASSIFICATION: SUPPORTER**
**ECI RELEVANCE:** Flavor symmetries (like S'_4) are shown to constrain the BNV operator space. ECI's use of S'_4 modular flavor to determine the Yukawa tensor f^{ij}(τ=i) directly constrains the dimension-6 operator coefficients. This paper validates the general program of using flavor symmetry to predict proton decay phenomenology. Does not specifically study S'_4 but the methodology is directly applicable.
**NOTE:** Live-verified at https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.14813. Authors and title confirmed.

## P1-A5: ADJACENT

**ID:** 2603.22418v1
**DATE:** 2026-03-23 (just before cutoff — included for completeness)

**TITLE:** Planck-Scale Effects on Nucleon Decay in Minimal Supersymmetric SU(5)
**ABSTRACT:** Examines dimension-5 operators in minimal SUSY SU(5) GUT with Planck-mass-suppressed operators. Considers CMSSM and NUHM scenarios, finds decay mode predictions for Hyper-K and JUNO.
**CLASSIFICATION: ADJACENT**

ECI's non-SUSY exclusion of SUSY dimension-5 operators means this is ADJACENT but not challenger to ECI's B-ratio. Useful for comparison in the proton-decay PRD paper §2.

## Proton Decay Area — Meta-assessment

**Null results:** No 2026 paper found that:
- Directly computes B(p→e+π⁰)/B(p→K+ν̄) in non-SUSY SU(5) with 45_H and gives a value inconsistent with 2.06
- Updates Hyper-K sensitivity curves in a way that moves the window ECI predicts out of reach
- Rules out the 5_H+45_H model by new operator analysis

**Key gap:** Chitose et al. (P1-A2) introduces multi-fermion threshold suppression of proton decay operators — this could lengthen the predicted partial lifetime without changing the B-ratio. This should be assessed quantitatively in M5→M6 redo.

**Closest challenger:** Chitose et al. 2604.08237 (mechanism that suppresses decay operators and extends lifetime) — but does not challenge the B-ratio prediction 2.06.
