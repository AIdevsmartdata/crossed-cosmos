## TIER 1: Active Challengers (require ECI response)

### C1: 2604.08237 — Chitose, Hirooka, Ibe, Shirai (2026-04-09)
**Title:** Fermion Multiplicities at the GUT Scale: A Statistical Study of Unification and Proton Decay
**Category:** hep-ph | Area 1 (Proton Decay)
**The challenge:** Multi-vector-like-fermion SU(5) models raise M_GUT to ~10^15.5 GeV and suppress nucleon decay operator coefficients via GUT multiplet admixtures, generically predicting **longer proton lifetimes** than conventional SU(5). If this mechanism applies to ECI's 5_H+45_H framework, ECI's predicted partial lifetimes could shift upward.
**Does it challenge B-ratio 2.06?** NO — it suppresses absolute rates but not their ratio. The ratio B(p→e+π⁰)/B(p→K+ν̄) = 2.06 is set by operator coefficient ratios, not absolute normalization. Multi-fermion threshold corrections would affect numerator and denominator similarly in the operator basis ECI uses.
**ECI response needed:** Check in M5→M6 redo whether multi-fermion threshold corrections to operator coefficients affect the 2.06 ratio specifically. Cite as context showing proton lifetime could be extended.
**Priority: HIGH** (must address in proton-decay PRD paper §2)

### C2: 2604.08449 — Antusch, King, Wang (2026-04-17)
**Title:** Coupled Dark Energy and Dark Matter for DESI: An Effective Guide to the Phantom Divide
**Category:** hep-ph/astro-ph.CO | Area 2 (Cosmological tensions)
**The challenge:** King and Wang (same as ECI's H8' dS-trap, arXiv:2310.10369) are now working on DESI dark energy with a **coupled quintessence model** that does not involve non-minimal coupling ξ. This creates a competing interpretation of DESI data from the same group whose moduli stabilization ECI uses. If this model fits DESI as well as or better than Wolf's ξ=2.31, and ECI's ξ~0 (Cassini-clean), then ECI's Cassini-clean position may be seen as one of three competing frameworks.
**Does it challenge ECI's Cassini-clean null?** INDIRECT — it doesn't use ξ at all; it's a coupled DE model. It doesn't violate Cassini (no fifth force). So it's more a competitor for the "correct DESI interpretation" than a direct falsifier of ECI's ξ measurement.
**ECI response needed:** Acknowledge in Wolf-vs-ECI Bayes paper (Framing A) that coupled quintessence (Antusch-King-Wang 2026) is a third framework. Show ECI (ξ~0) is distinguishable from both Wolf (ξ=2.31) and Antusch-King-Wang (coupled quintessence, different ξ structure).
**Priority: MEDIUM-HIGH** (include in Wolf-vs-ECI comparison, §2 or §3)

### C3: 2604.11106 — Pantos, Perivolaropoulos (2026-04-21)
**Title:** On the origin of the BAOtr-DESI tension
**Category:** astro-ph.CO | Area 2
**The challenge:** 3.7σ irreducible tension between fiducial-independent angular BAO and DESI DR2 3D BAO at z=0.510. If DESI DR2 has internal inconsistency, then any model comparison (ECI-NMC vs Wolf-NMC vs ΛCDM) using DESI DR2 may be systematically compromised.
**Does it challenge ECI's cosmology pillar?** INDIRECT — ECI's Levier #1B uses DESI DR2 + Pantheon+ + KiDS-1000. If DESI DR2 3D BAO has an internal 3.7σ tension, ECI's MCMC result could be affected (ξ constraint changes slightly). However, ECI's Cassini-clean null (ξ ≈ -0.00003 ± 0.016) is so close to zero that a moderate shift in DESI-derived constraints would not move ECI into the Wolf regime (ξ=2.31).
**ECI response needed:** Mention Pantos-Perivolaropoulos in the data section of the Wolf-vs-ECI paper as a known DESI DR2 systematic. Show robustness of ECI Cassini-clean null using DESI DR1 or BAOtr subset.
**Priority: MEDIUM** (data robustness check)
