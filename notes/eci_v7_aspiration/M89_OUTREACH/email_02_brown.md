# Email 2 — Francis Brown

## Subject

Numerical curiosity at weight 5, level 4, CM: π·L(f,1)/L(f,2) = 6/5 — possible single-valued period?

---

## Body

Dear Professor Brown,

I hope you will excuse a brief unsolicited note. I am an independent researcher and have recently verified, to 80-digit precision, that for the newform f = 4.5.b.a (weight 5, level 4, CM by Q(i)):

    π · L(f, 1) / L(f, 2) = 6/5   (exactly rational, Ω-independent).

Reading your recent paper with T. J. Fonseca (arXiv:2508.04844), I am exploring whether this rationality could be interpreted in the framework of single-valued periods on the moduli stack M_{1,3}. Your result applies at weight 4, level 1, where the mixed Tate property of M_{1,3} over ℤ (your 2012 paper, arXiv:1102.1312) is the key input. The question I cannot settle from the literature is whether M_{1,3}^{Γ_1(4)} is expected to carry a mixed Tate structure over ℤ[i, 1/2].

If you are aware of any work — or known obstruction — bearing on level-4 mixed Tate structures or the weight-5 case, I would be very grateful for a pointer. I write in a spirit of verification, not claim.

Yours sincerely,
