# Email 3 — Antonio Lei

## Subject

Iwasawa/BDP for K = Q(i) at p = 2 ramified — does any framework handle this case?

---

## Body

Dear Professor Lei,

I am an independent researcher working on a specific CM newform: f = 4.5.b.a (LMFDB; weight 5, level 4, character χ_{-4}, CM by K = Q(i)). The form has a_2 = -4, Steinberg local type at p = 2, with p = 2 ramified in Q(i) as (1+i)².

I have been studying the Iwasawa Main Conjecture in this regime and have found that the recent paper of Longo, Vigni and Wang (arXiv:2501.03673) comes very close but explicitly excludes my case via Assumption 1.1: their hypotheses require disc(K) odd, p splits in K, and infinity-type (1+ℓ, -ℓ) with ℓ ∈ ℤ. For f = 4.5.b.a with K = Q(i), all three conditions fail simultaneously (disc = -4 even, p = 2 ramified, weight k = 5 giving ℓ = 3/2 ∉ ℤ).

I am trying to understand whether:
(a) any existing framework — including work by you or your collaborators (e.g. Castella-Wan, Pollack-Weston "On anticyclotomic μ-invariants") — handles the BDP/Rubin-formula construction at **p ramified** in an imaginary quadratic field, or
(b) this genuinely remains open, constituting a gap in the anticyclotomic IMC theory.

I have numerically verified π·L(f,1)/L(f,2) = 6/5 (80-digit, PARI + Sage) and would like to understand whether an analytic Selmer group or L_p^{anti-cyc}(f,T) can even be defined at this ramified prime. Any orientation — published or in preparation — would be very helpful.
