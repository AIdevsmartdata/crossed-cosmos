# Email 4 — Francesc Castella

## Subject

BDP Heegner cycles at p = 2 ramified in Q(i) — obstruction or gap in the literature?

---

## Body

Dear Professor Castella,

I am an independent researcher studying a specific CM newform f = 4.5.b.a (LMFDB; weight 5, level 4, CM by K = Q(i), Steinberg at p = 2 with a_2 = -4). My focus is the anticyclotomic Iwasawa Main Conjecture for this form.

The difficulty is that K = Q(i) and p = 2 places us outside the standard BDP hypothesis: p is **ramified** (not split) in Q(i), disc(K) = -4 is even, and the weight k = 5 gives a non-integer BDP infinity-type parameter ℓ = 3/2. The paper of Longo-Vigni-Wang (arXiv:2501.03673), which extends the Rubin formula to (1+ℓ, -ℓ) infinity types, explicitly lists these as excluded by Assumption 1.1.

Your work on BDP-type constructions and Heegner cycles (e.g. in the context of Selmer groups for CM forms) is the closest I can find to the techniques that might handle a non-split prime. My questions:

1. Is there a version of the Heegner cycle/BDP construction that applies when **p ramifies** in K (rather than splits)?
2. For K = Q(i) specifically, is the ramified case at p = 2 known — or is it genuinely open in the IMC literature?
3. If you are aware of work (yours or collaborators') in preparation addressing this, I would be grateful for a pointer.

I have numerically confirmed π·L(f,1)/L(f,2) = 6/5 (80-digit verified, PARI/GP + Sage 10.7) and am trying to situate this in the Iwasawa-theoretic landscape before finalising a preprint.
