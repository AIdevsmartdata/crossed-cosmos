## 0. Statement of the gap

Brown-Fonseca [BF25] (arXiv:2508.04844, 69 pp., 2025) prove the Gross-Zagier
log-algebraicity conjecture geometrically in **level 1, weight 4** by exhibiting
new higher Green's functions as single-valued periods of a motive
constructed from the moduli stack `M_{1,3}` (elliptic curves with three marked
points). Their proof uses, as a hypothesis they verify, that `M_{1,3}` is
**mixed Tate over Z** in the sense of Deligne-Goncharov (and that this is
controlled by the kind of arguments Brown deployed for `M_{0,n}` in his 2012
proof of the Hoffman conjecture, [Br12], arXiv:1102.1312).

The ECI module M52 establishes that for the CM newform `f = 4.5.b.a`
(weight 5, level 4, CM by `K = Q(i)`),
\[
  R(f) \;=\; \pi \cdot L(f,1)/L(f,2) \;=\; 6/5 \;\in\; \Q,
\]
together with a fully rational Damerell ladder
`(α_1, α_2, α_3, α_4) = (1/10, 1/12, 1/24, 1/60)`. M95 Task A extends this
to a 4×4 lattice of `R_{m,n}` ratios, all six of which are rational
for `4.5.b.a` and for that form alone (the other three CM weight-5 dim-1 forms
tested have only 2/6 ratios in `Q`, with the other 4 in `Q(\sqrt{d}) \setminus Q`).

The mathematical question that connects M52 to BF25:

> Is `R(f) = 6/5` (and the full lattice of M52/M95) realisable as a *single-valued
> period* of an explicit motivic biextension over `Q(i)`, in the sense of [BF25]?

The blocking hypothesis identified by M84 is

> **(H1)** The moduli stack `M_{1,3}^{\Gamma_1(4)}` parametrising elliptic
> curves with three marked points and `\Gamma_1(4)` level structure, viewed
> as a Deligne-Mumford stack over `Spec\, Z[i, 1/2]`, is **mixed Tate**.

This file (i) restates Brown 2012's level-1 result and identifies *what
exactly* is used; (ii) catalogues what changes when one passes to
`\Gamma_1(4)`-level over `Z[i, 1/2]`; (iii) writes down the candidate
biextension for `f = 4.5.b.a`; (iv) identifies the Ext-group computation
where `[6/5]` should live; (v) names two specific theorems that would
close H1 in 6-12 months by a specialist.

## 1. What does Brown 2012 actually prove (and use)?

[Br12] (arXiv:1102.1312, *Annals of Mathematics*, 2012)
proves:

> The category `MT(Z)` of mixed Tate motives over `\mathrm{Spec}\, Z` is
> generated by the motivic fundamental group
> `\pi_1^{\mathrm{mot}}(P^1 \setminus \{0,1,\infty\})`.

Equivalently, every period of `MT(Z)` is a `Q`-linear combination of multiple
zeta values, and in particular the Hoffman generators `\zeta(n_1, \ldots, n_r)`
with `n_i \in \{2, 3\}` already span. As input, Brown uses
Deligne-Goncharov `[DG]` (Ann. Sci. ENS, [TBD: vol 38 (2005) 1-56, verify])
which sets up the abelian category `MT(O_{F,S})` of mixed Tate motives over
`O_{F,S}` for any number field `F` with `S` a finite set of places, gives
the formula

> `Ext^1_{MT(O_{F,S})}(Q(0), Q(n)) = K_{2n-1}(O_{F,S}) \otimes Q`

(Borel + Bloch-Kato), and `Ext^j = 0` for `j \geq 2` if `n \geq 2` (Beilinson-
Soulé vanishing for number rings, conjectural in general but **true** for `Z`
and for `O_F` ring of integers of a number field).

The two ingredients Brown 2012 needs that are particular to `M_{0,n}`:

**(BR1) Mixed-Tateness of the moduli space `M_{0,n}`.**
`M_{0,n}` is a hyperplane complement (`P^1 \setminus \{n+1\, \mathrm{pts}\}` for
`n=4`, then iterated), hence mixed Tate over `Z` for trivial reasons (the
cohomology is generated by `dz_i/(z_i - z_j)`). Concretely:
\[
  H^*(M_{0,n}) \cong \Lambda^*(\omega_{ij})/(\text{Arnold rels.}),
  \quad \omega_{ij} = d\log(z_i - z_j),
\]
and each `\omega_{ij}` is a Tate class. Therefore `M_{0,n}` is a "Tate
variety" in the strongest sense: `H^*(M_{0,n})` is a sum of `Q(-k)`'s.

**(BR2) Operadic / associator structure.**
Brown uses the cyclic operad structure on `\bigsqcup_n M_{0,n}` and the
Drinfel'd associator to identify the periods of `\pi_1^{\mathrm{mot}}(M_{0,4})`
with multiple zeta values.

When we pass to `M_{1,n}`, the genus-1 analogue, NEITHER ingredient is
automatic. `M_{1,1} \cong A^1` is mixed Tate (it's literally the `j`-line),
but `M_{1,n}` for `n \geq 2` carries an additional non-trivial elliptic
piece coming from `H^1(E)` of the universal elliptic curve.

## 2. What does Brown-Fonseca 2025 actually prove?

[BF25] (arXiv:2508.04844, 69 pp., 2025-08-06).
Subject tags: `math.NT, hep-ph, math.AG`. MSC: `11F67, 11F37, 11G15, 14F40,
14C15, 14D22, 19E15`.

What is **proven** (per the abstract, WebFetched 2026-05-06):

> "A completely geometric proof in level 1 and weight 4" of the Gross-Zagier
> log-algebraicity conjecture, **by showing that the moduli stack of elliptic
> curves with 3 marked points is mixed Tate**.

What is **conjectural** (per the abstract):

> "The Gross-Zagier conjecture is a consequence of a standard conjecture in
> the theory of motives." The standard conjecture is the existence of a
> well-behaved category of motives with the expected Ext-groups; given that,
> one can run the same argument at higher weight / level.

The Brown-Fonseca biextension at level 1, weight 4 is, in spirit:
\[
  B^{(4)} \;=\; \mathrm{Sym}^2 H^1(\mathcal{E}/M_{1,1}) \otimes \mathrm{Sym}^2 H^1
\]
(i.e., a quadruple `Sym^2`-product over the universal elliptic curve over
`M_{1,1}`), pulled back to `M_{1,3}` via the three sections. The single-valued
period giving the Gross-Zagier higher Green's function is a class in
`Ext^2_{MM}(Q(0), B^{(4)}(2))` whose Hodge realisation yields the requisite
real-analytic modular form.

The reason `M_{1,3}` is mixed Tate over `Z` (per BF25) is that, after
restricting to the locus where the three marked points are distinct from
the cusp, one can compactify and the resulting variety has a stratification
by products of `M_{1,1}`-strata each of which is mixed Tate. This is a
non-trivial check; it relies on Brown 2012 + the elliptic refinements
discussed in [Br18] (Brown, "Multiple modular values and the relative
completion of the fundamental group of `M_{1,1}`", `[TBD: verify ref]`).

## 3. What changes for `M_{1,3}^{\Gamma_1(4)}` over `Z[i, 1/2]`?

Three independent obstructions appear:

### 3.1. The base ring is not `Z` but `Z[i, 1/2]`

Mixed Tate motives over `Z[i, 1/2]` form a category `MT(Z[i, 1/2])` whose
Ext-groups are larger than `MT(Z)`. By Borel + Deligne-Goncharov,
\[
  \dim_Q \mathrm{Ext}^1_{MT(Z[i, 1/2])}(Q(0), Q(n))
  = d_n^+ + d_n^- + |S \setminus \{\infty\}|\cdot[F:Q] \cdot \delta_{n=1},
\]
where for `F = Q(i)`, `S = \{2, \infty\}`, the rank `d_n^\pm` of `K_{2n-1}(O_F)`
is `d_n^+ = 1` if `n` odd `\geq 3`, `0` else; `d_n^- = 0` if `n` even `\geq 2`
(via Borel rank + the Galois action on `K`-theory of imaginary quadratic
fields, see Borel `[TBD: Ann. ENS 7 (1974) 235-272, verify]`).

For `Q(i)` and `n = 2`, this gives `Ext^1_{MT(Z[i, 1/2])}(Q(0), Q(2))` of
dimension `1` (compared to `0` for `MT(Z)`). The extra direction is precisely
the **Beilinson regulator class** of `K_3^{\mathrm{ind}}(Z[i, 1/2]) \otimes Q`,
generated by the Bloch-Wigner dilogarithm value `D(i) = G` (Catalan's constant)
or equivalent.

This is interesting for ECI: `6/5` is *rational*, so it sits in
`Ext^0(Q(0), Q(0))^\vee = Q` — it is a period **in the rational sub-line**
of the Ext lattice. But then the *biextension realisation* of `6/5` requires
identifying the canonical class in `Ext^2_{MT(Z[i, 1/2])}(Q(0), B(2))` whose
period coincides with `R(f)`.

### 3.2. The level structure introduces ramification at 2

`\Gamma_1(4) = \{g \in \mathrm{SL}_2(Z) : g \equiv \binom{1\,*}{0\,1} \mod 4\}`.
The modular curve `X_1(4) = H^*/\Gamma_1(4)` has genus `0` and 3 cusps
[verified: standard fact, e.g. Diamond-Shurman ch. 3, [TBD: confirm cusp count]].
Width data: cusp `\infty` width 1, cusp `0` width 4, cusp `1/2` width 1.

For **`M_{1,3}^{\Gamma_1(4)}`**, the level structure adds a marking
of a 4-torsion point on the universal elliptic curve. Over `Z[1/2]`, this
is étale and the relative dimension stays 3 over `M_{1,1}^{\Gamma_1(4)}`.
Over `Z` (i.e. including `p = 2`), the level structure is RAMIFIED, hence
the qualifier `Z[i, 1/2]`.

Why also adjoin `i`? Because `\Gamma_1(4) \subset \mathrm{SL}_2(Z)` is the
stabiliser of a 4-torsion point with a chosen orientation, and for the
CM elliptic curve `E_i = C/Z[i]` the 4-torsion is `(1+i)/2`-generated,
so the natural model is over `Z[i]` rather than `Z`. (Concretely, the
canonical model `Y_1(4)/Q` has a `Q(i)`-rational 4-torsion point only
after base change to `Q(i)`.)

### 3.3. Weight 5 vs weight 4: an odd-weight subtlety

[BF25] proves weight 4 (even). For weight 5 (odd), the Eisenstein-symbol
construction differs:

- For weight `2k`, the relevant motive is `Sym^{2k-2} H^1(\mathcal{E})`,
  which is even-weight Hodge structure on the elliptic side.
- For weight `2k+1`, one combines `Sym^{2k-1} H^1(\mathcal{E})` with
  the nebentypus character. For `f = 4.5.b.a`, the nebentypus is
  `\chi_{-4}` (the non-trivial character mod 4), and the relevant
  motive is
  \[
    B_f \;=\; \mathrm{Sym}^4 H^1(\mathcal{E}/M_{1,1}^{\Gamma_1(4)}) \otimes Q(\chi_{-4}).
  \]

[TBD: verify §2 of BF25 explicitly addresses both odd and even weight.]
Per the abstract, BF25 says it "applies to both odd and even weight modular
forms" in the broader framework, but the GEOMETRIC (proven) part is
weight 4.

## 4. The biextension for `f = 4.5.b.a`

**Construction.** Let `\mathcal{E} \to Y_1(4)/Q(i)` be the universal
elliptic curve. The base `Y_1(4)_{Q(i)}` has, over `Z[i, 1/2]`, an integral
model that is a smooth curve of genus 0 with three (resp four after base
change) cusps.

Let `\mathcal{E}_i = C/Z[i]` be the CM elliptic curve over `Q(i)` with CM
by `Z[i]`. Its `H^1` decomposes via the Hecke Grössencharacter
`\psi : I_K \to C^\times` of conductor `(1+i)^2` and ∞-type `z`:
\[
  H^1(\mathcal{E}_i)_C \;\cong\; M_\psi \oplus M_{\bar\psi},
\]
where `M_\psi` is the rank-1 CM motive of `\psi`.

The CM newform `f = 4.5.b.a` is the theta-series `\theta(\psi^4)`. By the
Deligne-Serre construction, the motive of `f` is
\[
  M_f \;\cong\; \mathrm{Ind}^{Q}_{Q(i)} M_{\psi^4}
  \;\subset\; \mathrm{Sym}^4 H^1(\mathcal{E}_i).
\]
So the **candidate biextension** for the ECI period `R(f) = 6/5` is
\[
  B_f \;=\; \mathrm{Sym}^4 H^1(\mathcal{E}/Y_1(4)) \otimes Q(\chi_{-4})
  \quad\text{restricted to the CM point of `E_i`}.
\]
Equivalently, `B_f` is supported on the étale `(1+i)`-stalk of `M_{1,3}^{\Gamma_1(4)}`
above the CM elliptic curve `E_i`.

**Hodge realisation.** Over `Spec\, C`, the Betti realisation `B_{f, \mathrm{Bet}}`
is `Sym^4 H^1_{\mathrm{Bet}}(E_i, Q) \otimes Q(\chi_{-4})`, a 5-dimensional
`Q`-vector space. The de Rham realisation `B_{f, dR}` has a 5-step
Hodge filtration. By the Damerell-Shimura theorem [Dam70, Dam71, Shi76],
both `L(f, 1)` and `L(f, 2)` are periods of `B_f` on Frobenius-eigenlines:
\[
  L(f, m) \;=\; \langle \omega_m, \gamma_m \rangle_{B_f},
  \qquad m \in \{1, 2, 3, 4\},
\]
where `\omega_m \in F^? B_{f, dR}` is the Eisenstein cohomology class of
weight `m` and `\gamma_m \in B_{f, \mathrm{Bet}}^\vee` is the
corresponding cycle class.

The four classes `\omega_1, \ldots, \omega_4` lie on a single `Q`-line in
`B_{f, dR}` because `M_{\psi^4}` is rank 1, but the cycles `\gamma_m` differ
by **Tate twists**: `\gamma_2 = (2\pi i) \cdot \gamma_1` (in the appropriate
de Rham normalisation), etc. This is the motivic origin of the M52 ladder.

**Single-valued period.** In the sense of [BF25, Brown 2014, "Single-valued
multiple polylogarithms" (arXiv 1407.5167) — TBD verify], the single-valued
period of an `MT(O_S)`-motive is the de Rham–de Rham comparison after
applying complex conjugation; it lives in a **rational `Q`-vector space**
even when the periods themselves are transcendental. The conjecture of
[BF25] is that the single-valued period associated to the
biextension `B_f(2)` is precisely the higher Green's function appearing
in the Gross-Zagier formula.

**The class `[6/5]`.** The ratio `R(f) = 6/5` should correspond to a
specific class in
\[
  \mathrm{Ext}^2_{MM(Z[i, 1/2])}(Q(0), B_f(2))
\]
whose single-valued period equals `6/5`. This Ext-group is ZERO under the
Beilinson-Soulé vanishing axiom for `n \geq 2` and `B_f` mixed-Tate-pure,
because then `B_f(2)` is a Tate twist of a pure motive of weight `8`,
and `Ext^2 = 0` by the Beilinson-Soulé theorem for number rings. So the
class `[6/5]` should sit in **Ext^1 of a SHIFTED biextension**:
\[
  [6/5] \;\in\; \mathrm{Ext}^1_{MM(Z[i, 1/2])}(Q(0), B_f^{(1)}(2))
\]
where `B_f^{(1)}` is a 1-step extension of `B_f` (a "biextension" in the
sense of Mumford-Grothendieck). Explicitly, `B_f^{(1)}` is the
Deligne–Beilinson 1-extension classified by the Eisenstein-Kronecker
class of the cusp `0` of `X_1(4)`.

This is the analogue, for level 4 weight 5, of [BF25]'s construction at
level 1 weight 4. The HEART of the H1 problem is that, to make this
construction work over `Z[i, 1/2]`, one needs `M_{1,3}^{\Gamma_1(4)}` to
be mixed Tate, exactly as in [BF25].

## 5. The Ext computation (Beilinson regulator goal)

By Borel + Deligne-Goncharov (cf. [DG, Sec. 1] and [Br12, Cor. 5.6]), for
`F = Q(i)` and `S = \{(1+i), \infty\}` (so `O_S = Z[i, 1/2]`):

| n | `\dim Ext^1_{MT(O_S)}(Q(0), Q(n))` | description |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | `\#S - 1 = 1`        | global units mod torsion: `K_1(O_S) = O_S^\times \mod\,\mu` |
| 2 | `0`                  | `K_3(O_S)_Q = 0` for imag quad with `S = (1+i), \infty` (Borel rank `r_2 - r_1 = 1 - 0 = 1`, but for `Q(i)` `r_2 = 1`) [TBD: Borel rank check] |
| 3 | `1`                  | `K_5(O_F)_Q = Q` for imag quad |
| 4 | `0` or `1` | TBD |

[TBD: the precise Borel ranks for `K_*(Z[i, 1/2])` need a clean reference;
candidates include Levine "Mixed motives" [book, K-theory monograph TBD]
or Esnault-Levine "Tate motives and the fundamental group" [TBD]. M95
flags this as a clean lookup that takes the specialist 30 minutes
but takes a sub-agent ~1 hour with high hallucination risk for specific
Borel rank values.]

The Ext computation goal:

> **Goal.** Identify the class in `Ext^2_{MM(Z[i, 1/2])}(Q(0), B_f(2))`
> (or its Ext^1 shift via the biextension construction of §4) whose
> single-valued period equals `R(f) = 6/5`.

If `B_f` were "mixed Tate" (i.e., a successive extension of Tate twists
`Q(j)`), then `Ext^k(Q(0), B_f(2))` would decompose as a sum of standard
Beilinson regulator targets, and the identification of `[6/5]` would reduce
to a Borel regulator computation of an explicit 1-cycle. Because `B_f`
involves `\mathrm{Sym}^4 H^1(\mathcal{E}_i) \otimes Q(\chi_{-4})`, the
mixed-Tate hypothesis is **exactly H1**.

## 6. Two specific theorems that would close H1

The following theorems, if proven, would close the H1 hypothesis. Each is
within reach of a specialist working full-time for 6-12 months.

### Theorem H1.A (mixed Tate stack at `\Gamma_1(4)`, dim 1).

> **Theorem [target].** The Deligne-Mumford stack
> `M_{1,1}^{\Gamma_1(4)}` over `Z[i, 1/2]` is mixed Tate. Equivalently, its
> motivic cohomology `H^*_{\mathrm{mot}}(M_{1,1}^{\Gamma_1(4)}, Q(\bullet))`
> generates the same `\otimes`-subcategory of `MM(Z[i, 1/2])` as the Tate
> objects `Q(0), Q(1), Q(2), \ldots`.

**Why this is reachable.** `M_{1,1}^{\Gamma_1(4)} = Y_1(4) \times M_{1,1}/SL_2(Z)`-stuff;
explicitly `Y_1(4) \cong P^1 \setminus \{3 \text{ cusps}\}`, hence a hyperplane
complement modulo a finite group. Brown 2012 + Goncharov techniques apply
**modulo the issue of integrality at primes dividing the level (here 2)**.
The base ring `Z[i, 1/2]` is the natural choice that avoids the wild
ramification at `p = 2`.

**Strategy.** (a) Compactify `Y_1(4)/Z[i, 1/2]` to a smooth proper curve of
genus 0; (b) verify the cusps form a totally split divisor over `Z[i, 1/2]`;
(c) apply Deligne-Goncharov [DG, Thm. 4.2 — TBD verify] to conclude
mixed-Tateness of `M_{1,1}^{\Gamma_1(4)}`.

### Theorem H1.B (relative cohomology of universal elliptic curve, dim 1).

> **Theorem [target].** Let `\pi : \mathcal{E}^{\Gamma_1(4)} \to
> M_{1,1}^{\Gamma_1(4)}` be the universal elliptic curve with `\Gamma_1(4)`
> level structure over `Z[i, 1/2]`. Then `R^1\pi_* Q_\ell` (resp. its
> de Rham/Hodge counterparts) is **NOT mixed Tate**, but the
> `\mathrm{Sym}^4` of its Eisenstein quotient
> `Eis^4 := \mathrm{Sym}^4 R^1\pi_* / (\text{cuspidal part})` IS mixed Tate
> over `Z[i, 1/2]`.

**Why this is reachable.** The cuspidal part of `Sym^4 R^1\pi_*` is the
motive of cuspforms of weight 5 level 4 nebentypus `\chi_{-4}`, which is
`M_{4.5.b.a} \cong \mathrm{Ind}^Q_{Q(i)} M_{\psi^4}`. Outside this cuspidal
part, the remaining "Eisenstein part" `Eis^4` has a Hodge structure of
type `(0,4) + (1,3) + (2,2) + (3,1) + (4,0)` with each line generated by
an Eisenstein-Kronecker class. By the Deligne-Beilinson construction,
each such class is a Tate motive over `Q(i)` after restricting to the CM
locus; so `Eis^4` is mixed Tate.

**Strategy.** (a) Identify the Eisenstein decomposition of `Sym^4 R^1\pi_*`
via Eichler-Shimura (cf. Scholl 1990, "Motives for modular forms",
Invent. Math. 100, [TBD verify]); (b) show the Eisenstein piece is mixed
Tate by checking each Eisenstein-Kronecker class is in `MT(Z[i, 1/2])`;
(c) reduce H1 to Theorem H1.A via the Leray spectral sequence.

### Why these are TWO theorems and not one

H1.A handles the BASE; H1.B handles the FIBRES (and explains why the
cuspidal piece is treated separately — it's where `f = 4.5.b.a` lives).
The combination (H1.A + H1.B + a routine Leray argument over `Z[i, 1/2]`)
implies H1 for the relevant cohomological piece supporting the
biextension `B_f`. The cuspidal piece does NOT need to be mixed Tate —
the BF25 framework treats cusp forms as a "purely motivic" input, and
single-valued periods of them are computable directly via Petersson
inner products.

## 7. Probability estimate (honest)

Per M84, D4-#7 (Brown-Fonseca × M52) sits at **20-25%** probability of
yielding a publishable connection to `R(f) = 6/5` within 12 months. After
the M95 deepening, I revise to **18-22%** because:

- **Up:** The biextension `B_f = Sym^4 H^1(\mathcal{E}_i) \otimes Q(\chi_{-4})`
  is now WRITTEN DOWN, not just sketched. The Ext^2 computation has a
  precise target. The two H1 theorems are concrete and have specialist
  parties (Brown himself, Fonseca, possibly Asvin G or Roy Joshua at
  Ohio State [TBD: confirm interest]).
- **Down:** The technical work of verifying Theorem H1.B is genuinely
  hard. `Sym^4 R^1\pi_*` involves degree-4 elliptic motives, and the
  Eisenstein decomposition over a non-trivial level (here 4) is more
  intricate than the level-1 case. Estimated 6-12 months by a specialist;
  realistically 12-18 months for a paper.
- **Down:** The CONNECTION from `Ext^2_{MM}` class to "the single-valued
  period equals 6/5" is non-trivial; it requires the **Beilinson regulator
  conjecture** (BSD-style equality of motivic and analytic L-values) at
  weight 5, level 4, CM. This conjecture is OPEN in the form needed.
- **Up:** M52 anchors the analytic side. The 4×4 lattice from M95 Task A
  gives 6 independent rational entries (cubed in 4.5.b.a), providing more
  numerical evidence for any motivic story than a single ratio would.
