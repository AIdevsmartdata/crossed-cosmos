WHAT THE PAPER DOES

ECI framework axiom A6 uses PH_k (persistent homology in degree k) on
cosmological density super-level sets as a complexity observable.
Until v6.0.3, this was postulated.  The paper derives it from microlocal
sheaf theory under two hypotheses:
  M1: the density field is constructible
  M2: the persistence module is tame

MAIN THEOREM (Theorem 2.1, section 3): There is a functor
  Psi: {tame persistence modules over k} --> D^gamma(V; k)
satisfying:
  E1: PH_k(f) lifts canonically to the pushforward R f_* k_X
  E2: Psi is an isometry between interleaving and convolution distances
      (derived isometry theorem: Berkouk-Ginot 2018, arXiv:1805.09694)
  E3: Stability: d_conv <= ||f - g||_{L^inf}
  E4: Barcode recovery via Krull-Schmidt decomposition in D^gamma

COROLLARY 3.2: Under M1+M2, ECI axiom A6 is DERIVED: PH_k is the
degree-k cohomology of R f_* k_X in D^gamma(V; k).

SECTION 4 — HAMILTONIAN INTERTWINING:
The modular Hamiltonian flow sigma_t^omega (Bisognano-Wichmann) corresponds
to a Hamiltonian isotopy phi_t^H.  Theorem 4.3 constructs the sheaf
quantization K^H (via Guillermou-Kashiwara-Schapira 2012, arXiv:1005.1517)
and proves the intertwining equation:
  Psi(PH_k(sigma_t^omega . f)) ≅ K^H_t o Psi(PH_k(f))  in D^gamma(V; k)
