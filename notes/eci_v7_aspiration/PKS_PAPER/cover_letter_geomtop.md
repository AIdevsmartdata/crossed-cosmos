Dear Editors of Geometry & Topology,

I submit the attached manuscript, "Microlocal sheaves and the
persistent-homology axiom: an equivalence theorem," for consideration
for publication.

**Scientific content.** The paper establishes a precise categorical
equivalence between the persistent-homology functor PH_k and the
derived category D^gamma(V; k) of gamma-sheaves on a finite-dimensional
real vector space V, equipped with the convolution distance of Kashiwara
and Schapira.  The main theorem (Theorem 2.1) shows that:

(E1) every tame persistence module PH_k(f) lifts canonically, via the
pushforward sheaf R f_* k_X, to a gamma-sheaf;

(E2) the lift is an isometry between the interleaving distance and the
convolution distance, via the derived isometry theorem of Berkouk and
Ginot (arXiv:1805.09694);

(E3) the lift is stable under L^infinity perturbations of f, recovering
the classical bottleneck stability;

(E4) the barcodes of PH_k(f) are recovered by the Krull-Schmidt
decomposition of the lifted sheaf.

As a structural contribution, Section 4 constructs a sheaf quantization
of the modular Hamiltonian flow (Bisognano-Wichmann / Tomita-Takesaki)
via the Guillermou-Kashiwara-Schapira framework (Duke Math. J. 161,
2012, arXiv:1005.1517), and proves an intertwining identity showing that
the modular flow acts on persistence barcodes via a Fourier-Mukai
functor.

**Relation to existing work.** The paper is firmly anchored in the
microlocal sheaf literature:
- Kashiwara-Schapira (arXiv:1705.00955): the microlocal interpretation
  of barcodes as gamma-sheaves, and the stability theorem for direct
  images
- Guillermou-Kashiwara-Schapira (arXiv:1005.1517, Duke Math. J. 2012):
  the sheaf quantization of Hamiltonian isotopies, the microsupport
  microsupport non-displaceability criterion
- Berkouk-Ginot (arXiv:1805.09694): the derived isometry theorem
- Berkouk-Ginot-Oudot (arXiv:1907.09759): the level-sets persistence
  and sheaf theory connection
- Berkouk-Petit (arXiv:1902.09933, AGT 21, 2021): ephemeral persistence
  modules and the observable category

The paper's contribution is to synthesise these results into a single
equivalence theorem with a clean proof outline, and to extend the
framework to the Hamiltonian intertwining setting relevant for
mathematical physics applications.

**Why Geometry & Topology.** The paper lies squarely at the interface
of symplectic geometry (Hamiltonian isotopies, microsupport) and
algebraic topology (persistent homology, derived categories of sheaves),
which is the core audience of Geometry & Topology.  The journal has
published closely related work in microlocal sheaf theory and symplectic
topology; the present paper would fit naturally in the same tradition.
