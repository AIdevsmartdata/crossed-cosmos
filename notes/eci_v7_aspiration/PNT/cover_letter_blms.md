**"Two LMFDB identifications for hatted weight-5 multiplets
of the metaplectic cover S'_4"**

by Kévin Remondière (independent researcher).

**Summary.**
The metaplectic cover S'_4 of the modular flavour group S_4, introduced by
Novichkov, Penedo, and Petcov (Nucl. Phys. B 963 (2021) 115301;
arXiv:2006.03058), is a central extension of order 48 whose "hatted"
representations carry modular forms of odd weight at level Γ(4).
The paper establishes two identifications between forms in this metaplectic
setting and classical newforms in the LMFDB:

1.  The hatted triplet **3̂,2(5)** (weight 5) coincides with the unique
    newform **LMFDB 4.5.b.a**: a CM form with complex multiplication by
    Q(i), level 4, weight 5, nebentypus χ₄, root number +1. Four
    independent verifications are presented, including a direct sympy
    q₄-expansion computation from the Novichkov–Penedo–Petcov formula,
    a verbatim q-expansion match with LMFDB to a(40), and the Grössen-
    character formula a(p) = 2·Re((a+bi)⁴) for p = a²+b² (verified at
    five primes p ∈ {5, 13, 17, 29, 37} beyond the Sturm bound B = 3).

2.  The hatted doublet **2̂(5)** (weight 5) coincides with **LMFDB
    16.5.c.a**: a non-CM form of level 16, weight 5, with coefficient
    field Q(√−3) and Galois dimension 2 (the orbit is not self-dual).
    The identification is verified at 14 primes p ≡ 1 (mod 4),
    p ≤ 113 — well beyond the Sturm bound B = 10 for this space.

As an immediate corollary, the restricted Hecke sub-algebra
H₁ = {T(p) : p ≡ 1 (mod 4)} acts diagonally on each multiplet, while
T(p) for p ≡ 3 (mod 4) is obstructed (10/10 primes tested, by the
χ₄-nebentypus). The first identification transfers the completed
L-function and the Damerell–Chowla–Selberg algebraic special values
(expressible via Γ(1/4)) to the metaplectic setting.

**Why this matters for BLMS readers.**
The result is a clean observation in the arithmetic of modular forms,
using only standard tools (Sturm bounds, Grössencharacter theory, Hecke
operators, LMFDB tables) but applied to a recently introduced and
mathematically rich setting. It provides the first systematic bridge
between the growing modular flavour symmetry literature
(Novichkov–Penedo–Petcov 2020; Liu–Yao–Ding 2020;
de Medeiros Varzielas–Paiva 2026) and the classical L-function database,
opening a natural classification programme: extend to all hatted
multiplets at all odd weights and to other metaplectic covers
(A'_4, A'_5, …). The paper requires no new conjecture: all claims
follow from standard theory plus explicit Hecke eigenvalue computations.
