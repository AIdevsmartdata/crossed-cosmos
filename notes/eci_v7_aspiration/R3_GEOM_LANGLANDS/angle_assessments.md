Date: 2026-05-06. Sub-agent R-3, Opus 4.7.

## Preliminaries — the geometric/arithmetic divide

GLC (Gaitsgory et al. 2024-2025, arXiv:2405.03599 + 2405.03648 + 2409.07051 + 2409.08670 + 2409.09856) is proved for:
- a smooth projective curve X over a field of characteristic 0
- the moduli stack Bun_G(X) of G-bundles
- the moduli stack LocSys_{G^∨}(X) of de Rham G^∨-local systems
- categorical equivalence L_G : D-mod(Bun_G)^{ren} ≃ IndCoh_{Nilp}(LocSys_{G^∨})

ECI's f = 4.5.b.a is a **classical (arithmetic) modular form of weight 5, level 4, with CM by Q(i)**, defined over Q — its Galois side is the ℓ-adic Hecke Grössencharacter ψ on Q(i) lifted to a 2-dim Galois rep of G_Q.

**The transfer "geometric → arithmetic" is one of THE deepest open programs of modern number theory.** It is NOT given by Gaitsgory's proof. The proof is over a curve in char 0; arithmetic Langlands lives over Spec(O_K) and is governed by the global Langlands conjecture (still wide open for general groups).

So when ECI proposes "geometric Langlands fits into ECI," we must distinguish:
1. Direct application of Gaitsgory's theorem — REQUIRES X = curve / char 0.
2. Function-field analogue — Drinfeld 1980s for GL_2.
3. Loose categorical analogy — ECI's H_1, S'_4, etc. live in classical Hecke-algebra setting.

Most plausible bridge: Xinwen Zhu **arXiv:2504.07502** ("Arithmetic and Geometric Langlands Program", 2025) — categorical methods inspire arithmetic results, but no direct theorem-transfer.

## Angle A — ECI's f as a point in Bun_{GL_2} / Hecke eigensheaf

### Claim under examination

f = 4.5.b.a (cusp form on X_0(4) of weight 5) ↔ Hecke eigensheaf F_f on Bun_{GL_2}(X) for X = X_0(4)^geom; and ECI's H_1 sub-algebra at p ≡ 1 mod 4 ↔ "split-prime restriction" of the Hecke eigensheaf.

### Issues

1. **X_0(4) is not the curve X in GLC.** In GLC, X is the curve over which we form Bun_G. We do NOT take Bun_{GL_2}(X_0(4)). The relationship between automorphic forms on X_0(N) and the curve "X" of GLC requires the function-field translation. There is no simple direct identification.

2. **What IS true and well-known**: classical f corresponds (via Eichler-Shimura / Wiles / Khare-Wintenberger style) to a 2-dim Galois rep ρ_f : G_Q → GL_2(Q_ℓ). Restricted to G_{Q(i)} this becomes the Grössencharacter ψ. The ℓ-adic local system σ_f on Spec(Z[1/2]) corresponding to ρ_f is what lives on the "arithmetic side."

3. **Geometric Langlands transfer**: One can ask for a function-field analog. For Spec(O_{Q(i)}) ≃ Spec(Z[i]) viewed analogously to a curve over a field, the geometric Langlands philosophy says: Hecke eigensheaves on Bun_{GL_2}(Spec Z[i]) ↔ rank-2 local systems on Spec(Z[i]). But Spec(Z[i]) is 1-dimensional arithmetic, NOT a curve over a field; this is the arithmetic Langlands setting. Gaitsgory's theorem does not apply.

4. **H_1 at p ≡ 1 mod 4**: in ECI, this is the closed sub-algebra T_1 ⊂ T (full Hecke algebra of GL_2) generated by {T_p : p ≡ 1 mod 4}, i.e., T_p for p split in Q(i). On the CM eigenform f = 4.5.b.a, the eigenvalue a_p factors as a_p = ψ(𝔭) + ψ(𝔭̄) where 𝔭|p in Q(i). For p split, this is the SPLIT-prime Hecke restriction.

5. **Categorical analogue**: in the Hecke category H = ⊕_V H_V (V running over Rep(G^∨) = Rep(GL_2)), the Hecke functor at a place v acts via the geometric Satake equivalence Rep(GL_2) ≃ Sat_v. There IS a notion of restriction to "split places": for v split in a quadratic extension K/F, the local Galois representation factors through G_K-locally and the Hecke action factors compatibly. **This is well-known to specialists** (e.g., Frenkel-Langlands-Ngô work, Arthur-Selberg trace formula for base change).

### Honest verdict on Angle A

**DEFER** — the analogue exists at high abstraction, but: (a) Gaitsgory's theorem does not directly apply (different setting), (b) the H_1 / split-prime sub-algebra interpretation is a classical / well-known specialist construction (e.g., automorphic induction from Q(i) to Q via Hecke 1936, JL classical), (c) ECI does NOT add new geometric content beyond the classical CM picture.

**What ECI adds**: an EXPLICIT algebra closure verification {T_p : p ≡ 1 mod 4} closed on hatted multiplets 3̂(3), 2̂(5), 3̂,2(5) verified to p=113. This is a numerical / structural finding about the S'_4 modular flavor multiplet structure, NOT about geometric Langlands. The "split-Hecke-subalgebra" framing is a re-labeling of the classical CM structure.

**Recommendation**: drop the "geometric Langlands" framing. Keep ECI's H_1 as a "split-Hecke sub-algebra of the S'_4 modular Yukawa" — classical & well-defined.

## Angle B — Geometric Eisenstein + S'_4 modular flavour

### Claim under examination

S'_4 (the binary octahedral, modular flavour group) acts on H^1(X_0(4), Q_ℓ); via geometric Eisenstein (Braverman-Gaitsgory math/9912097, Frenkel-Gaitsgory-Vilonen, GLC III 2409.07051), this action is the categorical Eisenstein lift; ECI is the τ=i specialization.

### Issues

1. **S'_4 is a finite group**, the binary octahedral 2.S_4 of order 48. In modular flavour (Feruglio 2017, NPP20 2020), it acts on the modular forms M_k(Γ(4)) via finite-quotient Γ̄_4 ≃ S_4 lifted to S'_4 with a Z/2 sign cover. **This is a finite-group action; categorical Eisenstein is not finite-dimensional.**

2. **GLC III (2409.07051) compatibility-with-parabolic-induction**: states that the Langlands functor L_G is compatible with the constant-term/Eisenstein operations of geometric induction Bun_T → Bun_B → Bun_G for parabolic B. This is structural for the categorical proof; it has no direct intersection with finite-group flavour symmetries.

3. **Mismatch of categories**:
   - S'_4 acts on M_5(Γ(4)) ≃ ⊕ multiplets. This is a finite-dim representation of a finite group.
   - Geometric Eisenstein produces an infinite-dim sheaf on Bun_G via parabolic induction.
   - The two notions don't intersect except by very general "they're both about modular forms" framing.

4. **Specific check**: Does S'_4 ⊂ Aut(modular curve X(4)) act on Bun_{GL_2}(X(4)) compatibly with Hecke? Yes — Aut(X(4)) acts on Bun_{GL_2}(X(4)) by pullback, and this commutes with Hecke (via geometric Satake formalism). But this is DEEPLY classical (since Bun is a moduli stack and Aut acts canonically). It is NOT a NEW connection between ECI and Gaitsgory.

### Honest verdict on Angle B

**DEFER (mostly DEAD-END)** — the S'_4 action on Bun_{GL_2}(X(4)) is well-known classical structure. Geometric Eisenstein operates at a different scale (categorical, infinite-dim). ECI's contribution at τ=i is a SPECIALIZATION of finite-group reps, not a NEW Eisenstein construction.

**Possible 50-word footnote**: "ECI's S'_4 modular flavour action lifts via classical functoriality to an action on Bun_{GL_2}(X(4)); compatibility with the geometric Hecke algebra is automatic from geometric Satake. This places ECI within the geometric-Langlands automorphic side conceptually, but Gaitsgory et al. (2024) does not yield arithmetic content for f=4.5.b.a beyond what classical Eichler-Shimura gives."

No paper-grade claim to extract.

## Angle C — M52 invariant 6/5 as a categorical / motivic invariant

### Claim under examination

The Ω-independent ratio **π · L(f, 1)/L(f, 2) = 6/5** (M52, verified PARI 80-digit) for f = 4.5.b.a is a categorical / motivic invariant living in the K-theory of LocSys_{GL_2}(X_0(4)) or analogous arithmetic setting; it should be expressible as a ratio of two motivic / K-theoretic classes attached to the eigensheaf.

### Reasoning

1. **L-values as periods of motivic cohomology**: by Beilinson's conjectures, special values of L(f, s) at s = 1, 2 (for weight-5 form, central + near-central points) are periods of regulators on motivic cohomology M_f ⊗ Q. Specifically:
   - L(f, 1) ↔ period of class in K_3(M_f) ⊗ Q
   - L(f, 2) ↔ period of class in K_5(M_f) ⊗ Q
   (precise indices depend on weight = 5 and the Deligne periods C^±)

2. **Ω-independence of ratio**: In ECI's M52, π · L(f,1)/L(f,2) = 6/5 cancels the period Ω_lemniscate^4 dependence (both numerator and denominator have Ω^4 in their algebraicity formulas). This is THE diagnostic that the ratio is intrinsic to f — independent of the chosen period normalization.

3. **Categorical reformulation**: in IndCoh_{Nilp}(LocSys_{GL_2}) (the spectral side of GLC), the eigensheaf F_σ for σ = local system attached to f admits a class [F_σ] ∈ K_0(IndCoh_{Nilp}). The L-values L(f, m) ought to be expressible as motivic/periods of [F_σ] paired against natural classes.

4. **The conjecture (R3 NEW)**:

> **Conjecture (R3-C-1)**: For f = 4.5.b.a with CM local system σ_ψ on Spec(Z[1/2]) attached to Hecke Grössencharacter ψ : I_{Q(i)} → C^* of conductor (1+i)^3, infinity-type z^4: the Ω-independent ratio **π · L(f, 1)/L(f, 2) = 6/5** equals the ratio [c_1(F_{σ_ψ}, motivic-deg-1)]/[c_2(F_{σ_ψ}, motivic-deg-2)] in K_0(IndCoh_{Nilp})_Q for the eigensheaf F_{σ_ψ}, where the "motivic" (Beilinson regulator) classes are the natural ones of weight 1 and 2 in the deg-shifted Brauer-cohomology coming from the local system.

This is **highly speculative** but PRECISE: it conjectures that the rational number 6/5 (lemniscate-period-clean) lifts to a categorical / K-theoretic identity.

### What needs to be checked

(a) Beilinson's conjectures for f = 4.5.b.a (weight 5, level 4) explicitly worked out — this requires computing K_{3,5}(M_f) regulator periods. Likely doable via Damerell ladder explicit (M52 already verified L-values).

(b) ECI's K_0 class on the eigensheaf side — does the categorical equivalence L_G translate the Galois Ω-independent ratio to a clean K-theoretic ratio? Open.

(c) A LOWER bar version: even if (b) is hard, one can check that 6/5 = 1 + 1/5 and ask whether the "1" and "1/5" decompose as motivic contributions from cuspidal vs Eisenstein components — concrete & testable via the motivic L-function.

### Honest verdict on Angle C

**SPECULATIVE-VIABLE-NEW-PISTE** with ~30-40% probability of yielding a publishable theorem. The conjecture is novel (not obviously in the literature), precise enough to attempt, and connects ECI's M52 finding to a structural categorical setting.

**Risks**:
- Beilinson's conjectures for weight 5 are partially open.
- The "categorical equivalence respects motivic regulators" is itself a deep open question (related to Deligne-Beilinson, MTM motives over Z[i]).
- Even if the conjecture is true, it may turn out to be a tautology once unpacked.

**Cost to test**: ~50-100 CPU-hr SageMath + Magma to compute Beilinson regulator classes for f = 4.5.b.a (weight 5 makes this hard but possible — see Bertolini-Darmon-Prasanna 2013 on similar weights).

**Distinguishing feature**: ECI provides the EXPLICIT specialization at τ=i (j=1728, lemniscate period). Geometric Langlands talks about Bun, LocSys abstractly; ECI proposes a SPECIFIC eigensheaf at the CM point with rational L-value ratio. This concrete-vs-abstract bridge IS what could justify "ECI adds something new."
