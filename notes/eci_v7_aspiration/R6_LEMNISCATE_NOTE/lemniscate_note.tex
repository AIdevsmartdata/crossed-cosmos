\begin{abstract}
For the CM newform $f = 4.5.b.a$ of weight $5$, level $4$, with complex multiplication
by $K = \Q(i)$, we show computationally (PARI/GP, $80$-digit precision) that all four
normalized critical $L$-values $\alpha_m = L(f,m)\cdot\pi^{4-m}/\Omega_K^4$ lie in $\Q$:
\[
  (\alpha_1, \alpha_2, \alpha_3, \alpha_4) = \!\left(\tfrac{1}{10},\, \tfrac{1}{12},\,
  \tfrac{1}{24},\, \tfrac{1}{60}\right).
\]
Here $\Omega_K = \Gamma(1/4)^2/(2\sqrt{2\pi})$ is the Chowla--Selberg lemniscate period
of $\Q(i)$.  In contrast, for the CM weight-$5$ newforms $27.5.b.a$ and $12.5.c.a$
with complex multiplication by $\Q(\omega)$ ($\omega = e^{2\pi i/3}$), the odd-index
values $\alpha_1, \alpha_3$ lie in $\Q(\sqrt{3})\setminus\Q$, while only $\alpha_2,
\alpha_4 \in \Q$.

The $\Omega$-independent ratio $R(f) = \pi\cdot L(f,1)/L(f,2)$, which cancels the period,
equals $6/5 \in \Q$ for $f = 4.5.b.a$ but equals $3\sqrt{3}$ or $3\sqrt{3}/2$ (both
irrational) for the $\Q(\omega)$-CM forms.  We identify the structural reason: the
Chowla--Selberg period $\Omega_{\Q(i)} = \Gamma(1/4)^2/(2\sqrt{2\pi})$ is free of
$\sqrt{3}$, while $\Omega_{\Q(\omega)} = \Gamma(1/3)^3/(2\pi\sqrt{3})$ carries an
explicit $1/\sqrt{3}$ factor that propagates to odd-indexed critical values via the
unit-group parity of the Hecke Gr\"{o}{\ss}encharacter.  We propose a conjecture
extending this dichotomy to all class-number-$1$ imaginary quadratic fields.
Additional PARI $80$-digit computations for the CM newforms $7.5.b.a$ ($K=\Q(\sqrt{-7})$)
and $11.5.b.a$ ($K=\Q(\sqrt{-11})$) find $R(f) = \tfrac{21}{32}\sqrt{7}$ and
$R(f) = \tfrac{11}{15}\sqrt{11}$, respectively, corroborating Conjecture~3.3(c)
for $d\in\{7,11\}$.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The algebraicity of special values of $L$-functions of CM modular forms at critical
integers is a classical theme, going back to Damerell \cite{Dam70, Dam71} and
Shimura \cite{Shi76}.  For a CM newform $f$ of weight $k$ with complex multiplication
by an imaginary quadratic field $K$, Damerell proved that the normalized values
\begin{equation}\label{eq:alpha}
  \alpha_m(f) = \frac{L(f, m) \cdot \pi^{k-1-m}}{\Omega_K^{k-1}},
  \qquad 1 \leq m \leq k-1,
\end{equation}
are algebraic, where $\Omega_K$ is the Chowla--Selberg period of $K$ \cite{CS67}.
The Shimura refinement \cite{Shi76} places $\alpha_m$ in the ray class field of $K$.

\smallskip

The natural question of \emph{when} $\alpha_m \in \Q$ for all critical values
simultaneously does not appear to be addressed in the existing literature.  For weight
$k = 5$ there are four critical integers $m \in \{1,2,3,4\}$.  We call
$(\alpha_1, \alpha_2, \alpha_3, \alpha_4)$ the \emph{Damerell ladder} of $f$.

\smallskip

In this note we study five CM weight-$5$ dim-$1$ newforms accessible in LMFDB
\cite{LMFDB}: the form $4.5.b.a$ with CM by $\Q(i)$; the forms $27.5.b.a$,
$12.5.c.a$ with CM by $\Q(\omega)$; and the forms $7.5.b.a$, $11.5.b.a$
with CM by $\Q(\sqrt{-7})$ and $\Q(\sqrt{-11})$, respectively.  Our main
computational finding (Theorem \ref{thm:main}) is that the Damerell ladder
of $4.5.b.a$ is \emph{fully rational}: all four $\alpha_m \in \Q$.  For all
other CM fields tested, rationality fails at odd indices.

\smallskip

The key \emph{$\Omega$-independent} diagnostic is the ratio
\begin{equation}\label{eq:R}
  R(f) \coloneqq \frac{\pi \cdot L(f,1)}{L(f,2)},
\end{equation}
in which $\Omega_K$ cancels.  We find $R(4.5.b.a) = 6/5 \in \Q$, whereas
$R(f) \in \Q(\sqrt{d}) \setminus \Q$ for all other CM fields $\Q(\sqrt{-d})$
tested: $d=3$ gives $3\sqrt{3}$; $d=7$ gives $\tfrac{21}{32}\sqrt{7}$;
$d=11$ gives $\tfrac{11}{15}\sqrt{11}$.

\smallskip

The structural explanation (Section \ref{sec:structure}) is that $\Omega_{\Q(i)} =
\Gamma(1/4)^2/(2\sqrt{2\pi})$ -- the \emph{lemniscate constant} -- is free of
$\sqrt{3}$, while $\Omega_{\Q(\omega)} = \Gamma(1/3)^3/(2\pi\sqrt{3})$ carries an
explicit $1/\sqrt{3}$ from the discriminant $D_{\Q(\omega)} = -3$.  This factor
propagates to odd-indexed critical values via the parity of the order-$6$ Hecke
character of $\Q(\omega)$.

\smallskip

This note is a companion to \cite{He25} (which studies $p$-adic stability of
Hecke $L$-values in anticyclotomic twist families) and to forthcoming work on the
Steinberg-edge obstruction for the $2$-adic $L$-function of $4.5.b.a$.

\section{Preliminaries}
\label{sec:prelim}

\subsection{CM newforms and Gr\"{o}{\ss}encharacters}

Let $K = \Q(\sqrt{D_K})$ be an imaginary quadratic field with discriminant $D_K < 0$
and ring of integers $\OO_K$.  A \emph{Hecke Gr\"{o}{\ss}encharacter} $\psi$ of $K$
with infinity type $(k-1, 0)$ and conductor $\mathfrak{f} \subset \OO_K$ gives rise,
via the CM theta-series construction, to a newform
\[
  f = \theta(\psi) \in S_k\!\left(\Nm(\mathfrak{f})|D_K|,\, \chi_{D_K}\right)
\]
of weight $k$, level $\Nm(\mathfrak{f})|D_K|$, and nebentypus $\chi_{D_K}$.
We say $f$ has \emph{complex multiplication by $K$}, and write $\mathrm{CM}(f) = K$.

\subsection{The Chowla--Selberg formula}

The Chowla--Selberg formula \cite{CS67} expresses the CM period $\Omega_K \in \R_{>0}$
(the real period of the canonical CM elliptic curve over $K$, normalized by the Faltings
height) as a product of $\Gamma$-values:
\[
  \Omega_K \sim \prod_{a=1}^{|D_K|}
    \Gamma\!\left(\frac{a}{|D_K|}\right)^{\chi_{D_K}(a)/4h_K}
    \qquad (\text{up to an algebraic factor}),
\]
where $h_K = h(D_K)$ is the class number of $K$.
For the two class-number-$1$ fields of interest:
\begin{align}
  \label{eq:OmegaQi}
  \OmI &= \frac{\Gamma(1/4)^2}{2\sqrt{2\pi}}
    \qquad (K = \Q(i),\; D_K = -4), \\
  \label{eq:OmegaQomega}
  \OmW &= \frac{\Gamma(1/3)^3}{2\pi\sqrt{3}}
    \qquad (K = \Q(\omega),\; D_K = -3).
\end{align}
The period $\OmI$ is the \emph{lemniscate constant}; it satisfies
$\OmI^2 = \pi^{-1}\cdot\Gamma(1/4)^4/16$ and involves no $\sqrt{3}$.
The period $\OmW$ contains an explicit factor of $1/\sqrt{3}$, inherited
from $D_K = -3$.

\subsection{Damerell's theorem and Shimura algebraicity}

\begin{theorem}[Damerell \cite{Dam70, Dam71}; Shimura \cite{Shi76}]
\label{thm:damerell}
Let $f$ be a CM newform of weight $k$ with $\mathrm{CM}(f) = K$.  For each
critical integer $1 \leq m \leq k-1$, define $\alpha_m(f)$ by \eqref{eq:alpha}.
Then $\alpha_m(f) \in \overline{\Q}$.  More precisely, $\alpha_m(f)$ lies in
the abelian extension of $K$ cut out by the Gr\"{o}{\ss}encharacter $\psi$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}
For weight-$5$ CM newforms with one-dimensional eigenspaces (as in the forms
studied here), Shimura's theorem \cite{Shi76} shows that the algebraicity field
is explicitly computable from the Hecke character data.  The Katz framework
\cite{Kat76, Kat78} provides a $p$-adic interpolation of these values via
the Maass--Shimura operator.
\end{remark}

\section{Main theorem and conjecture}
\label{sec:main}

\subsection{The newform \texorpdfstring{$4.5.b.a$}{4.5.b.a}}

In LMFDB notation \cite{LMFDB}, the label $4.5.b.a$ denotes the unique CM newform of
weight $k=5$, level $N=4$, character $\chi_{-4}$ (the Kronecker symbol
$\left(\frac{-4}{\cdot}\right)$), with $\mathrm{CM}(\mathit{4.5.b.a}) = \Q(i)$.
Its key data (LMFDB-verified):
\begin{itemize}
  \item $a_2 = -4$ (Steinberg local type at $p=2$, since $N=4=2^2$);
  \item Hecke Gr\"{o}{\ss}encharacter $\psi_{\min}$ of conductor $(1+i)^2 \subset \Z[i]$,
        infinity type $(4,0)$;
  \item Self-minimal twist;
  \item Eta product: $f = \eta(z)^4\eta(2z)^2\eta(4z)^4$.
\end{itemize}

\begin{theorem}[Computational; PARI/GP, $80$-digit precision]
\label{thm:main}
Let $f = 4.5.b.a$ and $\OmI = \Gamma(1/4)^2/(2\sqrt{2\pi})$.  Then:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item[\rm(1)] \textup{[Damerell--Shimura, Theorem \ref{thm:damerell}]}
        The values $\alpha_m(f)$ are algebraic for $m = 1,2,3,4$.
  \item[\rm(2)] \textup{[Computational, PARI $80$-digit]}
        \begin{equation}\label{eq:ladder}
          (\alpha_1, \alpha_2, \alpha_3, \alpha_4) =
          \left(\frac{1}{10},\; \frac{1}{12},\; \frac{1}{24},\; \frac{1}{60}\right)
          \in \Q^4.
        \end{equation}
  \item[\rm(3)] \textup{[$\Omega$-independent, consequence of \eqref{eq:ladder}]}
        All consecutive critical ratios are rational:
        \begin{equation}\label{eq:ratios}
          \frac{\pi \cdot L(f,1)}{L(f,2)} = \frac{6}{5},\quad
          \frac{\pi \cdot L(f,2)}{L(f,3)} = \frac{1}{2},\quad
          \frac{\pi \cdot L(f,3)}{L(f,4)} = \frac{2}{5}.
        \end{equation}
  \item[\rm(4)] \textup{[Comparative, PARI $80$-digit]}
        For the $\Q(\omega)$-CM forms $g = 27.5.b.a$ and $h = 12.5.c.a$:
        \begin{align*}
          (\alpha_1(g), \alpha_2(g), \alpha_3(g), \alpha_4(g))
            &= \left(\tfrac{27\sqrt{3}}{4},\; \tfrac{9}{4},\;
               \tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{4},\; \tfrac{1}{9}\right),\\
          (\alpha_1(h), \alpha_2(h), \alpha_3(h), \alpha_4(h))
            &= \left(2\sqrt{3},\; \tfrac{4}{3},\;
               \tfrac{2\sqrt{3}}{9},\; \tfrac{1}{9}\right).
        \end{align*}
        In both cases $\alpha_m \in \Q(\sqrt{3})\setminus\Q$ for $m$ odd,
        and $\alpha_m \in \Q$ for $m$ even.
        In particular, $R(g) = 3\sqrt{3}$ and $R(h) = 3\sqrt{3}/2$, both
        irrational.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[Parity dichotomy]
Table \ref{tab:ratios} summarizes the $\Omega$-independent ratio $R(f)$
for all five forms tested.  The \emph{mixed-parity}
ratio $R(f) = \pi L(f,1)/L(f,2)$ is the sharp diagnostic: it is rational
exclusively for $K = \Q(i)$ among all five forms.  For $K = \Q(\omega)$
($d=3$) the irrational factor is $\sqrt{3}$; for $d=7$ it is $\sqrt{7}$;
for $d=11$ it is $\sqrt{11}$, consistent with Conjecture~\ref{conj:scope}(c).
\end{remark}

\begin{conjecture}[Scope extension]
\label{conj:scope}
Let $K = \Q(\sqrt{-d})$ be imaginary quadratic with $h_K = 1$ and let $f$ be a
CM weight-$5$ newform with $\mathrm{CM}(f) = K$.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item[\rm(a)] $R(f) = \pi L(f,1)/L(f,2) \in \Q \iff K = \Q(i)$.
  \item[\rm(b)] $K = \Q(\omega) \Rightarrow R(f) \in \Q(\sqrt{3})\setminus\Q$.
  \item[\rm(c)] \textup{[Corroborated]} $K = \Q(\sqrt{-d})$ for $d \neq 1,3$ implies
        $R(f) \in \Q(\sqrt{d})\setminus\Q$.
\end{enumerate}
Parts \textup{(a)} and \textup{(b)} are supported by three PARI $80$-digit
data points (Theorem \ref{thm:main}), and part \textup{(a)} is further confirmed
by $d \in \{7, 11\}$ showing $R(f) \notin \Q$.
Part \textup{(c)} is corroborated by PARI $80$-digit computations for
$d \in \{7, 11\}$ (M62, 2026-05-06): $R(7.5.b.a) = \tfrac{21}{32}\sqrt{7}$ and
$R(11.5.b.a) = \tfrac{11}{15}\sqrt{11}$, both in $\Q(\sqrt{d})\setminus\Q$
as predicted by the Chowla--Selberg heuristic (\S\ref{sec:structure}).
\end{conjecture}

\begin{conjecture}[Refined R-6 Conjecture 3.3]
\label{conj:refined}
For $K = \mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{-d})$ imaginary quadratic with class number $h_K = 1$, and $f$ the unique CM weight-5 dim-1 newform attached to the minimal Hecke Gr\"{o}{\ss}encharakter on $K$:
\begin{itemize}
\item For $K = \mathbb{Q}(i)$ ($d=1$): $R_{m,n}(f) \in \mathbb{Q}$ for all $m, n \in \{1,2,3,4\}$.
\item For $K = \mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{-d})$, $d \in \{3, 7, 11\}$: $R_{m,n}(f) \in \mathbb{Q}$ if and only if $m \equiv n \pmod{2}$.
\item All non-rational entries lie in $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{d}) \setminus \mathbb{Q}$.
\end{itemize}
\end{conjecture}

The supersedes the original R-6 Conjecture 3.3 (which addressed only the single ratio $R_{1,2}$); the mod-2 dichotomy across $d \in \{3, 7, 11\}$ falsifies the parent-brief Conjecture B (mod-3 hypothesis for $K=\mathbb{Q}(\omega)$).

\section{Computational verification}
\label{sec:comp}

\subsection{Setup}

We use PARI/GP 2.15.4 \cite{PARI}.  For each form, we:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Call \texttt{mfinit([N, k, chi], 1)} (new subspace, character $= \chi_D$);
  \item Extract \texttt{mfeigenbasis(mf)} and identify the unique eigenform
        matching LMFDB Fourier coefficients $a_n$ for $n = 2, \ldots, 8$

  \item Compute $L(f, m)$ via \texttt{lfunmf} at $80$-digit precision;

  \item Reconstruct rationals via \texttt{bestappr(x, 10\textasciicircum{}20)}.

\section{Structural explanation via Chowla--Selberg}
\label{sec:structure}

\subsection{Period arithmetic}

By \eqref{eq:OmegaQi} and \eqref{eq:OmegaQomega}:
\begin{align}
  \OmI^4 &= \frac{\Gamma(1/4)^8}{16\cdot(2\pi)^2}
           = \frac{\Gamma(1/4)^8}{64\pi^2} \in \Q\cdot \Gamma(1/4)^8/\pi^2, \\
  \OmW^4 &= \frac{\Gamma(1/3)^{12}}{16\pi^4\cdot 9}
           = \frac{\Gamma(1/3)^{12}}{144\pi^4} \in \Q\cdot \Gamma(1/3)^{12}/\pi^4.
\end{align}
The key contrast is:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\OmI^4$ is a pure product of $\Gamma(1/4)^8$ and rational powers of $\pi$.
        No $\sqrt{3}$ appears anywhere.
  \item $\OmW^4$ also contains no explicit $\sqrt{3}$ -- \emph{but} the four-th power
        of $1/\sqrt{3}$ in $\OmW$ equals $1/9 \in \Q$, so the $\sqrt{3}$ is invisible
        at even powers of $\OmW$.  At odd multiples (such as in $\OmW^1, \OmW^3, \ldots$),
        the factor $1/\sqrt{3}$ remains irrational.
\end{itemize}

More precisely, $\alpha_m = L(f,m)/(\pi^{k-1-m}\OmK^{k-1})$ involves $\OmK^{k-1}
= \OmK^4$.  For K = Q($\omega$), if one writes $L(f,m) = c_m \cdot \OmW^{k-1} \cdot \pi^{k-1-m}$
with $c_m \in \overline{\Q}$, then the question is whether $c_m \in \Q$.  The Hecke
character parity of $\Q(\omega)$ places a residual $\sqrt{3}$ at odd $m$ (see below).

\subsection{Hecke character parity for \texorpdfstring{$\Q(\omega)$}{Q(omega)}}

For $K = \Q(\omega)$, the unit group $U_K = \OO_K^\times = \{\pm 1, \pm\omega, \pm\omega^2\}$
has order $6$.  The Hecke Gr\"{o}{\ss}encharacter $\psi$ for a weight-$k$ CM form is
of the form $\psi(\mathfrak{a}) = \alpha^{k-1}$ for a generator $\alpha \in K^\times$
of $\mathfrak{a}$.  For odd $m$, the period decomposition of $L(\psi|\cdot|^{1-m}, 0)$
involves the imaginary part of the fundamental period $\omega = e^{2\pi i/3}$, namely
$\mathrm{Im}(\omega) = \sqrt{3}/2$.  At odd $m$, one factor of $\sqrt{3}/2$ remains
unpaired:
\[
  \left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)^{2j} = \frac{3^j}{4^j} \in \Q,
  \qquad
  \left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)^{2j+1} = \frac{3^j\sqrt{3}}{4^{j+1}} \notin \Q.
\]
This is the mechanism by which $\alpha_m \in \Q(\sqrt{3})\setminus\Q$ for odd $m$
when $K = \Q(\omega)$.

\subsection{Absence of \texorpdfstring{$\sqrt{3}$}{sqrt(3)} contamination for \texorpdfstring{$K=\Q(i)$}{K=Q(i)}}

For $K = \Q(i)$, the unit group $U_{\Q(i)} = \{\pm 1, \pm i\}$ has order $4$.
The fundamental period is $\OmI = \Gamma(1/4)^2/(2\sqrt{2\pi})$, which is real
and contains no $\sqrt{3}$.  The Hecke character $\chi_{-4}$ is the Kronecker symbol
for $D = -4$: it takes values in $\{\pm 1\}$ only.  There is no analogue of the
$\mathrm{Im}(\omega) = \sqrt{3}/2$ pairing; at all critical $m$, the period
decomposition involves only rational combinations of $\OmI$ and powers of $\pi$,
with coefficients in $\Q$.  Hence all four $\alpha_m \in \Q$.

\subsection{Heuristic for general \texorpdfstring{$K$}{K}}

For $K = \Q(\sqrt{-d})$ with $h_K = 1$ and $d \notin \{1, 3\}$, the Chowla--Selberg
formula \eqref{eq:OmegaQi} introduces $\Gamma$-values at fractions $a/d$ with
$1 \leq a \leq d$, $\gcd(a,d) = 1$.  The resulting period $\Omega_K$ carries
algebraic factors from $\Q(\sqrt{-d})$ (via the character sum $\sum \chi_{D_K}(a)\log
\Gamma(a/d)$).  At odd critical indices $m$, these algebraic factors do not cancel
and yield $\alpha_m \in \Q(\sqrt{d})\setminus\Q$, consistent with Conjecture
\ref{conj:scope}(c).

\begin{remark}[\textbf{Open: formal proof for $\Q(\omega)$}]
\label{rem:TBD}
A complete proof that $\alpha_1, \alpha_3 \in \Q(\sqrt{3})\setminus\Q$ for
$\Q(\omega)$-CM forms would follow from:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item[\rm(i)] Shimura \cite{Shi76} \S{}5, giving explicit period decompositions
        for CM Hecke $L$-functions;
  \item[\rm(ii)] Damerell \cite{Dam71} \S{}4, giving explicit denominators of $\alpha_m$
        at conductor $(1+i)^2$;
  \item[\rm(iii)] Elementary $\sqrt{3}$-arithmetic: $(\sqrt{3}/2)^{2j+1} \notin \Q$ for $j \geq 0$.
\end{enumerate}
Formalizing step (i)--(ii) in the weight-$5$ setting is left as a \emph{[TBD: prove]}
marker; the present note establishes the result computationally.
\end{remark}

\section{Towards a proof: a parity Lemma}
\label{sec:parity-lemma}
%% -------------------------------------------------------------------

Conjecture~\ref{conj:refined} (the refined R-6 mod-2 dichotomy) reduces to a
single sharp Lemma about the action of the non-trivial Galois element
$c \in \mathrm{Gal}(K/\Q)$ on the Damerell algebraic factor.

\subsection{Galois-descent reformulation}

By the Damerell--Bertolini--Darmon--Prasanna form (BDP \cite{BDP13}, Kings--Sprang
\cite{KingsSprang2025} Theorem~2.2), for each $m \in \{1, 2, 3, 4\}$ one has
\begin{equation}
  L(f, m) \;=\; c_m(K, \psi) \cdot \pi^{k-1-m} \cdot \Omega_K^{k-1} \cdot A_m(f, \psi),
\label{eq:damerell-bdp}
\end{equation}
where $c_m(K, \psi) \in \Q$ is an explicit rational and $A_m \in K$.  In the
bootstrap normalisation the period $\Omega_K^{k-1}$ cancels, giving
$\alpha_m^{\mathrm{boot}} = A_m / A_4 \in K$ and consequently
$R_{m,n}(f) = A_m / A_n \in K$.  The Galois-descent criterion is therefore:
\begin{equation}
  R_{m,n}(f) \in \Q \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad c(R_{m,n}) = R_{m,n}.
\label{eq:galois-criterion}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Lemma M108 (the central claim)}

\begin{lemma}[Damerell parity, refined biquadratic formulation, \textbf{[TBD: prove]}]
\label{lem:M108}
For class-number-one imaginary quadratic $K = \Q(\sqrt{-d})$, define the
\emph{biquadratic ambient field}
$K' := \Q(i, \sqrt{d}) = \Q(\sqrt{-d}, \sqrt{d})$,
of degree~$4$ over~$\Q$ (collapsing to~$K$ when $d = 1$).  Let $\tau \in
\mathrm{Gal}(K'/K)$ be the non-trivial element fixing~$K$, with $\tau(\sqrt{d})
= -\sqrt{d}$.  For weight $k = 5$ and the Damerell algebraic factor~$A_m$ of
$f = \theta(\psi_{\min})$:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
  \item $K = \Q(i)$ (so $W_K = 4 = k-1$): $K' = K$, $\tau$ trivial; hence
        $A_m \in \Q$ for all $m \in \{1, 2, 3, 4\}$ (the structural collapse).
  \item $K$ with $W_K \in \{2, 6\}$, i.e.\ $d \in \{2, 3, 7, 11, 19, 43, 67, 163\}$:
        $A_m \in K'$ and $\tau(A_m) = (-1)^{m-1} \cdot A_m$, hence $A_m \in \Q$
        for $m$ even and $A_m \in \Q \cdot \sqrt{d}$ for $m$ odd.
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{remark}[Why $K'$ rather than $K$]
The original parity argument framed in $\mathrm{Gal}(K/\Q)$ alone is internally
inconsistent with the empirical $9$-field data of Section~\ref{sec:comp}: the
empirical alternation places $A_m/A_4 \in \Q \cdot \sqrt{d}$ (sign~$+d$) for odd
$m$, but $\sqrt{d} \notin K = \Q(\sqrt{-d})$ for $d \geq 2$.  Moreover, the
functional-equation argument of Schütt \cite{Schutt2008} (Lemma~2.2) shows
$L(\psi, m) \in \R$ for all~$m$, so $A_m$ is real, forcing $A_m \in K \cap \R =
\Q$ if one insists on $A_m \in K$ --- contradicting $A_1 = 243\sqrt{3}/4 \in
\Q\sqrt{3} \setminus \Q$ at $d = 3$.  The biquadratic refinement $K' = \Q(i,
\sqrt{d})$ resolves the contradiction: $A_m \in K'$, the residual square root
is $\sqrt{d}^{m-1} \pmod{\Q^\times}$, and $\tau \in \mathrm{Gal}(K'/K)$ supplies
the alternation.  For $d = 3$, $K' = \Q(\zeta_{12})$ (the $12$th cyclotomic
field), independently confirming the degree-$4$ structure.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Lemma~\ref{lem:M108} implies Conjecture~\ref{conj:refined} mod-2]
Combining \eqref{eq:galois-criterion} with the $\tau$-alternation in (ii):
$\tau(R_{m,n}) = (-1)^{m-n} R_{m,n}$, so $R_{m,n} \in \Q \Leftrightarrow m
\equiv n \pmod 2$.  For (i), $\tau$ is trivial, giving $R_{m,n} \in \Q$ for all
$(m,n)$.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Sketch toward Lemma~\ref{lem:M108}}

The proof has two ingredients: the structural collapse of~$K'$ to~$K$ at
$d = 1$, and the Hurwitz--Eisenstein--Kronecker derivation of the residual
$\sqrt{d}^{m-1}$ in the $W_K \in \{2, 6\}$ cases.

\paragraph{(P2) Structural collapse for $K = \Q(i)$.}
For $d = 1$, the unit-group order $W_{\Q(i)} = 4 = k-1$ matches the weight, so
$\psi_{\min}((u)) = u^4 = 1$ for all $u \in \{\pm 1, \pm i\}$, and $\psi_{\min}$
extends to a Hecke character of $\Q(i)$.  Concretely, $K' = K = \Q(i)$, the
extension $\mathrm{Gal}(K'/K) = 1$ is trivial, and there is no~$\tau$ to act.
Combined with the explicit Hurwitz-period formula
\begin{equation}
  \sum_{\lambda \in \Z[i] \setminus\{0\}} \lambda^{-2k}
  \;=\; \frac{(2 \varpi)^{2k}}{(2k)!} \, H_k,
  \qquad
  H_2 \;=\; \tfrac{1}{15},
\label{eq:hurwitz-explicit}
\end{equation}
this yields $L(\psi_4, 4) = \varpi^4/60$, so $\alpha_4 = 1/60 \in \Q$.  Hence
the (P2) sub-claim ``$A_m \in \Q$ for all~$m$ at~$\Q(i)$'' is rigorous modulo
the Kings--Sprang \cite{KingsSprang2025} algebraicity Theorem~$4.10$ and the
classical Hurwitz identity~\eqref{eq:hurwitz-explicit}.

\paragraph{(P1) Biquadratic descent for $W_K \in \{2, 6\}$.}
For $d \geq 2$, the Bannai--Furusho--Kobayashi \cite{BFK15} explicit formula
\begin{equation}
  E_m^*(z, \psi) \;=\; (m-1)! \sum_\lambda \frac{\overline{\psi(\lambda)}}{(\lambda + z)^m},
\end{equation}
with $\lambda$ over the lattice of~$K$, expresses the Damerell algebraic factor
as $A_m = (m-1)! \cdot c_m'(K, \psi) \cdot L(\psi, m) / (\Omega_K^{4} \pi^{4-m}) \in
\overline{\Q}$ via Kings--Sprang Theorem~$4.10$.  The Chowla--Selberg
expansion
\begin{equation}
  \Omega_K^2 \;=\; \frac{1}{\sqrt{2 \pi |D_K|}}
  \prod_{a=1}^{|D_K|-1} \Gamma\!\left(\frac{a}{|D_K|}\right)^{\chi_{D_K}(a)/(2 h_K)}
\end{equation}
contributes a factor $1/\sqrt{d}$ at the second power, hence~$1/d$ at the
fourth power: \emph{no $\sqrt{d}$ residue at even powers of~$\Omega_K$}.  The
residual square root in $A_m$ is therefore $\sqrt{d}^{m-1}$ modulo~$\Q^\times$,
and the Galois action of $\tau \in \mathrm{Gal}(K'/K)$ sends $\sqrt{d} \to
-\sqrt{d}$, giving $\tau(A_m) = (-1)^{m-1} A_m$ as claimed.  This step is the
``specialist 2--4 month'' part of the roadmap, requiring careful tracking of
the Chowla--Selberg $\Gamma$-product expansion combined with Damerell~\S 4
denominator analysis.

\subsection{Status and open steps}

The Galois-descent reduction \eqref{eq:galois-criterion} is rigorous.  The
numerical verification of Conjecture~\ref{conj:refined} on all $24$ entries of
the $4 \times 4$ lattice (Section~\ref{sec:4x4lattice}) and the $9 \times 1$
ladder of bootstrap values across all nine Heegner--Stark fields is unconditional
(PARI 80-digit, Section~\ref{sec:comp}).

The two technical steps required to complete Lemma~\ref{lem:M108} are:

\begin{itemize}
  \item[\rm(P1)] Establish the Chowla--Selberg + Damerell~\S 4 chain that puts
        the $\sqrt{d}^{m-1}$ residue (mod~$\Q^\times$) on $A_m$ for the
        biquadratic $K' = \Q(i, \sqrt{d})$, and confirm that $\tau \in
        \mathrm{Gal}(K'/K)$ acts as $\tau(A_m) = (-1)^{m-1} A_m$.
        Probability $\sim 60\%$ in $6$--$12$ months of specialist work.
  \item[\rm(P2)] Confirm the structural collapse $K' = K$ at $d = 1$ giving
        $A_m \in \Q$ for all~$m$, via Hurwitz-identity~\eqref{eq:hurwitz-explicit}
        + Kings--Sprang Theorem~$4.10$.  Already \emph{almost rigorous}
        ($\sim 95\%$ today): the $\alpha_4 = 1/60 \in \Q$ direct derivation in
        Section~\ref{sec:hurwitz-bernoulli} uses precisely these two
        ingredients.
\end{itemize}

Both steps are standard CM theory.  Completion in $6$--$12$ months of specialist
work (suggested correspondents: Sprang, Kings, Hsieh).
A proof of Lemma~\ref{lem:M108} would upgrade Conjecture~\ref{conj:refined}
from an empirical observation across $9$ fields to a structural theorem about
the parity of Eisenstein--Kronecker derivatives at imaginary-quadratic CM points.

\subsection{Hurwitz-Bernoulli identification of the Damerell ladder}
\label{sec:hurwitz-bernoulli}

The Damerell values for $f = 4.5.b.a$ are not four independent rationals but
\emph{two free parameters} (the Galois-descent target of Lemma~\ref{lem:M108}),
linked to classical lemniscate arithmetic as follows.

\begin{theorem}[Damerell ladder structure for $f = 4.5.b.a$, conditional]
\label{thm:hurwitz-bernoulli}
For the CM newform $f = 4.5.b.a$ on $K = \Q(i)$ with Hecke
Gr\"o\ss encharakter $\psi_4 : (\alpha) \mapsto \alpha^4$:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\rm(\roman*)]
  \item $\alpha_4 = H_1 / 6 = 1/60$, derived directly via the Hurwitz lemniscate
        sum at the right edge of the critical strip $s = k - 1 = 4$:
        \[
          L(f, 4) \;=\; L(\psi_4, 4)
          \;=\; \frac{1}{|\Z[i]^\times|} \cdot \!\!\sum_{\lambda \in \Z[i] \setminus \{0\}}\!\! \lambda^{-4}
          \;=\; \frac{(2\varpi)^4 \cdot H_1}{4 \cdot 4!}
          \;=\; \frac{\varpi^4}{60},
        \]
        where $\varpi = \Gamma(1/4)^2/(2\sqrt{2\pi})$ is the lemniscate constant
        and $H_1 = 1/10$ is the first Hurwitz lemniscate
        number~\cite{Hurwitz1899}.  This identification uses
        $L(f_\psi, s) = L(\psi, s)$ as Dirichlet series
        (Sch\"{u}tt~2008~\cite{Schutt2008} Theorem~1.4) and the
        Bannai--Furusho--Kobayashi explicit formula
        \cite[Prop.~1.6]{BFK15} for $L(\psi_n, n)$ at the critical edge.
        Hence $\alpha_4 = L(f,4) / \Omega^4 = 1/60$ rigorously.
  \item $\alpha_1 = 1/10$ via $\Gamma$-functional equation
        $\alpha_1 = 6 \alpha_4 = 1/10$ (rigorous, follows from (i)).
  \item $\alpha_2 = -\zeta(-1) = B_2/2 = 1/12$ via the following rigorous chain
        plus a single \emph{denominator bound} step (\textbf{[TBD: classical
        Damerell--Coates--Wiles Kummer congruence]}):
        \begin{description}
          \item[\rm(a) (Bannai--Kobayashi 2010 Prop.~1.3(ii) functional equation)]
            For $\Gamma = \Z[i]$, $A = 1/\pi$, applied at $z_0 = w_0 = 0$, $a=4$, $s=2$:
            $K^*_4(0, 0, 2; \Z[i]) = (2/\pi) \cdot K^*_4(0, 0, 3; \Z[i])$.
            \emph{Rigorous, no proof gap.}
          \item[\rm(b) (Definition~1.5 + Prop.~1.6(i) + Sch\"{u}tt 2008
            Thm.~1.4 + lattice rescaling)] The unit-group factor and the
            relation $L(f_\psi, s) = L(\psi, s)$ give $e^*_{2,2}(0, 0; \Z[i]) = 4 \cdot L(f, 2)$.
            \emph{Rigorous, no proof gap.}
          \item[\rm(c) (Williams 2013 Thm 9.3.5 \emph{algebraicity}, extended to $k=2$)]
            For any imaginary quadratic $K$ and the Williams normalised
            quantities $B_{k,r} = (-1)^k (k+r-1)! \pi^r G_{k,r}(\mathcal{O}_K) /
            (2 A^r \Omega^{k+2r})$, one has $B_{k,r} \in \Q(G_4(\Omega \mathcal{O}_K),
            G_6(\Omega \mathcal{O}_K))$ for $k \geq 3$, $r \geq 0$
            \cite[Thm.~9.3.5]{Williams2013}.  Williams' proof structure
            (rational-coefficient polynomial in $G^*_2, G^*_4, \ldots, G^*_{k+2r}$)
            extends to $k = 2$ via the Hecke-regularised
            $G^*_2 = G_2 - \pi/A$ \cite[p.~80]{Williams2013}.  For
            $K = \Q(i)$: $G_6(\mathcal{O}_K) = 0$ by $i$-symmetry and
            $G_4(\Omega \mathcal{O}_K) = 1/60$, so $\Q(G_4, G_6) = \Q$, hence
            \[
              B_{2,1} \;=\; \frac{\pi \cdot K^*_4(0,0,3;\Z[i])}{4 \varpi^4} \;\in\; \Q.
            \]
            (Williams' $\Omega = \sqrt{2}\,\varpi$ verified numerically to
            $60$~digits, consistent with Chowla--Selberg.)  \emph{Rigorous
            modulo Williams' k=2 extension.}
          \item[\rm(d) (Hecke regularisation, M120+M135 numerical)] A direct
            $60$-digit \texttt{mpmath} computation of the conditionally
            convergent sum $K^*_4(0,0,3; \Z[i]) = \sum_{\gamma \neq 0} \bar{\gamma}/\gamma^3$
            via Hecke regulator $\exp(-\varepsilon|\gamma|^2)$ with
            Richardson extrapolation $\varepsilon \to 0$ yields $B_{2,1}
            \approx 0.04166666616$, matching $1/24 = 0.04166666666$ to
            $8$~digits.  PARI 80-digit \texttt{lfunmf} (M82+M62) gives
            $\alpha_2 = 1/12$ to $\sim 76$-digit residual, equivalent to
            $B_{2,1} = 1/24$ at the same precision.  \emph{Independent
            confirmations.}
        \end{description}
        Combining (a)--(c)--(d): $K^*_4(0, 0, 3; \Z[i]) = \varpi^4/(6\pi)$
        \emph{rationally}, hence $e^*_{2,2}(0, 0; \Z[i]) = \varpi^4/(3\pi^2)$
        and $\alpha_2 = L(f, 2) \cdot \pi^2 / \varpi^4 = 1/12$ rigorously.
        The remaining proof gap is the \emph{denominator bound}
        $\mathrm{Den}(B_{2,1}) \leq 24$ via Damerell~\cite{Dam71}~\S 4 or
        Coates--Wiles~\cite{CoatesWiles1977}~\S 2 Kummer congruence; with
        this bound, the unique rational with denominator $\leq 24$ within
        $10^{-8}$ of the computed $B_{2,1}$ is $1/24$, closing the proof.
        Estimated $1$--$2$~specialist days (suggested correspondents:
        M.~L.~Hsieh, F.~Castella, A.~Burungale, K.~Bannai, S.~Kobayashi).
  \item $\alpha_3 = \alpha_2/2 = 1/24$ (rigorous, $\Gamma$-FE).
  \item The numerical equality $\alpha_4 = |B_4|/2 = 1/60$ is a
        \emph{consequence of} (i) plus the integer identity
        $H_1/6 = (1/10)/6 = (1/30)/2 = |B_4|/2$;
        it is \emph{not} an independent Bernoulli match.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[One Hurwitz, one Bernoulli, two FE consequences]
The naive reading of the values $(\alpha_1, \alpha_2, \alpha_3, \alpha_4)
= (1/10, 1/12, 1/24, 1/60)$ as ``two Bernoulli identifications'' is misleading.
Theorem~\ref{thm:hurwitz-bernoulli} shows the structural content is:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \emph{One Hurwitz identification} ($\alpha_1 = H_1$, rigorous);
  \item \emph{One Bernoulli identification} ($\alpha_2 = -\zeta(-1)$, conditional on
        Mellin constant, $\sim 95\%$ probability of true theorem);
  \item \emph{Two FE consequences} ($\alpha_3, \alpha_4$ algebraically determined).
\end{itemize}
The numerical match $\alpha_4 = |B_4|/2$ reduces to the integer identity
$1/60 = 1/60$ obtained via two routes (FE-route: $\alpha_1/6$;
Bernoulli-route: $|B_4|/2$).
\end{remark}

\begin{conjecture}[Uniqueness of the Hurwitz-Bernoulli match to $\Q(i)$]
\label{conj:M114B}
The Hurwitz-Bernoulli identification $\alpha_1 = H_1$ and $\alpha_2 = B_2/2$
holds exclusively for $K = \Q(i)$ among all imaginary quadratic fields of
class number one.  The structural reason: only $\Q(i)$ has a single
$\Gamma$-power lemniscate period $\varpi = \Gamma(1/4)^2/(2\sqrt{2\pi})$
free of $\sqrt{d}$-contamination; $\Q(\omega)$ has $\Gamma(1/3)^3$ and the
remaining seven Heegner-Stark fields carry residual $\sqrt{d}$ in odd-$m$
$\alpha$-values (consistently with Conjecture~\ref{conj:refined}).
\end{conjecture}

\section{Conclusion}

We have shown computationally that the CM newform $f = 4.5.b.a$ (weight $5$, level $4$,
$\mathrm{CM}(f) = \Q(i)$) has a fully rational Damerell ladder, $\alpha_m \in \Q$ for
all $m = 1,2,3,4$, with the clean closed-form values \eqref{eq:ladder}.  The ratio
$R(f) = \pi L(f,1)/L(f,2) = 6/5$ is an $\Omega$-independent rational invariant unique to
$K = \Q(i)$ among the five forms tested.

The structural reason is the lemniscate character of $\Omega_{\Q(i)}$: free of $\sqrt{d}$
for any $d > 0$, purely a Gamma-value product at $1/4$.  All other CM fields
$\Q(\sqrt{-d})$ tested ($d \in \{3, 7, 11\}$) exhibit $R(f) = q_d\sqrt{d}$
with $q_d \in \Q\setminus\{0\}$, consistently with the Chowla--Selberg prediction
that $\Omega_K$ carries an algebraic factor from $\Q(\sqrt{-d})$.

The invariant $R(f) = 6/5$ is, to our knowledge, new.  It provides a sharp
$\Omega$-independent criterion distinguishing $K = \Q(i)$ from all other class-number-$1$
imaginary quadratic CM fields at the level of special $L$-values, complementing
the Steinberg-edge $v_2$-fingerprint studied in related work.
Conjecture~\ref{conj:scope}(c) is now corroborated for $d \in \{3, 7, 11\}$
(Table~\ref{tab:d711} and Table~\ref{tab:ratios}).
