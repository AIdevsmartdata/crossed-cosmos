# M1 Inequality Audit — ECI v6 / v8-bis
**Date:** 2026-05-02
**Auditor:** Sonnet sub-agent, sandbox-isolated, with direct arXiv API verification
**Commissioning context:** v6.0.15 (commit 0c4d142) introduced footnote text reframing M1 as a "conditional theorem restricted to the KMS k-design regime." This audit was commissioned to verify that the chain composes into a proof under stated hypotheses.

**Verdict (TL;DR):** No. M1 must remain **Conjecture M1-C**. The chain has two independent decisive gaps. The v6 paper's existing POSTULATE label (v6_jhep.tex lines 232–238) is the correct position; v6.0.15's "conditional theorem" framing overclaims and should be retracted in v6.0.16.

All four chain papers verified via `https://export.arxiv.org/api/query?id_list=<id>`. Background papers Witten 2022 (arXiv:2112.12828) and CLPW 2022 (arXiv:2206.10780) verified by the same route.

## (a) What each paper actually proves

### 1. Faulkner–Speranza 2024 (arXiv:2405.00847)
- **Setting:** Crossed-product gravitational algebras; **Killing horizons explicitly required**; semiclassical limit as primary regime.
- **Proves:** Crossed-product entropy = generalized entropy in the semiclassical limit. Wall's GSL reproduced as relative-entropy monotonicity in a type-II∞ algebra. GSL for arbitrary Killing-horizon cuts.
- **Hypotheses:** H1 (Killing horizon); boost-KMS on the modular flow (H2 implicit); semiclassical limit for the entropy identification.
- **Algebra type:** Type-II∞ crossed-product. Right framework for M1.

### 2. Hollands 2020 (arXiv:2009.05024)
- **Setting:** General von Neumann algebras including type-II and type-III. No finite-dimensional restriction.
- **Proves:** A variational formula for a novel Kosaki-style divergence measure. Sandwiched Rényi bounds, fidelity limits, entropic certainty relation for von Neumann subalgebras. Application to QFT via Jones index.
- **Does NOT prove:** the inequality `−dS_rel/dτ ≤ |d⟨K⟩/dτ|` that the v6 proof sketch attributes to it. Hollands proves variational formulas for entropy *functionals*, not modular-energy-variation bounds. The fit to the needed intermediate lemma is poor; the v6 sketch correctly hedges by also citing Ceyhan–Faulkner and Longo, but the lemma still does not exist in the cited form.
- **Algebra type:** Fine. The problem is content, not algebra type.

### 3. Munson et al. 2024 (arXiv:2403.04828) — PRX Quantum 2025
- **Setting:** Explicitly **n-qubit finite-dimensional Hilbert spaces**. Product Hamiltonian on qubits. Landauer erasure as operational task. Complexity entropy defined operationally for computationally bounded observers on finite registers.
- **Proves:** Minimum thermodynamic work to reset an n-qubit state via a complexity-constrained process equals the state's complexity entropy. A chain rule for complexity conditional entropy is *conjectured*, not proved.
- **Does NOT prove:**
  (i) any bound `d⟨H_mod⟩/dτ_R ≤ κ_R · C_k` in any algebra;
  (ii) anything in infinite-dimensional Hilbert spaces;
  (iii) any statement using Tomita–Takesaki theory or modular flow;
  (iv) any equivalence between *complexity entropy* (Landauer-reset cost on qubits) and *Krylov/spread complexity* C_k (the measure in M1) — these are distinct objects.
- **Algebra type:** Implicitly type-I (matrix algebras, finite-dim). **No type-II structure. This is the decisive gap.**

### 4. Kirklin 2024 (arXiv:2412.01903) — JHEP 2025
- **Setting:** Perturbative gravity to all orders beyond semiclassical, no UV cutoff. Type-II∞ crossed-product (Faulkner–Speranza framework). Dynamical cuts as quantum reference frames.
- **Proves:** Modified GSL to all perturbative orders, reducing to the standard semiclassical GSL with the usual energy conditions. Entropy differences between regions outside two dynamical cuts bounded by a free-energy term.
- **Does NOT prove:** anything involving complexity or k-design.
- **Algebra type:** Type-II∞. No transplant issue; complexity is simply absent.

## (b) Composition analysis

**Solid links.** Faulkner–Speranza → Kirklin (explicit inheritance, type-II∞ throughout). Witten 2022 / CLPW 2022 → Faulkner–Speranza (shared algebraic home).

**Broken links — two independent gaps, each individually decisive:**

**Gap 1 (principal — the transplant failure).** Munson et al. prove a qubit-register thermodynamic theorem using complexity entropy for Landauer reset. Getting from there to `|d⟨H_mod⟩/dτ_R| ≤ κ_R · C_k` in a type-II∞ crossed-product requires three unpublished steps:
  (a) extending complexity entropy to semifinite type-II trace-class operators;
  (b) identifying the Landauer-reset operational task with a modular-flow operation on A_R;
  (c) establishing that complexity entropy and Krylov/spread complexity C_k agree on the relevant state space.
None of these three exists in published literature. The chain rule central to Munson's own applications is conjectured, not proved, within that paper.

**Gap 2 (source identification).** Even granting the transplant, no paper in the chain (and no paper in the broader literature as of 2026-05-02) proves the specific bound `|d⟨H_mod⟩/dτ_R| ≤ κ_R · C_k` in a type-II factor. The v6 proof sketch correctly labels this step as arising from Brown–Susskind (heuristic) and Caputa–Magán (structural scaffolding), neither of which is a proof.

**Published math.OA literature on the transplant.** Witten 2022 (arXiv:2112.12828), CLPW 2022 (arXiv:2206.10780), Chandrasekaran–Penington–Witten (arXiv:2209.10454) establish the type-II framework and GSL. None address complexity bounds. The transplant question is not proved possible or impossible — it is simply not addressed in published form.

---

## (c) Verdict

**M1 cannot be promoted to a theorem with H1–H4. It must remain Conjecture M1-C.**

H1–H4 are necessary consistency conditions for the conjecture, not sufficient hypotheses for a theorem. The 4-paper chain provides motivational scaffolding but not a proof. The two gaps are independent — fixing one does not fix the other.

The v6 paper is already mathematically honest: M1 is labelled **POSTULATE** at v6_jhep.tex lines 232–238 ("no type-II theorem is claimed"); the assumption summary at lines 586–594 states "a type-II theorem is absent from the literature." The v6.0.15 reframing as a "conditional theorem restricted to the KMS k-design regime" is not supported by the chain and would be a misrepresentation if left in.

---

## (d) Recommendation for v8-bis (and immediate v6.0.16 patch)

1. **Retain Conjecture M1-C.** Do not promote to theorem.
2. List H1–H4 as *necessary conditions for internal consistency*, not as *sufficient hypotheses implying the inequality*.
3. Add a half-page remark titled *The finite-dimensional complexity-thermodynamics gap* stating precisely: Munson et al. (arXiv:2403.04828) prove a thermodynamic work-cost theorem for n-qubit type-I systems; transplanting to type-II∞ crossed-product A_R requires three unpublished steps [(a)–(c) above].
4. Cite Munson et al. only as a finite-dimensional analogue that *motivates* the conjecture, with the transplant gap flagged inline. Do not cite as direct support.
5. State explicitly that proving or refuting M1-C in the type-II setting is the primary open mathematical problem left by this work.

Promoting M1 to a theorem in v8-bis on the basis of this chain would be inaccurate and, given the repo's documented history of post-publication citation audits, a predictable liability. Plan B (Conjecture M1-C) is the correct position; v8-bis should preserve it and make the transplant gap explicit. The v6.0.15 footnote text introduced on 2026-05-02 should be retracted in v6.0.16 with an explanatory paragraph.
