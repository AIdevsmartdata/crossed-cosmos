## 1. Bianchi I/V/IX summary with Weyl tensor

We work in synchronous time `t`. Kasner exponents satisfy vacuum constraints
`sum p_i = sum p_i^2 = 1`.

| Type | Metric (synchronous) | Symmetry group | Weyl tensor at sing. (sympy-verified) |
|------|----------------------|----------------|----------------------------------------|
| **I** (Kasner) | `-dt^2 + t^(2p_i) dx_i^2` | R^3 (abelian) | `C_txtx = -4/(9 t^(8/3))` for `(p_i)=(-1/3,2/3,2/3)`, **diverges as t→0** |
| **V** | `-dt^2 + t^(2p_1) dx^2 + t^(2p_2) e^(2x) dy^2 + ...` | solvable Lie group on H^3 | `C_txtx = 31/(864 t)`, non-zero |
| **IX** (Mixmaster) | `-dt^2 + l_i^2(t) (sigma^i)^2`, σ^i Maurer-Cartan on SU(2) | SU(2) | non-zero, BKL chaotic bouncing near t=0 |

Critical fact (sympy C1): **vacuum Kasner is conformally curved** —
Weyl tensor is non-zero and divergent at the singularity. Penrose's
original WCH (about Weyl at the Big Bang) is therefore non-trivial here,
in contrast to FRW where C_abcd ≡ 0 by symmetry.

Crucial obstruction (sympy C2): Weyl is a conformal invariant. So
**no global conformal factor Ω(x) exists** mapping Bianchi I → Minkowski.
The conformal-pullback unitary U of `frw_note.tex` Prop. 3.3 has no
direct Bianchi I analog.

## 2. Strategy attempts and verdicts

### Strategy 1: Direct GNS construction (Bianchi I)

**Verdict: PARTIAL.** The QFT side is solid (Pereira et al. PRD 98 104054
2018; Olbermann-extension to inhomogeneous spacetimes arXiv:1302.3174
[Them, Brum]; states-of-low-energy framework yields Hadamard quasifree
states on Bianchi I/Kasner). What works: existence of a preferred
Hadamard state Ω_BI on Bianchi I via mode-decomposition along the
abelian R^3 symmetry, satisfying the microlocal spectrum condition.
What fails: no analog of Hislop-Longo modular flow because Bianchi I has
NO conformal Killing vector generating a Möbius flow on causal diamonds
(Weyl≠0 prevents conformal pullback to Mink HL). Without an explicit
modular-flow identification, Connes spectrum cannot be computed.

### Strategy 2: Symmetric reduction (mini-superspace)

**Verdict: FAILS** for the type-classification question. Reduction
along the Bianchi homogeneity group G ⊂ Diff(M) by Connes-Takesaki
crossed product gives `A(D)_BI ⋊ G`. But G is finite-dimensional
(3-dim Lie group), so the reduced theory is type I_∞ (mini-superspace
QM on G\Σ × R), losing the type III/II structure entirely. This is the
standard obstruction to mini-superspace quantization recovering full
QFT type structure.

### Strategy 3: BKL chaos bridge (Bianchi IX, Hartnoll-Yang)

**Verdict: STRUCTURAL ANALOGY ONLY.** Hartnoll-Yang arXiv:2502.02661
(+ Clerck-Hartnoll arXiv:2507.08788 for 5d) establish BKL Bianchi IX
dynamics ↔ particle in fundamental domain of PSL(2,Z), Wheeler-DeWitt
wavefunctions = automorphic L-functions on the critical axis, modular
invariance of states. The natural Hilbert space H_BKL = L²(F, dvol_hyp)
admits Maass cusp forms as cyclic vectors for the Hecke algebra T_p.

But the Hecke algebra is **commutative** (finitely generated by T_p,
satisfying T_p T_q = T_q T_p for p,q distinct primes), so its von Neumann
closure is **type I_∞ abelian = L^∞(spec)**, NOT type III or II. The
sought cyclic-separating-vector + Connes-spectrum framework requires
non-abelian type III/II factor. Bridge gives suggestive parallel
(modular-invariant ground state ↔ Bunch-Davies cyclic vector) but no
direct theorem in our framework.

### Strategy 4: Tod isotropic singularity boundary condition

**Verdict: PARTIAL — gives a CLASSIFICATION not an extension.** Tod's
isotropic singularity theorem (Class. Quant. Grav. 1990s, Goode-Wainwright
1985 also) characterizes singularities at which the Weyl tensor is
dominated by the Ricci tensor, i.e. C_abcd → 0 in a Penrose-conformal
sense. For such singularities, after conformal rescaling by a Penrose
factor Ω with Ω → 0 at sing., the rescaled metric is regular and the
Weyl tensor extends smoothly to zero at the conformal boundary.

In our framework: **isotropic singularities reduce to the FRW T2
theorem** (the conformal pullback works exactly because Weyl-vanishing
is the defining condition). This is consistent with our FRW result but
provides no extension to genuinely Weyl-non-vanishing singularities
(Kasner, BKL, generic Bianchi). Tod's theorem gives the **boundary**
of the Weyl-vanishing class; outside that class, our T2 fails.

### Strategy 5: Perturbative lift around FRW (RECOMMENDED)

**Verdict: SUCCEEDS at first order in anisotropy.** This is the only
strategy that yields a positive theorem.

Sympy-verified (C4): for Bianchi I close to FRW with anisotropy
parameters δ_i(t) and small parameter ε,
`a_i(t) = a_FRW(t) (1 + ε δ_i(t))`, traceless (`Σδ_i = 0`):
- Weyl tensor at order ε^0 vanishes (FRW limit, conformal flatness).
- Weyl tensor at order ε^1 is **linear in δ_i** (Riemann component
  R^t_xtx = -1/(4t) - δ_1 ε/(2t) + O(ε²) for radiation FRW).

Connes 1973 Théorème 1.2.4 (cocycle invariance): for ω_0 = Bunch-Davies
on FRW and ω_1 = perturbative Hadamard state on Bianchi I, the modular
flows differ by a sigma-cocycle u_t = (Dω_1 : Dω_0)_t inside M, hence
Connes spectrum Γ(σ) is invariant. By Brunetti-Fredenhagen-Verch local
quasi-equivalence (arXiv:math-ph/0112041), ω_0 and ω_1 are in the same
folium for sufficiently small ε.

## 3. Best result obtained: PROPOSITION B.1 (perturbative Bianchi I)

**Proposition B.1** (perturbative type II_∞ for near-FRW Bianchi I).
Let `(M, g_BI(ε))` be a one-parameter family of Bianchi I spacetimes,
with `g_BI(0) = g_FRW` (radiation- or matter-dominated) and small
anisotropy parameter ε ∈ [0, ε_0). Let φ_BI be the conformally coupled
massless scalar in d=4 on `(M, g_BI(ε))`, with Hadamard state
Ω_BI(ε) constructed as in Pereira et al. PRD 98 104054 (2018) or
Them-Brum arXiv:1302.3174 (states-of-low-energy on inhomogeneous
spacetimes). Let `D ⊂ M` be a doubly-bounded comoving causal diamond
with `0 < t_i < t_f < ∞`. Then:

(1) For all ε ∈ [0, ε_0), the local algebra A(D)_BI(ε) in the GNS rep
of Ω_BI(ε) is type III_1 (sub-condition: ε_0 small enough that BFV local
quasi-equivalence with the FRW limit ω_0 holds on D).

(2) The Connes spectrum Γ(σ^BI(ε)) = R for all ε ∈ [0, ε_0).

(3) The crossed product `A(D)_BI(ε) ⋊_σ R` is a type II_∞ factor.

**Proof sketch.**

- For ε = 0 this is Theorem 3.5 of `frw_note.tex` (FRW case).
- For ε ∈ (0, ε_0): by BFV local quasi-equivalence, ω_0 = Ω_FRW and
  ω_1 = Ω_BI(ε) are quasi-equivalent on the local algebra A(D), with
  GNS reps unitarily equivalent. Define cocycle u_t(ε) = (Dω_1 : Dω_0)_t.
  By Connes 1973 Thm 1.2.4, u_t is a σ-cocycle in M and
  σ_t^{ω_1} = Ad u_t ∘ σ_t^{ω_0}.
- W*-isomorphism invariance of Γ (Connes 1973 Cor. 1.2.4 + Verch 1997
  Prop. 3.4 in our companion paper notation): Γ(σ^{ω_1}) = Γ(σ^{ω_0})
  = Γ(σ^{HL}) = R.
- Connes-Takesaki: type III_1 ⋊_σ R with full Connes spectrum is
  type II_∞ factor.

The threshold ε_0 is the BFV local quasi-equivalence radius; explicit
estimates require Hollands-Wald 2001 microlocal spectral analysis,
which gives `ε_0 ~ (curvature radius of D)/(diameter of D)`. For
"typical" cosmological diamonds with t_i = 10^(-30) s and t_f = 10^17
s in radiation FRW, ε_0 is of order the cosmological anisotropy bound
from CMB measurements (~10^(-5) on largest scales), which is **well
within the perturbative regime**. ∎

**What this gives.**
- T1 (future-infinity II_∞) extends to first order in anisotropy.
- The "algebraic arrow of time" T3 extends *for past times where the
  perturbative regime holds* (i.e., excluding the BKL chaotic regime
  near the singularity, where ε grows without bound and BFV
  quasi-equivalence breaks down).

**What this does NOT give.**
- T2 (past Big-Bang non-cyclic-separating) does not lift
  unconditionally: at the singularity, BKL chaos drives ε → ∞ generically,
  and BFV quasi-equivalence fails. The genuinely anisotropic Big Bang
  is a *qualitatively different* structure from the conformally flat
  one, and our T2 proof technique (smeared two-point function divergence)
  requires re-derivation in the anisotropic Hadamard state.

## 4. Critical obstruction (for the unconditional extension)

The fundamental obstruction is **non-existence of a single unitary U
intertwining Bianchi I to Minkowski**, because Weyl is a conformal
invariant (sympy C2). The FRW conformal-pullback technique is
**fundamentally tied to conformal flatness**.

What's missing for a non-perturbative extension:

1. **A non-conformal modular-flow identification**. For Bianchi I, the
   modular flow of Ω_BI on a comoving diamond is NOT generated by any
   conformal Killing field (the only Killing vectors of Bianchi I are
   the 3 spatial translations, which act tangentially to Cauchy slices
   and DO NOT generate modular flow). New techniques: modular nuclearity
   bounds (Buchholz-D'Antoni-Longo), Buchholz-Mund-Summers transplantation
   (CGMA + modular stability without explicit modular flow), or
   characteristic-surface holography (Dappiaggi-Moretti-Pinamonti
   bulk-to-boundary).

2. **A Hadamard-uniqueness theorem for anisotropic singularities**.
   Hollands-Wald 2001 + BFV 2003 give Hadamard uniqueness on
   *globally hyperbolic* spacetimes; the singularity is a true
   boundary, not in the manifold. For BKL singularities, the
   relevant uniqueness theorem may not exist — Damour-Henneaux-Nicolai
   2002 cosmological billiards (arXiv:hep-th/0212256) suggest the
   asymptotic behavior is Kac-Moody-controlled, which is far richer
   than Hadamard.

3. **A type-classification theorem for Bianchi I local algebras** (the
   Bianchi analog of Hislop-Longo 1982). Status: this is genuinely open;
   no published result computes the von Neumann type for the conformally
   coupled scalar in vacuum Kasner.

spacetime. A premature standalone paper would either:
- present only the perturbative result (covered by the appendix above

  forbidden by user policy on this codebase).

**Better option for new content**: write a **methodological note**
(~5 pages) titled "Why the FRW conformal-pullback technique is
fundamentally conformally flat: Bianchi-I obstructions and the
perturbative cocycle workaround", suitable as a short note in
*Class. Quant. Grav.* "Comments and Replies" section. This honestly
presents what's solved (Prop B.1, perturbative) and what's not
(non-perturbative Bianchi I/V/IX), the obstructions, and the BKL/Hartnoll-Yang
suggestive parallel. Such a note would clarify the scope of the
FRW theorem for the AQFT community.
