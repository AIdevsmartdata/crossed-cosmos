\subsection{Nested-diamond Kosaki index obstruction}\label{ssec:nested-diamond}

The trio open problem of~\cite[item 4]{KrylovTrioClose2026} asks whether the
inclusion of local algebras of nested FRW comoving diamonds has a Kosaki/Jones
index equal to $\exp(C\,R_{\text{proper}}(\eta_c))$ for some absolute constant
$C>0$. We resolve this in the negative for the standard index.

\begin{theorem}[Nested-diamond Kosaki index is infinite]\label{thm:nested-index}
Let $D_\OO(\eta_i,\eta_f)$ and $D_{\OO'}(\eta_i',\eta_f')$ be two
doubly-bounded comoving causal diamonds in radiation-dominated FRW with
$0<\eta_i<\eta_i'<\eta_f'<\eta_f<\infty$. Let $\AA(D_\OO)_\FRW$ and
$\AA(D_{\OO'})_\FRW$ be the associated local von Neumann algebras in the
GNS representation of the conformal vacuum (Section~\ref{sec:algebras}).
Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] By isotony, $\AA(D_{\OO'})_\FRW \subset \AA(D_\OO)_\FRW$, and the
inclusion is W$^*$-isomorphic via $\mathrm{Ad}\,U$ (Proposition~\ref{prop:U-iso})
to the corresponding split inclusion of Minkowski local algebras
$\AA(M_{\OO'})_\Mink \subset \AA(M_\OO)_\Mink$ on the conformally identified
nested Minkowski diamonds $M_{\OO'}\subset M_\OO$.

\item[(ii)] The Minkowski inclusion in (i) is a standard split inclusion of
type III$_1$ factors in the sense of Doplicher--Longo~\cite{DoplicherLongo1984};
the type-III$_1$ classification of $\AA(M_\OO)_\Mink$ for the free massless
scalar is due to Buchholz--Wichmann~\cite{BuchholzWichmann1986} and the
splitness for nested causal diamonds bounded away from each other follows from
the nuclearity / trace-class condition of Buchholz~\cite{Buchholz1974}, see
also~\cite{BuchholzDAntoniLongo1990}.

\item[(iii)] No normal conditional expectation
$E : \AA(D_\OO)_\FRW \to \AA(D_{\OO'})_\FRW$ exists. Equivalently, the Kosaki
index of the inclusion is infinite:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:kosaki-infty}
[\AA(D_\OO)_\FRW : \AA(D_{\OO'})_\FRW]_{\mathrm{Kosaki}} = +\infty.
\end{equation}

\item[(iv)] In particular, the conjectured identity
$[\AA(D_\OO)_\FRW : \AA(D_{\OO'})_\FRW] = \exp(C\,R_{\text{proper}}(\eta_c))$
is false in any non-trivial restricted class of nested FRW diamonds: the
left-hand side is $+\infty$ by~\eqref{eq:kosaki-infty}, while the right-hand
side is finite ($R_{\text{proper}}(\eta_c) = 2 a_0 \eta_c R_d <\infty$ for
$0<\eta_c, R_d<\infty$, sympy-verified in
\texttt{/tmp/nested\_diamond\_index.py}).
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
(i) The unitary $U$ of Proposition~\ref{prop:U-iso} is built independently of
the choice of diamond (its action on the Fock space is determined by the
field rescaling $\varphi = \tilde\varphi/a$ on the joint Cauchy data). The
test-function rescaling $f\mapsto a^3 f$ preserves supports, so for any open
$\OO''\subset \overline{D_\OO}$ with $\overline{\OO''}\subset D_\OO$ we have
$U\AA(\OO'')_\FRW U^{-1} = \AA(M_{\OO''})_\Mink$. Applying this to
$\OO''=D_{\OO'}$ gives the W$^*$-isomorphism of inclusions.

(ii) Type III$_1$: standard free-field result for double cones in 4D
Minkowski (Buchholz~\cite{Buchholz1974}, Buchholz--Wichmann~\cite{BuchholzWichmann1986}
on causal independence and the energy-level density of states; Wollenberg
1992), transported by the conformal-pullback unitary $U$ as in the proof of
Theorem~\ref{thm:main}. Splitness: Buchholz--D'Antoni--Longo~\cite{BuchholzDAntoniLongo1990}
\emph{Nuclear maps and modular structures II} establishes the distal split
property for a wide class of models including the free massless scalar;
nested causal diamonds with non-zero spacelike separation between
$\partial D_{\OO'}$ and $\partial D_\OO$, which follows from
$\eta_i<\eta_i'$ and $\eta_f'<\eta_f$, fall in this class.

(iii) For a split inclusion $N\subset M$ of type III$_1$ factors, the relative
commutant $N'\cap M$ is a non-trivial type III von Neumann algebra (the image
of one tensor factor in the splitness factorization
$N\subset N\otimes \mathbb{C}\subset N\otimes M' \subset \mathcal{B}(\HH)$).
Any normal conditional expectation $E:M\to N$ would have to restrict to a
normal state on $N'\cap M$; equivalently a tracial weight on the type III
factor $N'\cap M$, which does not exist. Hence no normal $E$ exists, and the
Kosaki index, defined as $\inf_E \mathrm{Ind}(E)$ over the (empty) set of
normal conditional expectations, is $+\infty$. This argument is recorded in
the literature in the form ``spacetime inclusions cannot have finite
index''~\cite[\S2]{LeutheusserLiu2025} (see also discussion in
\cite[\S3]{XYZ2026qftprotocol}).

(iv) Direct computation of $R_{\text{proper}}(\eta_c)$ in radiation-dominated
FRW: using $a(\eta)=a_0\eta$, the integrated proper distance from $\eta_i$ to
$\eta_f$ along the comoving worldline is
\begin{equation}
R_{\text{proper}}(\eta_c) := \int_{\eta_i}^{\eta_f} a(\eta)\,\mathrm{d}\eta
 = \frac{a_0}{2}\bigl[(\eta_c+R_d)^2-(\eta_c-R_d)^2\bigr] = 2 a_0 \eta_c R_d.
\end{equation}
For $\eta_c, R_d>0$ this is finite, hence $\exp(C\,R_{\text{proper}}(\eta_c))<\infty$
for any $C\in\mathbb{R}$, contradicting~\eqref{eq:kosaki-infty}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Holographic redefinition]\label{rem:holo-redef}
Leutheusser--Liu~\cite{LeutheusserLiu2025} introduce a \emph{new} index
$(\mathcal{M}:\mathcal{N})_{\text{holo}} := \exp(C\,\mathrm{Vol}(\mathfrak{b}))$,
where $\mathfrak{b}$ is a maximal-volume bulk slice in AdS subregion-subalgebra
duality. Their explicit disclaimer (their Section~II): ``it is not clear if
[this] can be related to the Kosaki index. Instead, we may view [it] as a
definition of a new index using holography.'' Theorem~\ref{thm:nested-index}
shows that the corresponding identification fails for the genuine Kosaki index
in the FRW comoving-diamond setting. Whether an analogous \emph{holographic}
identity holds for FRW is open and would require an independent definition of
the bulk-volume analogue $\mathrm{Vol}(\mathfrak{b}_{\text{FRW}})$, which we
do not have.
\end{remark}

\begin{question}[Non-Kosaki invariant matching $\exp(C\,R_{\text{proper}})$]
\label{q:non-kosaki-invariant}
Does there exist an algebraic invariant $J(N\subset M)$ of the standard split
inclusion $\AA(D_{\OO'})_\FRW \subset \AA(D_\OO)_\FRW$ of type III$_1$
factors such that
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] $J\in(0,\infty)$ for every nested pair with
$0<\eta_i<\eta_i'<\eta_f'<\eta_f<\infty$, and
\item[(b)] $J = \exp(C\,R_{\text{proper}}(\eta_c))$ for some absolute
constant $C$?
\end{enumerate}
Candidates explicitly excluded by Theorem~\ref{thm:nested-index}: the Kosaki
index, the Jones index, the $\mu$-index of Kawahigashi--Longo--Müger
\cite{KLMmui1999}, and the dimension of the relative commutant. Candidates
worth investigating (each non-trivial): the Buchholz--D'Antoni--Longo
\emph{split distance}~\cite{BuchholzDAntoniLongo1990}, the Connes--Wassermann
fermionic depth invariant transported via boson--fermion duality, and the
Longo \emph{entropy of localized states}~\cite{Longo2020Ent}. An affirmative
answer would close the Krylov--Diameter trio chain
of~\cite{KrylovTrioClose2026}; a negative answer would establish that the
``volume = log index'' programme of Leutheusser--Liu cannot be lifted from AdS
to FRW without additional data.
\end{question}
