## Section A — Acceptance criteria for any v8 proposal

Before we accept any mathematical programme as a host for v6/v7, it
must provide, RIGOROUSLY:

1. A type-II semifinite von Neumann algebra or a faithful substitute
   (type II_1 factor, crossed product, or explicitly-isomorphic replacement).
2. A one-parameter automorphism group with physical time interpretation
   (Tomita–Takesaki flow, or the arithmetic analogue).
3. A complexity functional C: States → R_{≥0} compatible with Haar
   random-unitary saturation and Brown–Susskind monotonicity.
4. A topological invariant on classical density fields (PH_k or a
   categorical substitute — e.g. an Euler characteristic on a persistence
   module, or a characteristic class on a gerbe).
5. A natural differential inequality structure OR an equivalent
   integrated form (modular-time derivative + source term + activator).

If a programme supplies (1)-(3) rigorously and (4)-(5) programmatically,
it is a HOOK-PROGRAMMATIC candidate. If it supplies all 5 rigorously, a
HOOK-RIGOROUS candidate.

## Section G — What we refuse to produce

- A new "law" of any kind.
- A new "fundamental constant".
- A new falsifier (cosmological or otherwise) without a D19+ pipeline.
- A claim of "connection" between programmes that rests only on shared
  terminology (e.g. "both have `complexity`").
- Any Einstein-analogy framing.

If the three agent reports only yield ANALOGY-level findings, the honest
deliverable is a 100-word document saying exactly that, with the
identification of which programmes to watch for future progress.
