# v8 Math Survey — p-adic / Adelic / Motivic Hooks into the ECI v6 Framework

**Author:** research-survey agent (Opus 4.7), 2026-04-22
**Scope:** Does any p-adic, adelic, or motivic programme in mathematical
physics provide *rigorous* mathematical hooks into the four v6 ingredients:
(i) type-II crossed-product algebra \(\mathcal{A}_R\);
(ii) modular (Connes–Tomita–Takesaki) flow \(\tau_R\);
(iii) \(k\)-design complexity \(\mathcal{C}_k\);
(iv) persistent-homology topological activator \(\Theta(\mathrm{PH}_k)\)?
**Constraint:** negatives reported honestly. Per PRINCIPLES.md rule 1,
every citation below has been verified via WebSearch against arXiv or
journal metadata before inclusion. No new equations, no unification

## 1. Surveyed Programmes and Key References

### 1.1 p-adic mathematical physics (Volovich school)

- **Volovich (1987)**, *Number Theory as the Ultimate Physical Theory*
  (reprinted *p-Adic Numbers, Ultrametric Analysis and Appl.* 2010; preprint
  copy at empslocal.ex.ac.uk). Proposes that sub-Planckian spacetime is
  non-Archimedean. No modular-flow or type-II statements.
- **Vladimirov, Volovich, Zelenov (1994)**, *p-Adic Analysis and
  Mathematical Physics*, WS. Standard reference for p-adic QM with
  complex-valued wavefunctions on \(\mathbb{Q}_p\); builds a
  Heisenberg–Weyl representation but not a Tomita–Takesaki theory.
- **Dragovich et al. (2009)**, *On p-Adic Mathematical Physics*,
  arXiv:0904.4205 (verified). Survey of p-adic QM, adelic strings,
  p-adic path integrals. Entropy only at the level of counting states on
  \(\mathbb{Q}_p\); no type-II crossed product.
- **Aniello, Mancini, Parisi et al. (2023)**, *A p-Adic Model of Quantum
  States and the p-Adic Qubit*, *Entropy* 25(1), 86 (verified, MDPI).
  Introduces p-adic statistical operators and a p-adic counterpart of
  POVMs, and — crucially — defines **von Neumann and Rényi entropies in
  the p-adic setting**. Still commutative / trace-class formalism; no
  modular automorphism group.

### 1.2 p-adic / arithmetic holography

- **Manin, Marcolli (2002)**, *Holography principle and arithmetic of
  algebraic curves*, arXiv:hep-th/0201036, *ATMP* 5, 617 (verified).
  Identifies Euclidean AdS\(_3\) black-hole data with Arakelov geometry
  at arithmetic infinity.
- **Heydeman, Marcolli, Saberi, Stoica (2016)**, *Tensor networks,
  p-adic fields and algebraic curves: arithmetic and the
  AdS\(_3\)/CFT\(_2\) correspondence*, arXiv:1605.07639 (verified).
- **Gubser, Knaute, Parikh, Samberg, Witaszczyk (2017–2019)**
  p-adic AdS/CFT on Bruhat–Tits trees; JHEP 04 (2019) 170 shows
  **Ryu–Takayanagi-type entanglement entropy** holds on the tree.
  Entropy is area of a cut through the tree, a **classical**, not
  crossed-product, quantity.
- **Marcolli (2023+)**, lecture notes *Entropy, holography, and p-adic
  geometry* (caltech CMS75HolographySlides.pdf, verified) — explicitly
  articulates an entropy / holography programme over \(\mathbb{Q}_p\).

### 1.3 Adelic / Bost–Connes quantum statistical mechanics

- **Bost, Connes (1995)**, *Hecke algebras, type III factors and phase
  transitions with spontaneous symmetry breaking in number theory*,
  *Selecta Math.* 1 (verified). **Explicit type III\(_\lambda\) factors,
  KMS\(_\beta\) states, modular group generated by \(\log p\)**.
- **Connes, Marcolli, Ramachandran (2006)**, *KMS states and complex
  multiplication*, Part II (Abel Symposium preprint, verified).
- **Laca, Larsen, Neshveyev (2012)**, *Bost–Connes systems associated
  with function fields*, arXiv:1112.5826 (verified). Type III\(_{q^{-\beta n}}\).
- **Connes, Consani (2024)**, *Knots, Primes and the adele class space*,
  arXiv:2401.08401 (verified).
- **Connes, Consani, Moscovici (2023/2024)**, *Zeta zeros and prolate
  wave operators*, arXiv:2310.18423 (verified, v2 May 2024).
- **Connes, Consani, Moscovici (2025)**, *Zeta Spectral Triples*,
  arXiv:2511.22755 (verified).

### 1.4 Motivic periods and Feynman amplitudes

- **Kontsevich, Zagier (2001)**, *Periods*, in *Mathematics Unlimited*.
- **Brown (2015)**, *Notes on Motivic Periods*, arXiv:1512.06410
  (verified). Defines motivic periods, coaction on Feynman amplitudes,
  cosmic Galois group.
- **Brown (2014)**, *Single-valued motivic periods and multiple zeta
  values*, *Forum Math. Sigma* 2 (verified).
- **Brown, Schnetz**, graphical-function technology for \(\phi^4\)
  periods (2012–2022). Outputs rational functions in MZVs; no modular
  automorphism, no operator algebra.
- No 2024–2025 Brown–Schnetz **review** paper was surfaced despite
  targeted search. The programme continues but has not pivoted toward
  modular flow or generalised entropy.

### 1.5 Connes noncommutative geometry + arithmetic bridge

- **Connes, Marcolli (2008)**, *Noncommutative Geometry, Quantum Fields
  and Motives*, AMS Colloquium 55 (verified PDF at caltech). Full
  synthesis: Bost–Connes, motivic Galois theory, renormalisation,
  spectral triples.
- **Marcolli (2005)**, *Lectures on Arithmetic Noncommutative Geometry*
  (verified, AMS ULect).

### 1.6 Non-archimedean persistent homology

- **Mémoli, Needham, and Cojocaru-Belu / Elchesen et al.**,
  *A new non-archimedean metric on persistent homology* (cophenetic
  metric), *Comput. Stat.* 37 (2022) (verified via ideas.repec.org).
  Non-archimedean metric **on** the space of persistence diagrams — not
  persistent homology of a p-adic space. Purely metric-theoretic.

### 2.1 Type-II crossed-product algebra
Bost–Connes algebras are explicitly **type III**, not type II. CLPW/DEHK's
crossed-product construction takes an initial type III\(_1\) algebra and
crosses with the modular automorphism \(\mathbb{R}\)-action to obtain a
type II\(_\infty\) algebra with a trace. **No paper in the surveyed
corpus performs the analogous crossed product on a BC-type algebra.**
Verdict: **ANALOGY**. Programmatic hook exists (the modular group of the
BC KMS state is well-defined, so the crossed product is formally
constructible), but the resulting algebra has not been written down, and
no arithmetic analogue of the CLPW trace \(\operatorname{Tr}_R\) is
known.

### 2.2 Modular flow \(\tau_R\)
This is the **strongest hook**. Bost–Connes (1995) produces a C*-dynamical
system whose KMS states at \(\beta > 1\) live on type III factors; the
modular group (Tomita–Takesaki flow) is generated by
\(\sigma_t = \prod_p p^{it N_p}\), i.e. logarithms of primes. Connes,
Consani, Moscovici (2023, arXiv:2310.18423; 2025, arXiv:2511.22755)
extend this to spectral triples whose Dirac operator encodes zeta zeros.
**This is a published, rigorous Tomita–Takesaki theory on an arithmetic
C*-algebra.** It does not, however, govern any observer subregion
\(R\) of a gravitational spacetime; the "geometry" is the adele class
space, and the flow is number-theoretic, not a local Rindler
automorphism. Verdict: **HOOK-RIGOROUS** as a p-adic Tomita–Takesaki
theory; **ANALOGY** as a hook to gravitational modular flow.

### 2.3 \(k\)-design complexity
No paper in the surveyed corpus formulates unitary \(k\)-designs over
\(\mathbb{Q}_p\), over \(\mathrm{GL}_n(\mathbb{Q}_p)\), or over adelic
groups. The p-adic qubit literature (Aniello et al. 2023) defines states
and POVMs but not design ensembles; the p-adic AdS/CFT tensor networks
are finite combinatorial objects on a tree (Heydeman–Marcolli–Saberi–Stoica)
and tempt an analogy, but no **frame potential, design-depth, or
design-growth theorem** over a non-Archimedean field exists. A search
for "p-adic unitary k-design" returns zero direct matches. Verdict:
**ANALOGY at best**. The p-adic Haar measure on \(\mathrm{GL}_n(\mathbb{Z}_p)\)
would be the natural starting point; no-one has done it.

### 2.4 Persistent-homology activator
Two distinct constructions appear in the literature, **neither of them
our object**:
(a) the cophenetic non-archimedean metric on the space of persistence
diagrams (Elchesen et al. 2022) — this is a non-archimedean metric on
the **output** of ordinary persistent homology; not persistent homology
of a \(\mathbb{Q}_p\)-valued function;
(b) arithmetic topology (Mazur, Morishita; Connes–Consani 2401.08401)
treats primes as knots but does not produce filtration-indexed Betti
numbers. No motivic or arithmetic framework **naturally produces a
persistent-homology-like topological activator** of the type used in
v6. Verdict: **ANALOGY only**; the v6 \(\mathrm{PH}_k[\delta n]\)
functor has no p-adic lift in the literature.

## 3. Specific Questions from the Brief

**Q1. Is there a p-adic version of Tomita–Takesaki modular theory?**
*Yes, via the Bost–Connes system (1995) and its extensions by Connes,
Marcolli, Ramachandran (2006), Laca–Larsen–Neshveyev (2012), and the
recent Connes–Consani–Moscovici spectral-triple line
(arXiv:2310.18423, 2401.08401, 2511.22755).* These yield type III
factors with explicitly computed modular automorphism groups generated
by \(\{\log p\}_{p\text{ prime}}\). Crucially, these algebras live over
the adele class space, **not over a subregion of a semiclassical
spacetime**; transfer to a CLPW-style observer algebra has not been
performed.

**Q2. Can \(k\)-design complexity be formulated over \(\mathbb{Z}_p\)
or on adelic structures?**
*Not in the published literature.* The p-adic qubit / p-adic Hilbert
space formalism exists (Aniello–Mancini–Parisi 2023, Entropy 25, 86),
but no frame-potential or design-growth theorem has been written over
\(\mathrm{GL}_n(\mathbb{Q}_p)\) or adelic groups.

**Q3. Does any motivic / arithmetic framework naturally produce a
persistent-homology-like topological activator?**
*No.* Motivic cohomology (Voevodsky / Brown) is functorial and
non-filtered in the way v6 needs; arithmetic topology (Morishita)
supplies knot / prime analogies but no filtered Betti number. The only
non-archimedean connection to PH is metric (cophenetic, Elchesen et al.
2022), not ontological.

## 4. Bottom Line (≈100 words)

The one rigorous hook is **Bost–Connes**: a genuine p-adic
Tomita–Takesaki theory on a type III factor whose modular flow is
generated by logarithms of primes, now sharpened by Connes–Consani–
Moscovici (2024–2025). It does **not** currently give a type-II
crossed-product algebra for a gravitational subregion, because no-one
has performed the CLPW-style crossed product on an arithmetic KMS
state. For \(k\)-design complexity and the persistent-homology
activator, the p-adic / motivic literature supplies analogies at best —
no theorem applies. Net assessment: p-adic physics offers **one
programmatic bridge** to modular flow (worth a footnote in v6+1), and
**no rigorous hook** to the v6 inequality itself. Negative report
stands.
