# v8 Survey — Primes, RH, Connes programmes vs. ECI v6 modular flow

**Scope.** Honest research map: do number-theoretic/RH operator-algebra
programmes give a *rigorous* hook to the v6 JHEP paper's type-II
crossed-product GSL with modular flow $\tau_R$? No new equation; no
unification claim. PRINCIPLES rule 1 (honesty gate) applies: every
citation verified via WebSearch on 2026-04-22.

---

## 0. The v6 ingredients (what we need a hook for)

From `v6_jhep.tex` §1–§3:

- **I1.** Type-II (semifinite) crossed-product observer algebra $\AR$
  (CLPW / DEHK construction).
- **I2.** Tomita–Takesaki modular flow $\sigma^R_{\tauR}$ generated by a
  modular Hamiltonian $K_R$; $\tauR$ is **Connes–Rovelli thermal time**.
- **I3.** A *differential* GSL of the form $d\Sgen/d\tauR \ge 0$ along
  the modular orbit (upgrade from the static CLPW/DEHK inequality).
- **I4.** Spectral / $k$-design source term controlling internal
  production, tied to chaos on the factor.

Any claimed "hook" must touch at least one of I1–I4 through a
**definition** (rigorous) or a **theorem** (rigorous), not a slogan.

## 1. Programme-by-programme survey

### 1.1 Bost–Connes system (1995) — the canonical arithmetic KMS

- **Reference.** J.-B. Bost, A. Connes, *Hecke algebras, type III
  factors and phase transitions with spontaneous symmetry breaking in
  number theory*, Selecta Math. (N.S.) **1** (1995) 411–457.
  DOI 10.1007/BF01589495. Verified.
- **Structure.** C*-dynamical system $(\mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{BC}},
  \sigma_t)$ built from a Hecke algebra on $\mathbb{Q}/\mathbb{Z}$;
  $\sigma_t$ is a *one-parameter automorphism group* whose generator
  $H_{\mathrm{BC}}$ has spectrum $\{\log n : n\ge 1\}$. Partition
  function $Z(\beta)=\zeta(\beta)$.
- **Phase transition.** At $\beta=1$ (critical inverse temperature)
  spontaneous symmetry breaking by the idele class group
  $\hat{\mathbb{Z}}^*$; for $\beta>1$ KMS$_\beta$ states are unique /
  parameterised by embeddings; for $0<\beta\le 1$ **no KMS state exists**
  (the system "melts"). Verified against Wikipedia BC entry and
  Connes' own 2004 Abel notes.
- **Factor type.** Low-temperature KMS$_\infty$ yields **type III**
  factors (GNS of extremal $\beta=\infty$ state) — **not type II**. At
  infinite temperature the weak closure is type I$_\infty$.

### 1.2 Connes 1999 — trace formula / adele class space

- **Reference.** A. Connes, *Trace formula in noncommutative geometry
  and the zeros of the Riemann zeta function*, Selecta Math. (N.S.)
  **5** (1999) 29–106, DOI 10.1007/s000290050042; arXiv:math/9811068.
  Verified.
- Spectral realisation of critical zeros as *absorption spectrum* of
  the scaling action on $L^2$ of adele class space $\mathbb{A}_k /
  k^*$. Reduces RH to validity of a trace formula (Weil explicit
  formula ↔ geometric trace).
- **Not a type-II crossed product.** The algebra is the (type III)
  noncommutative space of adele classes; the relevant flow is the
  scaling/idele-class flow, which *is* a Tomita flow of a KMS state
  closely related to BC.

### 1.3 Connes–Consani: Weil positivity, $\zeta$-cycles, spectral triples

- **Weil positivity & archimedean trace formula.** A. Connes,
  C. Consani, Selecta Math. **27** (2021) art. 77,
  DOI 10.1007/s00029-021-00689-4; arXiv:2006.13771. Verified.
- **Spectral triples and $\zeta$-cycles.** Enseign. Math. **69** (2023),
  arXiv:2106.01715. Verified.
- Geometric reformulation of RH via positivity of a Weil functional and
  spectral-triple data whose Dirac operator encodes the zeta zeros.
  Spectral triples live naturally in *type II* (finite-trace)
  territory only when restricted; the core object is the scaling
  action on adelic Sonin-space cutoffs, i.e. still type III semifinite
  data with a classical (not modular) flow.

### 1.4 Connes–Consani–Moscovici 2024 — prolate wave operators

- **Reference.** A. Connes, C. Consani, H. Moscovici, *Zeta zeros and
  prolate wave operators*, arXiv:2310.18423v2 (May 2024); Annals of
  Functional Analysis **15** (2024) no. 4, art. 87,
  DOI 10.1007/s43034-024-00388-z. Verified.
- **Content.** Semilocal prolate operator = (scaling)$^2$ + grading;
  its *negative* eigenvalues (Sonin space) reproduce the UV
  asymptotics of squares of zeta zeros. Constructs an isospectral
  family of Dirac operators.
- **Sequel.** Connes–Consani–Moscovici, *Zeta Spectral Triples*,
  arXiv:2511.22755 (Nov 2025). Verified. Rank-one perturbations of the
  scaling spectral triple on $[\lambda^{-1},\lambda]$; spectra
  numerically match low zeros using only primes $p\le\lambda^2$.
- The "time" here is the generator of scaling / dilation, Mellin-dual
  to $s$ on the critical line. It is *the* Connes time but not a
  Tomita flow on a type-II crossed product.

### 1.5 Berry–Keating and the $xp$ programme

- **Reference.** M. V. Berry, J. P. Keating, *H=xp and the Riemann
  zeros*, in *Supersymmetry and Trace Formulae* (Kluwer 1999)
  355–367; and *The Riemann zeros and eigenvalue asymptotics*, SIAM
  Review **41** (1999) 236–266, DOI 10.1137/S0036144598347497.
  Verified.
- Semiclassical $H=xp$ Hamiltonian whose smoothed counting function
  matches Riemann–von Mangoldt. **No rigorous self-adjoint operator**;
  phase-space closure remains open.

### 1.6 Sierra et al. — modified $xp$ / Dirac $xp$

- G. Sierra, *The H=xp model revisited and the Riemann zeros*,
  Phys. Rev. Lett. (version) arXiv:1102.5356 (2011). Verified.
- G. Sierra, J. Rodríguez-Laguna, *H=xp with interaction and the
  Riemann zeros*, Nucl. Phys. B (arXiv:math-ph/0702034, 2007).
  Verified.
- G. Sierra, *General covariant xp models and the Riemann zeros*,
  J. Phys. A (arXiv:1110.3203, 2011); and Dirac-type xp model,
  arXiv:1205.6755 (2012). Verified.
- Modified Hamiltonian $H=x(p+\ell_p^2/p)$ admits closed orbits whose
  semiclassical quantisation reproduces the average Riemann zeros.
  Again: heuristic / semiclassical, not a rigorous algebra/flow.

### 1.7 Random matrix / Montgomery–Dyson–Katz–Sarnak

- H. L. Montgomery, *The pair correlation of zeros of the zeta
  function*, Proc. Symp. Pure Math. **24** (1973) 181–193.
- N. Katz, P. Sarnak, *Random matrices, Frobenius eigenvalues, and
  monodromy*, AMS Colloq. Pub. **45** (1999); and *Zeroes of zeta
  functions and symmetry*, Bull. AMS **36** (1999) 1–26. Verified via
  Wilson RMT notes and standard references.
- Pair-correlation of nontrivial zeros $\leftrightarrow$ GUE statistics.
  Predicts **k-design-like** statistics at the zeros — this is the
  **closest** structural overlap with v6's I4 (spectral/k-design
  source), but it is a *statistics* match, not an algebra-level hook.

### 1.8 Recent (2020–2026) modular-flow × arithmetic

- No paper surfaced in searches that constructs a **type-II**
  crossed-product factor with an arithmetic modular flow. The
  recurring objects are: (a) BC and its function-field / number-field
  generalisations (type III); (b) scaling on adele class space
  (type II$_\infty$ weights but as a noncommutative-geometric, not
  crossed-product, object); (c) CCM prolate Dirac (commutative
  operator-theoretic).
- Laca–Larsen–Neshveyev and successors extended BC to function
  fields, global fields, Toeplitz variants (e.g. arXiv:1010.4766,
  2503.03033). All **type III / Toeplitz**, not type II
  crossed-product observer algebras.

## 3. Can BC-style KMS$_\beta$ replace the v6 type-II $\rho_R(\tauR)$?

**Short answer: No — suggestive, not rigorous. Saying otherwise would
violate PRINCIPLES rule 1.**

Concretely:

1. **Factor type mismatch.** BC GNS factors at $\beta<\infty$ are
   **type III$_1$** (Bost–Connes 1995, Thm. on p. 443). CLPW/DEHK
   $\AR$ is **type II$_\infty$** by construction (crossed product of a
   type III$_1$ local algebra by the modular automorphism of the
   vacuum). A type-II trace underwrites the entire $\Sgen$ functor in
   v6 §2; BC has no semifinite trace at physical $\beta$.

2. **Time parameter mismatch.** BC $\sigma_t$ is the arithmetic
   scaling flow ($H_{BC}=\log n$); $\tauR$ in v6 is the modular
   parameter of an *observer* state on a gravitational subregion.
   Connes' thesis gives $\sigma_t$ canonical mod inner automorphisms,
   but this does **not** identify BC time with QRF time — they live
   on different algebras.

3. **Phase transition is the wrong phenomenon.** The $\beta=1$ BC
   transition is spontaneous breaking of $\hat{\mathbb{Z}}^*$, with
   order parameter given by Galois-theoretic data (class field theory
   for $\mathbb{Q}^{\mathrm{ab}}$). Nothing in v6's I3 (differential
   GSL along $\tauR$) corresponds to a symmetry-breaking KMS
   transition; dS$_{\mathrm{gen}}$/d$\tauR\ge 0$ is a *monotonicity*
   along a given modular orbit, not an order parameter.

4. **What *is* rigorous.** BC does give a genuine, textbook example of
   a Tomita flow with thermodynamic ($\beta$) interpretation and an
   explicit zeta-partition function. If v6 ever needs a *concrete
   arithmetic illustration* of "a KMS state on a C*-algebra whose flow
   is both physical and number-theoretic", BC is citable. That is a
   rhetorical, not mathematical, use.

5. **Retrofit would require** (a) constructing the crossed product
   $N \rtimes_\sigma \mathbb{R}$ of a BC-derived type III factor by
   its modular flow — yielding a type II$_\infty$ factor
   (Takesaki duality, standard), (b) identifying the new $\tauR$ with
   a physical QRF time, and (c) reproducing the v6 differential
   inequality from BC's explicit formula trace. Step (a) is routine;
   steps (b)–(c) are **open research**, not a substitution. No paper
   in the 2020–2026 window surveyed above performs (b) or (c).

## 4. What *would* be a rigorous hook (for a hypothetical v8)

- Apply Takesaki duality to the BC type-III factor to obtain its
  type-II$_\infty$ core; interpret its canonical trace as a toy
  $\Sgen$ functor. This is definitional, not deep, but it would
  upgrade row I1 to HOOK-RIGOROUS **for the toy model only**.
- Import Connes–Consani prolate Dirac as a concrete *spectral source*
  for I4 and test whether its UV eigenvalue statistics saturate a
  $k$-design bound. Would upgrade one cell of the I4 row to
  HOOK-RIGOROUS *for the statistics question*, still analogy for the
  physical GSL.
- Neither of the above collapses the analogy between arithmetic and
  gravitational $\tauR$; that would require new mathematics.

## 6. Bottom line (~100 words)

The Connes programmes (BC, adele trace formula, Weil positivity,
CCM prolate, Berry–Keating/Sierra, Montgomery–Katz–Sarnak) provide a
rich ecosystem of *Tomita flows with arithmetic content*, but none of
them lives on a type-II crossed-product observer algebra in the
CLPW/DEHK sense. The Bost–Connes system is the only rigorous
KMS-thermodynamic construction among them; its factor type (III$_1$),
its time parameter (arithmetic scaling), and its phase transition
(Galois symmetry breaking at $\beta=1$) are **structurally disjoint**
from v6's differential GSL. Substituting $\rho_R(\tauR)$ by a BC KMS
state is *suggestive, not rigorous* — a rhetorical illustration at
best. No cell in the verdict table reaches HOOK-RIGOROUS for the v6
object. Open avenue: Takesaki-dual the BC type-III factor to extract
a toy type-II$_\infty$ core, then ask the v6 questions there; this is
future work, not a v6 rewrite.
