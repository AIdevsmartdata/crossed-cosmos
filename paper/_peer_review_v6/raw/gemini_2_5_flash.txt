Q1. Is this a genuinely new formal result, or a rewriting of existing GSL statements (Wall 2011, Faulkner-Speranza 2024, Kirklin 2025)?
This is a genuinely new formal result that significantly extends existing GSL statements. While the bound recovers Wall's monotonicity and the Faulkner--Speranza modular form in the $\Theta \to 1$ limit, its novelty lies in introducing $k$-design complexity as the driving source term and incorporating a topological activator $\Theta$ derived from a dequantised density field. Furthermore, the modular-time differential nature of the bound represents a distinct structural advancement over previous static type-II second laws. It's an extension and refinement, not a mere re-derivation.

Q2. Which of M1, M2, M3 is the weakest postulate? Should any be demoted to "conjecture" or strengthened?
M3 (Topological activator profile) is the weakest postulate, as it is explicitly labeled "CONJECTURAL" and relies on an externally inspired parameter $\alpha$ that is treated as a prior-anchored fit. M2 (Semi-classical recovery of $\dn$), labeled "ANSATZ," is also quite weak, having only partial verification on a toy model and lacking a full proof for Gaussian recovery. M1, though a "POSTULATE" without a formal type-II theorem, is the most robust as it is anchored in established complexity theory. M3 should remain a conjecture until a first-principles derivation for $\alpha$ is provided, and M2 critically needs strengthening with a full semi-classical proof.

Q3. Referee recommendation: PUBLISH / MINOR REVISIONS / MAJOR REVISIONS / REJECT? Justify in one paragraph.
MAJOR REVISIONS. The paper presents a compelling conceptual framework and a potentially important differential-inequality upper bound. However, its immediate publishability is hampered by the foundational reliance on three unproven assumptions, M1, M2, and especially M3, which are explicitly acknowledged by the authors as "POSTULATE," "ANSATZ," and "CONJECTURAL," respectively. The strength of the main result, though stated as an inequality, critically hinges on these assumptions. While the paper genre allows for formal statements, the current work would be substantially stronger with more rigorous derivations or extensive theoretical justification for M2's Gaussian recovery and M3's specific functional form and parameters. The current "proof sketch" relies too heavily on these unproven claims for direct publication.

Q4. One specific additional result the authors could derive (NOT "more rigour" handwaving) that would make the paper stronger. Max 2 sentences.
The authors could derive a first-principles theoretical anchor for the exponent $\alpha$ in M3, potentially from the underlying algebraic structure of $\AR$ or a more fundamental aspect of persistent homology. Such a derivation would elevate M3 from a "CONJECTURAL" fit parameter to a theoretically grounded quantity, significantly strengthening the predictive and explanatory power of the bound.
