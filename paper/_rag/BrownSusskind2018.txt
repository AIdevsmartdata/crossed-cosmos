Brown, Adam R.; Susskind, Leonard — "The Second Law of Quantum Complexity"
arXiv:1701.01107 [hep-th] (submitted 2017-01-04; rev 2018-12)
Phys. Rev. D 97, 086015 (2018). DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevD.97.086015

Abstract: Thermodynamic framework for quantum computational complexity featuring a "Second Law of Complexity." Connects circuit complexity of K qubits to positional entropy in a classical system of 2^K d.o.f.; Kolmogorov complexity of quantum Hamiltonians maps to kinetic entropy. Uncomplexity is a resource for directed quantum computation, identified with spacetime volume behind black-hole horizons.

Why cited (v6): second law of complexity as programmatic analogue of monotone complexity source in dS_gen/dτ_R.
