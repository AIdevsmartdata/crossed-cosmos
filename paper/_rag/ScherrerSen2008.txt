                                                        The thawing quintessence model with a nearly flat potential provides a natural mechanism to
                                                      produce an equation of state parameter, w, close to −1 today. We examine the behavior of such
                                                      models for the case in which the potential satisfies the slow roll conditions: [(1/V )(dV /dφ)]2 ≪ 1
                                                      and (1/V )(d2 V /dφ2 ) ≪ 1, and we derive the analog of the slow-roll approximation for the case in
                                                      which both matter and a scalar field contribute to the density. We show that in this limit, all such
                                                      models converge to a unique relation between 1 + w, Ωφ , and the initial value of (1/V )(dV /dφ). We
