# v4.5 — A3 remediation: decision note

**Issue.** All three v4.4 peer-pre-reviewers (Claude Opus 4.7 self, Gemini 2.5
Pro, Mistral Magistral) converged on A3 (Cryptographic Censorship transposed
to FLRW/de Sitter) as the paper's single weakest element. Two remedies were
on the table: **(X)** demote A3 to a speculative-bridge paragraph, dropping it
from the numbered axioms, or **(Y)** keep A3 as an axiom and add an explicit
~1-page toy dictionary specifying a conjectural CFT→cosmology map.

**Choice: Option Y.**

**Justification.**
(1) The reviewers themselves framed the remedy disjunctively: Claude wrote
"demoted to Discussion *or* supported by a toy calculation", Magistral asked
for "more concrete derivations/evidence", Gemini flagged the absence of a
"rigorous mathematical dictionary". A dictionary directly answers all three.
(2) Option X would leave §5 "Primordial cosmology" hollow — its three
paragraphs all lean on A3, and two of them (Big-Bang-as-decodability,
inflation-as-algebraic-necessity) have no other anchor. Demoting A3 without
replacing the load-bearing text would shrink the paper by structure, not by
honest pruning.
(3) Option Y converts A3 from an interpretive layer (Gemini's complaint) into
a *testable* conjecture: the toy dictionary names a specific operational
correspondence (modular k-designs ↔ horizon coarse-grained entropy on the
observer worldline) and identifies one falsifiable consequence (a bulk-
geometry exclusion for cosmological perturbations). Honest, explicit, and
defensible at framework-section review.

Scope: §5 rewritten to depend on the dictionary, not on A3 itself;
"conditional on A3" phrasing audited (none found in §3.5/§3.6 — A3 is only
used in §5 and the §1 axiom list).
