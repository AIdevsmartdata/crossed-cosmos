This paper extends FRW Theorems 3.5/3.6 (companion paper) from spatially-flat FRW to anisotropic Bianchi I (Kasner) cosmologies. The manuscript is submission-ready: 1-2 weeks of editorial polish only; all load-bearing technical results are now closed.

**BIPP Theorem.** A_BI(D_R) is type III_1 (BFV); its modular crossed product with R is type II_∞ (Witten 2022); the modular Hamiltonian K_BI has explicit Plancherel form on the BN23 mode functions, with absolute convergence on C_c^∞(D_R) (Lemma BIPP-1) and reduction to K_FRW at the boost-generator level in the isotropic limit (Lemma BIPP-2).

**Two technical lemmas closed in this revision.**
- Lemma BIPP-1 (Plancherel convergence): ‖K_BI[f]‖² < ∞ for f ∈ C_c^∞(D_R). Proof uses BN23 Schwartz-class spatial smearing, an Olver-type WKB UV bound on the BN mode functions T_k(t), and Lebesgue dominated convergence on the modular spectral measure (Hislop-Longo 1982 for the conformal-vacuum leading contribution; BN23 Theorem 3.4 for the anisotropic Jacobian J_BN). Closed-form rho-integral ∫₀^∞ ρ⁴(1+ρ²)^{-N} dρ = (1/2) B(5/2, N-5/2) verified by sympy + mpmath@200dps.
- Lemma BIPP-2 (FRW reduction): in the isotropic limit a_1=a_2=a_3=a, ω_k(t)² becomes spherically symmetric in vec k (sympy-verified), the SO(3) rotation generator B_{12} is a Killing vector of the isotropic metric, and the per-sector modular generator K_BI^{(rho,k)} matches K_FRW^{(rho,|k|)}. Modulo a unitary intertwiner (BFV local quasi-equivalence), K_BI = K_FRW.

**Critical NO-GO caught (Lemma 4.2 / Cor 4.3).** Sympy verifies that vacuum Bianchi I has NO isotropic solution and is generically non-conformally-flat. Therefore the conformal pullback U_BI: H_Mink → H_BI used in FRW Theorem 3.5/3.6 does NOT extend to general anisotropic BI. The BIPP theorem deliberately bypasses this via Tomita-Takesaki direct on the Banerjee-Niedermaier state.
