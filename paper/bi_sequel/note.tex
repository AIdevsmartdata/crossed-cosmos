\begin{abstract}
We extend the type $\mathrm{II}_\infty$ classification of the cosmological-observer
algebra from the FRW past-light-cone diamond \cite{ECI-FRW-note,Witten2022} to
the anisotropic Bianchi-I (Kasner) backgrounds. The key technical input
is the construction of exact Hadamard \emph{States of Low Energy} on Bianchi-I
spacetimes due to Banerjee--Niedermaier \cite{BN23}, which replaces the
conformal-vacuum reference state used in the FRW case. We prove the
\emph{BIPP theorem} (Bianchi-I Past-light-cone Plancherel decomposition):
the local algebra $\cA_\BI(\cD_R)$ on a past-light-cone diamond of areal
radius $R$ is type $\mathrm{III}_1$ (BFV \cite{BFV03}), and its crossed product
by the modular flow generated by a $\BN$-state is a factor of type
$\mathrm{II}_\infty$. The modular Hamiltonian $K_\BI$ is given in closed
Plancherel form on the spatial $\T^3$. Two technical lemmas close the
construction: (BIPP-1) the Plancherel integral defining $K_\BI$ converges
absolutely on $C_c^\infty(\cD_R)$, via BN23 Schwartz-class smearing
combined with an Olver-type WKB UV bound and Lebesgue dominated
convergence; (BIPP-2) in the isotropic limit $a_1=a_2=a_3=a$ the
per-sector generator $K_\BI^{(\rho,\vec k)}$ reduces to $K_\FRW^{(\rho,|\vec k|)}$
of the companion paper, with the boost generator $B_{12}$ of the
SO(3) rotation group recovering the FRW spatial isotropy generator. We
identify two structural obstructions: (i) the conformal pullback
$U_\FRW : \cH_\Mink \to \cH_\FRW$ underlying Lemma 3.3
of \cite{ECI-FRW-note} \emph{does not} extend to general anisotropic Kasner,
because vacuum Bianchi-I admits no isotropic solution and is generically
non-conformally-flat; (ii) the $\BN$ state is Hadamard but not the
restriction of a Minkowski vacuum, so the modular Hamiltonian must be
constructed intrinsically by Tomita--Takesaki on the BFV net rather than
by pullback. The isotropic limit recovers $K_\FRW$ at the boost-generator
level (Lemma~\ref{lem:BIPP-2}). Time-to-pub: 1--2 weeks editorial polish.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The FRW past-light-cone diamond carries a type $\mathrm{II}_\infty$
crossed-product algebra whose generalised entropy $S_\mathrm{gen}=\langle K\rangle
+S_\mathrm{vN}+\Delta_\mathrm{anom}$ enters the R6 carve-out of the ECI
framework \cite{ECI-FRW-note}. Two prerequisites enter the proof:
\begin{enumerate}\setlength\itemsep{2pt}
\item BFV local quasi-equivalence \cite{BFV03} on the diamond yields a
type $\mathrm{III}_1$ factor;
\item Connes--Takesaki structure \cite{Connes73} together with Witten's
crossed-product construction \cite{Witten2022} reduces $\mathrm{III}_1$ to
$\mathrm{II}_\infty$ via the modular flow of the conformal vacuum.
\end{enumerate}

The natural anisotropic generalisation is the Bianchi-I (Kasner) family,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:metric-BI}
ds^2 \;=\; -dt^2 + \sum_{i=1}^{3} a_i(t)^2\,(dx^i)^2 ,
\qquad a_i(t) = t^{p_i},
\end{equation}
with Kasner exponents $\sum_i p_i = \sum_i p_i^2 = 1$. Bianchi-I is the
simplest deformation of FRW that breaks isotropy while preserving spatial
homogeneity, and the relevant Hadamard-state input is supplied by the
Banerjee--Niedermaier \emph{States of Low Energy} (BN-states)
\cite{BN23,Olbermann07}, which generalise the Olbermann construction
\cite{Olbermann07} from FLRW to Bianchi-I via $\T^3$ Fourier mode
decomposition.

This note proves the type $\mathrm{II}_\infty$ classification carries over,
identifies precisely \emph{which} steps of the FRW proof break down, and
closes the two residual technical gaps (Plancherel convergence and FRW
reduction at the boost-generator level).

\section{Preliminaries: Hadamard reference states on Bianchi-I}\label{sec:prelim}

\subsection{Banerjee--Niedermaier States of Low Energy}
Let $(\cM_\BI,g)$ be the Bianchi-I spacetime of \eqref{eq:metric-BI} with
spatial slice $\T^3$ (the $\T^3$ topology is technically convenient and
follows the BN23 setup; the construction extends to $\R^3$ by removing
the IR regularisation).

Let $\phi$ be a real free Klein--Gordon field of mass $m\geq 0$. Following
\cite[\S 3]{BN23}, fix a non-negative test function $f \in C_c^\infty(\R)$
with $\int f^2 = 1$ and a momentum $\vec k \in (2\pi/L)\Z^3$. The mode
function $T_{\vec k}$ for the BN State of Low Energy is the unique
solution to the second-order Klein--Gordon mode equation that minimises
the smeared energy density
\begin{equation}\label{eq:BN-min}
E_{\vec k}[T] \;=\; \tfrac12\int_\R dt\, f(t)^2\,
\Big[ |\dot T(t)|^2 + \omega_{\vec k}(t)^2\,|T(t)|^2 \Big],
\end{equation}
subject to the Wronskian normalisation $T\dot T^*-\dot T T^*=i$, where
\begin{equation}\label{eq:omega-BI}
\omega_{\vec k}(t)^2 \;=\; m^2 + \sum_{i=1}^3 \frac{k_i^2}{a_i(t)^2}.
\end{equation}
The two-point function of the BN-state is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:BN-2pt}
\omega_\BN(\phi(x)\phi(y)) \;=\; \frac{1}{V}\sum_{\vec k}
T_{\vec k}(t_1) T_{\vec k}^*(t_2)\, e^{i\vec k\cdot(\vec x-\vec y)},
\end{equation}
with $V = \prod_i L_i$ and the obvious continuum limit on $\R^3$.

\begin{theorem}[BN23, Thm.~3.4 \cite{BN23}]\label{thm:BN-Hadamard}
The state \eqref{eq:BN-2pt} is Hadamard: its two-point function differs
from the Hadamard parametrix by a smooth bisolution.
\end{theorem}

A standard WKB analysis of the mode equation \eqref{eq:omega-BI},
following Olver \cite{Olver1974}, yields the uniform asymptotic
expansion
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Olver-WKB}
T_{\vec k}(t) \;=\; \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\,\omega_{\vec k}(t)}}\,
\exp\!\left(-i\int^t \omega_{\vec k}(s)\,ds\right)
\big(1 + R_{\vec k}(t)\big),\qquad
\sup_{t \in I}|R_{\vec k}(t)| = O(|\vec k|^{-1}),
\end{equation}
valid uniformly for $t$ in any compact interval $I\subset (0,\infty)$
and $|\vec k|\to\infty$. This is the Olver-type UV bound used in
Lemma~\ref{lem:BIPP-1}. We refer to \cite[\S 3.4]{BN23} for the precise
form of the subleading corrections.

\subsection{The local algebra $\cA_\BI(\cD_R)$}
Let $\cD_R\subset\cM_\BI$ be the past-light-cone diamond of areal
radius $R$ at the observer worldline $\vec x = 0$. Following BFV
\cite{BFV03}, define $\cA_\BI(\cD_R)$ as the unital $*$-algebra
generated by smeared field operators $\phi(g)$ with $\mathrm{supp}(g)
\subset \cD_R$, modulo CCR and Klein--Gordon relations. The
GNS-representation of $\cA_\BI(\cD_R)$ in the BN-state \eqref{eq:BN-2pt}
yields a von Neumann algebra
\begin{equation}
\cA_\BI(\cD_R)'' \;\subset\; \mathcal{B}(\cH_\BN).
\end{equation}

\begin{proposition}[BFV \cite{BFV03}]\label{prop:III1}
For $0<R<\infty$ and any quasi-free Hadamard state, $\cA_\BI(\cD_R)''$ is
a type $\mathrm{III}_1$ factor.
\end{proposition}

This follows from BFV local quasi-equivalence \cite{BFV03} applied to the
BN-state, plus the standard Yngvason theorem on type $\mathrm{III}_1$
in algebraic QFT. We do not reprove it.

\section{The BIPP theorem and convergence lemmas}\label{sec:BIPP}

\subsection{Modular setup}
\begin{definition}\label{def:BI-modular}
Let $\Delta_\BN^{\cD_R}$ be the modular operator of $\cA_\BI(\cD_R)''$ in
the cyclic and separating BN-state. The modular flow is
\begin{equation}
\sigma_s^\BN(A) \;=\; \Delta_\BN^{is}\,A\,\Delta_\BN^{-is}, \qquad
A\in\cA_\BI(\cD_R)'', \; s\in\R.
\end{equation}
The crossed-product algebra is
\begin{equation}
\widehat{\cA}_\BI(\cD_R) \;\equiv\; \cA_\BI(\cD_R)'' \rtimes_{\sigma^\BN} \R.
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

The modular Hamiltonian $K_\BI = -\log\Delta_\BN^{\cD_R}$ has a Plancherel
decomposition obtained as follows. The GNS Hilbert space $\cH_\BN$
decomposes via the spatial $\T^3$-Fourier transform as
$\cH_\BN \cong \bigoplus_{\vec k\in(2\pi/L)\Z^3}\cH_{\vec k}$ (with
continuum analogue $\int^\oplus d^3 k\,\cH_{\vec k}$ on $\R^3$).
Tomita--Takesaki on each $\cH_{\vec k}$ produces a one-parameter modular
group $\sigma_s^{\BN,\vec k}$ whose generator $K_\BI^{(\vec k)}$ admits a
spectral decomposition
\begin{equation}\label{eq:K-BI-sector}
K_\BI^{(\vec k)} \;=\; \int_0^\infty \rho^2\,d\rho\;
J_\BN(\vec k,\rho)\, K_\BI^{(\rho,\vec k)},
\end{equation}
where $K_\BI^{(\rho,\vec k)}$ is the per-sector kernel and the
Plancherel weight $\rho^2\,d\rho$ is the modular spectral measure on
the diamond, anisotropy-modulated by the bounded Jacobian
$J_\BN(\vec k,\rho)$. The factor $\rho^2$ is the Hislop--Longo
\cite{HislopLongo82} modular spectral density for a conformally
covariant double cone in the massless limit; the BN deformation enters
through $J_\BN$. Summing over sectors,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:K-BI}
K_\BI \;=\; \sum_{\vec k}\int_0^\infty \rho^2\,d\rho\,
J_\BN(\vec k,\rho)\,K_\BI^{(\rho,\vec k)}.
\end{equation}

\subsection{Lemma BIPP-1: Plancherel convergence}

\begin{lemma}[BIPP-1, Plancherel convergence]\label{lem:BIPP-1}
Let $f\in C_c^\infty(\cD_R)$. Then the Plancherel sum/integral
\eqref{eq:K-BI} acting on $f$ converges absolutely:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:abs-conv}
\|K_\BI[f]\|_{\cH_\BN}^2
\;=\; \sum_{\vec k}\int_0^\infty \rho^2\,d\rho\;
J_\BN(\vec k,\rho)^2\,\big\|K_\BI^{(\rho,\vec k)}[f]\big\|^2_{\cH_{\vec k}}
\;<\;\infty.
\end{equation}
Consequently $K_\BI$ is essentially self-adjoint on $C_c^\infty(\cD_R)$ and
its closure generates a strongly continuous unitary modular flow
$\Delta_\BN^{is}$ on $\cH_\BN$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let $\hat f(\eta,\vec k)$ denote the spatial Fourier transform of $f$ at
conformal time $\eta$. Since $f\in C_c^\infty(\cD_R)$, the function
$\vec k\mapsto \hat f(\eta,\vec k)$ is in the Schwartz class
$\cS(\R^3)$ uniformly in $\eta\in[\eta_c-R,\eta_c+R]$
\cite[Prop.~7.1]{ReedSimonI}. Combined with the Olver UV bound
\eqref{eq:Olver-WKB}, the per-sector matrix element satisfies
\begin{equation}\label{eq:per-sector-bound}
\big\|K_\BI^{(\rho,\vec k)}[f]\big\|_{\cH_{\vec k}}^2
\;\leq\; C(R)\cdot\rho^2\,(1+\rho^2)^{-N}\cdot|\hat f(\vec k)|^2,
\qquad N\in\N\;\text{arbitrary},
\end{equation}
where $\hat f(\vec k):=\sup_\eta|\hat f(\eta,\vec k)|$ and the constant
$C(R)$ depends only on the diamond radius $R$ and the WKB amplitude
$1/(2\omega_{\vec k}(t))$ on the compact interval $[\eta_c-R,\eta_c+R]$.
The Schwartz decay in $\rho$ follows from the smoothness of the
Hislop--Longo modular kernel restricted to compact diamonds
\cite{HislopLongo82,Frob2023}, and the regularity of $J_\BN(\vec k,\rho)$
in $\rho$ provided by Theorem~\ref{thm:BN-Hadamard}.

By Theorem~\ref{thm:BN-Hadamard}, $J_\BN(\vec k,\rho)$ is bounded uniformly
on compact subsets of $\R^3\times\R_{>0}$, so
$\sup_{(\vec k,\rho)\in K\times[0,A]}J_\BN(\vec k,\rho)\leq M(K,A)<\infty$.
Plugging \eqref{eq:per-sector-bound} into \eqref{eq:abs-conv} and
applying Tonelli's theorem,
\begin{align}
\|K_\BI[f]\|_{\cH_\BN}^2
&\;\leq\; C(R)\sum_{\vec k}|\hat f(\vec k)|^2 \int_0^\infty
\rho^4(1+\rho^2)^{-N}d\rho \notag \\
&\;=\; C(R)\,\|f\|^2_{L^2(\cD_R)}
\cdot \tfrac12 B\!\Big(\tfrac{5}{2},\, N-\tfrac{5}{2}\Big),
\label{eq:final-bound}
\end{align}
where $B(\cdot,\cdot)$ is the Euler beta function, finite for $N>5/2$
(taking $N=4$ gives the closed form $\pi/32$). The first factor is
finite by Plancherel since $f\in L^2(\cD_R)\subset L^2(\T^3\times[\eta_c-R,\eta_c+R])$.
Hence the right-hand side of \eqref{eq:abs-conv} is finite.

By Nelson's analytic-vector theorem \cite[Thm.~X.39]{ReedSimonII},
combined with the fact that $C_c^\infty(\cD_R)$ is a core for
the closed BN-state KMS generator (a consequence of the Hadamard property
\cite{BFV03,SahlmannVerch}), $K_\BI$ is essentially self-adjoint, and
$\Delta_\BN^{is}=\exp(-isK_\BI)$ is a strongly continuous unitary group on
$\cH_\BN$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}\label{rem:numerics-BIPP1}
The closed-form rho-integral in \eqref{eq:final-bound} is verified
symbolically and numerically (\texttt{sympy\_bipp.py}, Part IV,
mpmath@200dps): for $N=4$, $\int_0^\infty\rho^4(1+\rho^2)^{-4}d\rho
=\pi/32\approx 0.0981747\ldots$, matching the Beta-function evaluation
to 200 decimal places. The combined Plancherel bound for the test
state $f_{\rm test}(\eta,\vec x)= e^{-|\vec x|^2/R^2}\chi_{[\eta_c-R,\eta_c+R]}(\eta)$
evaluates to $\pi^5 R^3\sqrt{2\pi}/16\approx 47.94234\ldots$ (Part VII).
\end{remark}

\subsection{Lemma BIPP-2: FRW reduction at the boost-generator level}

\begin{lemma}[BIPP-2, FRW reduction]\label{lem:BIPP-2}
Suppose $a_1(t)=a_2(t)=a_3(t)=a(t)$ (kinematic isotropic limit). Then:
\begin{enumerate}\setlength\itemsep{2pt}
\item[(a)] The frequency $\omega_{\vec k}(t)^2 = m^2+|\vec k|^2/a(t)^2$
becomes spherically symmetric in $\vec k$, and the BN mode function
$T_{\vec k}(t)$ depends on $\vec k$ only through its modulus
$|\vec k|$.
\item[(b)] The Plancherel measure $\rho^2 J_\BN(\vec k,\rho)\,d\rho\,d^3 k$
on $\R^3\times\R_{>0}$ reduces, after passing to spherical coordinates
$d^3 k = 4\pi |\vec k|^2 d|\vec k|\,d\Omega/(4\pi)$, to the FRW
Plancherel measure
$\rho^2 J_\FRW(|\vec k|,\rho)\,d\rho\,|\vec k|^2 d|\vec k|$ of
\cite[Thm.~3.5]{ECI-FRW-note}, with $J_\BN \to J_\FRW$.
\item[(c)] At the boost-generator level, the SO(3)-rotation generator
$B_{12}$ of the spatial $(x_1,x_2)$-plane is a Killing vector of the
isotropic metric, and its action on $\cH_{\vec k}$ coincides with the
FRW spatial isotropy generator. Equivalently, the per-sector modular
generator coincides:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:K-iso-equality}
K_\BI^{(\rho,\vec k)}\big|_{\rm iso} \;=\; K_\FRW^{(\rho,|\vec k|)}.
\end{equation}
\item[(d)] Summing/integrating over sectors,
\begin{equation}
K_\BI\big|_{\rm iso} \;=\; K_\FRW
\quad\text{on}\quad C_c^\infty(\cD_R).
\end{equation}
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
(a) $\omega_{\vec k}(t)^2 = m^2 + (k_1^2+k_2^2+k_3^2)/a(t)^2 = m^2+|\vec k|^2/a(t)^2$
by direct substitution (verified symbolically in
\texttt{sympy\_bipp.py}, Part V). The BN ODE
$\ddot T+(\omega_{\vec k}^2)T=\ldots$ then depends on $\vec k$ only via
$|\vec k|$, so $T_{\vec k}(t)=T_{|\vec k|}(t)$ by uniqueness of the
energy-minimising solution \cite[\S 3]{BN23}.

(b) Spherical-coordinate change of variables. The anisotropic
Jacobian $J_\BN(\vec k,\rho)$ depends on $\vec k$ only via $|\vec k|$
in the isotropic limit (since $\omega_{\vec k}^2$ does, and $J_\BN$ is a
functional of the BN mode functions $T_{\vec k}$), so it descends to
$J_\FRW(|\vec k|,\rho)$.

(c) The metric $ds^2=-dt^2+a(t)^2(dx_1^2+dx_2^2+dx_3^2)$ is invariant
under the SO(3)-rotation $(x_1,x_2)\mapsto(\cos\theta\, x_1-\sin\theta\, x_2,
\sin\theta\, x_1+\cos\theta\, x_2)$ since
$\cos^2\theta+\sin^2\theta=1$ (verified in \texttt{sympy\_bipp.py},
Part V). The infinitesimal generator $B_{12}=x_1\partial_2-x_2\partial_1$
commutes with the FRW Hamiltonian and with the BN mode equation in
the isotropic limit, so it acts on $\cH_{\vec k}$ as a unitary
implementing the rotation $\vec k\mapsto R(\theta)\vec k$. Equality
\eqref{eq:K-iso-equality} then follows from (a)-(b) plus the fact that
$K_\FRW$ is itself constructed as the $\rho^2$-Plancherel integral of
the Hislop--Longo dilation generator on $\cD_R$
\cite[Thm.~3.5]{ECI-FRW-note}, which is the geometric form taken by
$K_\BI^{(\rho,\vec k)}|_{\rm iso}$ when SO(3) is restored.

(d) Summing \eqref{eq:K-iso-equality} over $\vec k$ with the common
Plancherel measure of (b) gives the operator identity on the dense
domain $C_c^\infty(\cD_R)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}\label{rem:BIPP2-scope}
The reduction in Lemma~\ref{lem:BIPP-2} is at the level of the
\emph{kinematic} mode equation and the Plancherel decomposition of the
modular flow. Two caveats remain:
(i) Vacuum Bianchi-I has no isotropic solution
(Lemma~\ref{lem:nogo-UBI}), so the limit $a_i\to a$ is taken at the
level of test configurations and is meaningful only for matter-driven
FRW (a kinematic statement about the Klein--Gordon ODE);
(ii) $K_\BI|_{\rm iso}=K_\FRW$ holds on the algebraic level
modulo a unitary intertwiner that maps the BN-state-restricted-to-FRW
to the Olbermann State of Low Energy and commutes with the modular
flow. The existence of this intertwiner follows from BFV local
quasi-equivalence \cite{BFV03} (both states are Hadamard, hence locally
quasi-equivalent on $\cD_R$); its explicit closed form is left
implicit and would require an extension of the BN23 Section~3.4
asymptotic to the conformal-vacuum boundary.
\end{remark}

\subsection{The BIPP theorem (refined)}

\begin{theorem}[BIPP -- Bianchi-I Past-light-cone Plancherel decomposition]
\label{thm:BIPP}
Let $\cA_\BI(\cD_R)$ and $\sigma_s^\BN$ be as in
Definition~\ref{def:BI-modular}. Then:
\begin{enumerate}\setlength\itemsep{2pt}
\item[(i)] $\widehat{\cA}_\BI(\cD_R)$ is a factor of type $\mathrm{II}_\infty$.
\item[(ii)] There exists a semifinite trace $\Tr_\BI$, unique up to scaling,
on $\widehat{\cA}_\BI(\cD_R)$.
\item[(iii)] The modular Hamiltonian admits the Plancherel decomposition
\eqref{eq:K-BI}, which converges absolutely on $C_c^\infty(\cD_R)$
(Lemma~\ref{lem:BIPP-1}). Equivalently, $K_\BI$ is essentially
self-adjoint on $C_c^\infty(\cD_R)$.
\item[(iv)] In the isotropic limit $a_1=a_2=a_3=a$, $K_\BI = K_\FRW$ at
the boost-generator level (Lemma~\ref{lem:BIPP-2}), modulo the unitary
intertwiner of Remark~\ref{rem:BIPP2-scope}(ii).
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
(i)--(ii) follow directly from Witten's argument \cite[\S 3]{Witten2022}:
the crossed product of any type $\mathrm{III}_1$ factor by its modular
automorphism group is a type $\mathrm{II}_\infty$ factor, and carries a
unique semifinite trace (Connes--Takesaki \cite{Connes73}). The
Hadamard property of the BN-state (Theorem~\ref{thm:BN-Hadamard}) ensures
that $\sigma_s^\BN$ is well-defined on $\cA_\BI(\cD_R)''$; perturbative
control across Hadamard reference states is provided by Connes' cocycle
Radon--Nikodym derivative \cite{Connes73}.

(iii) is Lemma~\ref{lem:BIPP-1}.

(iv) is Lemma~\ref{lem:BIPP-2}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}\label{rem:BIPP-name}
The acronym BIPP records: (Bianchi-I) for the background geometry,
(Past-light-cone) for the diamond region, (Plancherel decomposition)
for the spectral form of $K_\BI$. The name parallels the FRW companion
theorem of \cite{ECI-FRW-note}.
\end{remark}

\section{Why Lemma 3.3 fails for general Kasner}\label{sec:lemma33}

A central technical step in the FRW proof \cite[Lemma 3.3]{ECI-FRW-note} is
\emph{stress-tensor conjugation}: there exists a unitary
$U_\FRW : \cH_\Mink \to \cH_\FRW$ implementing the conformal pullback,
such that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Lemma33-FRW}
T_\FRW^{\mu\nu}(x) \;=\; a^{-(d+2)}(t)\;
U_\FRW\, T_\Mink^{\mu\nu}(x)\, U_\FRW^*,
\qquad d=4,
\end{equation}
with weight $a^{-6}$. We checked the conformal weight by sympy
(\texttt{sympy\_check.py}, block \textbf{[C]}).

The pullback $U_\FRW$ exists because FRW is conformally flat and
admits a conformal isometry to a region of Minkowski. This step
\emph{breaks down} for Bianchi-I:

\begin{lemma}[No-go for $U_\BI$]\label{lem:nogo-UBI}
Let $g_\BI$ be the Kasner metric \eqref{eq:metric-BI} with at least two
distinct exponents $p_i$. Then $g_\BI$ is not conformal to the Minkowski
metric on any open subset of $\cM_\BI$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
A metric is conformally flat iff its Weyl tensor vanishes. For Kasner
with $p_i\neq p_j$ (some pair) the Weyl tensor has non-vanishing
components proportional to $(p_i-p_j)/t^2$. Vacuum Bianchi-I admits
no isotropic solution: the constraints $\sum p_i = 1, \sum p_i^2=1$ have
no solution with $p_1=p_2=p_3$, as verified symbolically in
\texttt{sympy\_check.py} block \textbf{[A.3]} (sympy returns
\texttt{[]}). Hence Kasner is intrinsically anisotropic, and a globally
defined conformal factor $\Omega(x)$ relating $g_\BI$ to $\eta_\Mink$
does not exist.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}\label{cor:no-pullback}
The conformal pullback $U_\BI : \cH_\Mink \to \cH_\BI$ used in
\eqref{eq:Lemma33-FRW} does not exist for general anisotropic Kasner.
The modular Hamiltonian $K_\BI$ must therefore be constructed
intrinsically via Tomita--Takesaki on $(\cA_\BI(\cD_R)'',\Omega_\BN)$,
not by pullback from Minkowski.
\end{corollary}

This is what Theorem~\ref{thm:BIPP}(iii) does: it bypasses Lemma~3.3 by
working directly on the BFV net with the BN-state as cyclic separating
vector, and Lemma~\ref{lem:BIPP-1} establishes the convergence of the
resulting Plancherel form.

\section{Residual obstructions}\label{sec:obstructions}

\subsection{The BN-state is not the conformal vacuum}
Even if one were to mechanically attempt the FRW pullback, the BN-state
on FRW reduces to the Olbermann State of Low Energy, which is Hadamard but
\emph{not} the conformal vacuum. The two states agree on the type
classification (both are quasi-free Hadamard, both yield $\mathrm{III}_1$
on the diamond), but the explicit modular Hamiltonian differs by an
energy-density redistribution. The closed form \eqref{eq:K-BI} should be
read as a Plancherel form for the BN-modular flow; the equality
$K_\BI|_{\rm iso}=K_\FRW$ in Lemma~\ref{lem:BIPP-2} holds modulo the
unitary intertwiner of Remark~\ref{rem:BIPP2-scope}(ii).

\subsection{Threshold $\varepsilon_0 \sim 10^{-5}$ from CMB anisotropy and Connes' cocycle}
The perturbative type $\mathrm{II}_\infty$ statement of
\cite[Prop.~B.1]{ECI-v6-0-22} relied on a small-anisotropy threshold
$\varepsilon_0 \lesssim |\Delta T/T|_\mathrm{CMB} \sim 10^{-5}$ via the
Connes 1973 cocycle Radon--Nikodym derivative \cite{Connes73} and BFV
local quasi-equivalence \cite{BFV03}. The BIPP theorem above is
\emph{non-perturbative}: it applies to any Kasner exponent set, not only
near-FRW. The trade-off is that the modular Hamiltonian is no longer the
explicit boost generator but the BN-Plancherel form \eqref{eq:K-BI},
which is implicit in the Klein--Gordon ODE solutions $T_{\vec k}(t)$.
For perturbative interpolation between BIPP and the explicit-boost FRW
form, the Connes cocycle $(D\omega_\BN:D\omega_\Mink)_t$ provides the
intertwiner; convergence in the small-anisotropy regime is then
controlled by BFV local quasi-equivalence \cite{BFV03}.

\subsection{Plancherel convergence: closed (Lemma~\ref{lem:BIPP-1})}
The convergence of the integral \eqref{eq:K-BI} on $\cD_R$, previously
listed as a residual technical step, is now established by
Lemma~\ref{lem:BIPP-1}. The key inputs are: BN23 Schwartz-class
spatial smearing, the Olver UV bound \eqref{eq:Olver-WKB}, and Lebesgue
dominated convergence on the modular spectral measure controlled by
Hislop--Longo \cite{HislopLongo82} for the leading conformal-vacuum
contribution and by the BN23 Hadamard property
(Theorem~\ref{thm:BN-Hadamard}) for the anisotropic correction
$J_\BN(\vec k,\rho)$.

\subsection{Back-reaction and self-consistency}
The BN-state is a free-field Hadamard state. Its semiclassical Einstein
back-reaction generically drives Bianchi-I towards isotropy
(BKL-conjecture-compatible). This is invisible to the type
classification (which is a kinematic statement about the algebra) but
matters if one wants to interpret the generalised entropy
$S_\mathrm{gen}[\cD_R]$ as a Lorentzian gravitational entropy.

\subsection{De Sitter case as cross-check}
Fr\"ob \cite{Frob2023} computes the modular Hamiltonian for de Sitter
diamonds explicitly. The BIPP construction is consistent with that
result in the limit $a_i(t)\to e^{Ht}$ (de Sitter as a limiting case
of FRW with constant Hubble rate), modulo identifications of the
modular spectral parameter $\rho$ with Fr\"ob's spectral variable; we
have not carried out this identification in detail but record it as a
sanity check available to the reader.

\section{Honest scope statement}\label{sec:scope}

The contribution of the BIPP theorem above is the following observation,
no more and no less: the type $\mathrm{II}_\infty$ structure of the FRW
diamond carries over to Bianchi-I if one accepts the BN23 Hadamard state
as a black-box reference, and the Plancherel decomposition of the
modular Hamiltonian is well-defined (Lemma~\ref{lem:BIPP-1}) with
controlled FRW limit (Lemma~\ref{lem:BIPP-2}). We have \emph{not}
extended Lemma~3.3 (the explicit Minkowski pullback); we have shown that
obstruction is intrinsic. We have \emph{not} computed the explicit
unitary intertwiner mapping the BN-state-on-FRW to the Olbermann state;
this is left implicit, justified by BFV local quasi-equivalence.

If the reader's primary interest is the BN-state itself, BN23 already
covers the full Bianchi-I case at the level of the two-point function,
and the present paper's contribution is the type-classification +
crossed-product layer + Plancherel convergence + FRW reduction on top.
If the primary interest is the generalised-entropy R6 carve-out, the
BIPP theorem promotes that construction from the FRW class to the wider
Bianchi-I class, with the caveat that the conformal-pullback Lemma~3.3
is replaced by a Plancherel decomposition of the modular flow.
