The Comment identifies two technical issues in Bianconi (2025) that are independent of the framework's broader merits:

(1) Eq. (38) of the paper asserts H̃ = Tr(g̃ ln g̃⁻¹) = 0 for the spacetime metric. Direct symbolic and 200-digit numerical computation (sympy and mpmath, full code reproduced in the Comment's appendix) shows that for any Lorentzian metric with signature (-1,+1,+1,+1), this trace evaluates to ±iπ ≠ 0 by the principal-branch logarithm. The claim H̃ = 0 holds only under an implicit Wick rotation to Euclidean signature, which is neither stated nor justified in the paper. The Einstein-Hilbert limit and the emergent cosmological constant Λ both depend on this simplification.

(2) The emergent Λ is parameterized by the auxiliary G-field VEV, which is unconstrained within the framework. The paper provides no first-principles formula relating this VEV to the observed Λ_obs ≈ 10⁻¹²² (Planck units). The cosmological-constant problem is therefore relocated, not solved.

For full transparency, the Comment also explicitly states that algebraic-QFT approaches to gravity (Witten 2022, CLPW 2023, Faulkner-Speranza 2024) which the author co-references do NOT supply a Λ derivation either; both programmes leave the ~10¹²² hierarchy as an open question.

The Comment is 4 pages in revtex4-2 PRD style, includes a sympy/mpmath verification appendix, and 12 arXiv-API-verified references. The tone is technical and respectful: the deficiencies are framed as requests for clarification rather than refutations of the broader programme.
