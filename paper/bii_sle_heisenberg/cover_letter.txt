Dear Editor,

This paper extends Banerjee-Niedermaier 2023 (J. Math. Phys. 64 (2023) 113503,
arXiv:2305.11388) to the Bianchi type II cosmology on Heisenberg-group nilmanifold
spatial slices via the Stone-von Neumann Plancherel decomposition.

**Result.** The SLE state on Bianchi II x Nil^3 is Hadamard (Radzikowski 1996)
and locally covariant (Brunetti-Fredenhagen-Verch 2003). The proof uses three
ingredients:

  (1) [B4] A two-index uniform Bogoliubov bound
        |beta_{n,omega}(f)|^2 = O((omega^2 + n)^{-N})  for all N,
      with the joint UV parameter Lambda_eff = omega^2 + n. (Lemma B-G1.)

  (2) [G5/G1.4 = present update] The global pseudodifferential calculus on
      graded nilpotent Lie groups developed by Fischer-Ruzhansky 2016
      (Quantization on Nilpotent Lie Groups, Progress in Mathematics 314,
      Birkhauser, DOI 10.1007/978-3-319-29558-9, OPEN ACCESS CC BY 4.0).
      Specifically: symbol classes S^m_{rho,delta}(H_3 x \hat{H_3}) [FR §5.2.2],
      composition formula [FR Thm 5.5.12], parametrices for elliptic symbols
      [FR Thm 5.8.7], explicit Heisenberg-group calculus [FR Ch.6], plus
      Sobolev embedding H^s(H_3) into L^infty for s > Q/2 = 2 [FR Thm 4.4.25].
      Combined with Beals-Greiner 1988 and Ponge 2008 for the Heisenberg-
      manifold (contact-manifold) extension, this gives the local equivalence
      between the FR pseudodifferential wavefront set and the classical
      Hormander wavefront set on M = R x Nil^3 (treated as a smooth 4-manifold).

  (3) Hormander Vol III pseudodifferential calculus on the smooth 4-manifold
      M (Grundlehren 274, DOI 10.1007/978-3-540-49938-1) for the temporal
      direction and the global wavefront-set glueing.

**Critical correction (B4, retained from prior cover letter).** A previous
prompt suggested the single-epsilon scaling omega -> Omega/eps, n -> N/eps^2
to merge the two UV directions. Sympy refutes this: the cross-term
(2n+1)|omega|/(a_1 a_2) scales O(eps^{-3}) while omega^2/a_3^2 scales
O(eps^{-2}). The CORRECT joint UV parameter is Lambda_eff = omega^2 + n;
under the anisotropic dilation (omega, n) -> (R*omega, R^2*n) consistent
with Heisenberg's homogeneous degree (1,1,2) and Q = 4, Lambda_eff is
homogeneous of degree 2 (verified in sympy_FR_calculus.py Part 4).
