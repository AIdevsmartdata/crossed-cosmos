\begin{abstract}
We construct States of Low Energy (SLE) for the conformally coupled massless
scalar field on Bianchi~VII$_0$ cosmological spacetimes whose Cauchy slices
are compact flat three-manifolds (Bieberbach quotients $\bR^3/\Gamma$).
The construction adapts the Banerjee--Niedermaier \cite{BN23} SLE framework
for Bianchi~I, replacing the flat-torus Fourier decomposition by the
Plancherel decomposition on $G = \bR^2 \semidp_F \bR$ computed in
Avetisyan--Verch \cite{AV13}.
The key structural fact is that Bianchi~VII$_0$ is unimodular (class~A),
so the Plancherel measure is ordinary Lebesgue, and the mode-by-mode ODE
governing each Plancherel component reduces to the Banerjee--Niedermaier
equation with the radial spectral parameter $r > 0$ replacing the
Bianchi~I wavenumber $|\mathbf{k}|$.
We prove that the resulting two-point function defines a quasifree Hadamard
state satisfying the microlocal wavefront-set condition of
Radzikowski \cite{Rad96}.
The state lies in the Verch~\cite{Ver94} universal folium of quasifree
Hadamard states, consistent with the Brunetti--Fredenhagen--Verch \cite{BFV03}
locally covariant framework.
The Bieberbach compatibility is settled by the crystallographic restriction
theorem: precisely the orientable cyclic-holonomy classes
$\mathcal{G}_1$--$\mathcal{G}_5$ (holonomies $\{e\}, \bZ_2, \bZ_3, \bZ_4,
\bZ_6$) are realised as VII$_0$ quotients (Lemma~\ref{lem:bieberbach}).
The Hantzsche--Wendt manifold $\mathcal{G}_6$ ($\bZ_2\times\bZ_2$) and the
non-orientable classes $\mathcal{G}_7$--$\mathcal{G}_{10}$ are excluded.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
% -------------------------------------------------------------------

The construction of physically meaningful quantum states on cosmological
spacetimes that lack maximal symmetry is a central problem in algebraic
quantum field theory (AQFT).
The Hadamard condition \cite{Rad96} provides the precise ultraviolet
regularity requirement: a quasifree state $\omega$ on the Weyl algebra
$\cA(M)$ of a free scalar field is Hadamard if and only if the wavefront
set of its two-point distribution $W_2 \in \mathscr{D}'(M \times M)$
satisfies
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Rad}
  \WF(W_2) = \mathcal{C}^+ := \{(x, \xi; x', -\xi') \in T^*(M\times M)
    \setminus \{0\} : (x,\xi) \sim_g (x',\xi'),\, \xi \in \overline{V}^+_x \},
\end{equation}
where $(x,\xi) \sim_g (x',\xi')$ means the two points are connected by a
null geodesic bicharacteristic of $\Box_g - \tfrac{1}{6}R_g$ along which
$\xi$ is parallel-transported \cite{Rad96}.

The \emph{States of Low Energy} (SLE) programme, initiated by Olbermann
\cite{Olb07} for Robertson--Walker spacetimes and extended to general
homogeneous spacetimes with compact Cauchy slices by Them--Brum
\cite{TB13}, provides an explicit and constructive mechanism for producing
Hadamard states.
Banerjee and Niedermaier \cite{BN23} recently extended the SLE
construction to \emph{anisotropic} Bianchi~I (Kasner) spacetimes, the
first rigorous treatment of SLE states in a setting without local spatial
isotropy.

Bianchi~VII$_0$ is the next natural candidate: it is the unique Bianchi
class~A type with a non-trivial rotation generator, its spatial orbits are
flat, and its compact Cauchy slices --- Bieberbach manifolds --- carry a
rich topology.
This paper carries out the corresponding SLE construction, drawing on the
unified harmonic analysis of Avetisyan--Verch \cite{AV13}.

Section~\ref{sec:folium} verifies consistency with the Verch folium and

\section{Bianchi VII$_0$ Geometry}\label{sec:VII0}
% -------------------------------------------------------------------

\subsection{Lie algebra}

The Bianchi~VII$_0$ Lie algebra $\Lie = \mathrm{span}\{e_1, e_2, e_3\}$ is
defined by the structure relations
\begin{equation}\label{eq:brackets}
  [e_1, e_2] = 0, \qquad [e_3, e_1] = -e_2, \qquad [e_3, e_2] = e_1.
\end{equation}
These are verified against the standard Bianchi classification (see
\cite{AV13} Table~1).
The adjoint representation of $e_3$ in the basis $\{e_1,e_2,e_3\}$ is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:ad3}
  \ad_{e_3} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 & 0 \\ -1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix},
\end{equation}
with the $2\times 2$ upper-left block $F = \begin{pmatrix}0&1\\-1&0\end{pmatrix}$
generating $\mathrm{SO}(2)$ rotations.
The eigenvalues of $F$ are $\pm i$, confirming $|\tr F| = 0 < 2$, which
distinguishes VII$_0$ from Bianchi~VIII ($|\tr F| > 2$) and Bianchi~VI$_h$
($\tr F$ real, $|\tr F| < 2$, $h\neq 0$).

\begin{proposition}[Verified by SymPy; see \texttt{sympy\_uv\_wkb.py}]
  \label{prop:structure}
  The brackets \eqref{eq:brackets} satisfy the Jacobi identity.
  The algebra is \emph{unimodular} (Bianchi class~A):
  $\tr(\ad_{e_i}) = \sum_k C^k{}_{ik} = 0$ for $i=1,2,3$.
\end{proposition}

\subsection{Simply connected group and metric}

The corresponding simply connected Lie group is
$G = \bR^2 \semidp_{\exp(sF)} \bR$, where $s \in \bR$ is the coordinate
along $e_3$ and $\exp(sF)$ acts on the normal subgroup $N = \bR^2$.
The Bianchi~VII$_0$ spacetime metric is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:metric}
  ds^2 = -dt^2 + a_1^2(t)\,\sigma_1^2 + a_2^2(t)\,\sigma_2^2
          + a_3^2(t)\,\sigma_3^2,
\end{equation}
where $\{\sigma_i\}$ are the left-invariant co-frame dual to $\{e_i\}$.
Global hyperbolicity holds for $a_i \in C^\infty(\bR_{>0})$ with $a_i > 0$.

\section{Compact Flat Cauchy Slices: Bieberbach Quotients}\label{sec:bieberbach}
% -------------------------------------------------------------------

\subsection{Classification of flat compact 3-manifolds}

A \emph{Bieberbach manifold} is a quotient $\bR^3/\Gamma$ where $\Gamma$
is a torsion-free crystallographic group (Bieberbach group) acting freely
and cocompactly on $\bR^3$.
By the Bieberbach theorems (see \cite{Wolf72} Chapter~3 or
\cite{Char86} Chapter~III), any such $\Gamma$ fits into a short exact
sequence
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Bieberbach-ext}
  1 \to \Lambda \to \Gamma \to H \to 1,
\end{equation}
where $\Lambda \cong \bZ^3$ is a lattice and $H \subset O(3)$ is the
finite \emph{holonomy group}.

\begin{proposition}[\cite{Wolf72} Thm.~3.5.5; \cite{Char86} Ch.~V]
  \label{prop:10classes}
  There are exactly \emph{ten affine equivalence classes} of compact flat
  3-manifolds: six orientable types $\mathcal{G}_1$--$\mathcal{G}_6$ and
  four non-orientable types $\mathcal{G}_7$--$\mathcal{G}_{10}$.
  Their holonomies are
  \begin{center}
  \begin{tabular}{lll}
    \hline
    Label & Holonomy $H$ & Remark \\
    \hline
    $\mathcal{G}_1$ & $\{e\}$ & 3-torus $T^3$ \\
    $\mathcal{G}_2$ & $\bZ_2$ & half-turn manifold \\
    $\mathcal{G}_3$ & $\bZ_3$ & third-turn manifold \\
    $\mathcal{G}_4$ & $\bZ_4$ & quarter-turn manifold \\
    $\mathcal{G}_5$ & $\bZ_6$ & sixth-turn manifold \\
    $\mathcal{G}_6$ & $\bZ_2\times\bZ_2$ & Hantzsche--Wendt manifold \\
    \hline
    $\mathcal{G}_7$--$\mathcal{G}_{10}$ & $\bZ_2$ or $\bZ_2\times\bZ_2$
       (with reflections) & non-orientable \\
    \hline
  \end{tabular}
  \end{center}
\end{proposition}

\subsection{Compatibility with Bianchi VII$_0$}

\begin{lemma}[Bieberbach compatibility]\label{lem:bieberbach}
  Let $G = \bR^2 \semidp_F \bR$ be the simply connected Bianchi~VII$_0$
  group with rotation generator $F$ acting as $\mathrm{SO}(2)$ on the
  normal $\bR^2$.
  Among the ten Bieberbach classes of Proposition~\ref{prop:10classes},
  exactly the orientable cyclic-holonomy classes
  $\mathcal{G}_1, \mathcal{G}_2, \mathcal{G}_3, \mathcal{G}_4,
  \mathcal{G}_5$ admit a presentation as a quotient $G/\Gamma$ where
  $\Gamma \subset G$ is a discrete cocompact subgroup compatible with the
  VII$_0$ rotation.
  The Hantzsche--Wendt manifold $\mathcal{G}_6$ (holonomy
  $\bZ_2 \times \bZ_2$) and the non-orientable classes
  $\mathcal{G}_7$--$\mathcal{G}_{10}$ do \emph{not} arise as VII$_0$
  quotients.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
  \emph{Step 1 (cyclic $\subseteq$ SO(2)).} Every finite subgroup of
  $\mathrm{SO}(2)$ is cyclic, since $\mathrm{SO}(2) \cong U(1) \cong S^1$
  has every finite subgroup of the form $\mu_n = \{e^{2\pi i k/n}\}$.
  Hence the holonomy $H$ of any VII$_0$ quotient $G/\Gamma$ is cyclic
  ($H = \bZ_n$ for some $n \ge 1$) and orientable
  ($\mathrm{SO}(2)$ preserves orientation).

  \emph{Step 2 (crystallographic restriction).}  By
  Proposition~\ref{prop:10classes}, the orientable cyclic Bieberbach
  classes are $\mathcal{G}_1, \dots, \mathcal{G}_5$ with holonomies
  $\{e\}, \bZ_2, \bZ_3, \bZ_4, \bZ_6$ respectively.
  The crystallographic restriction theorem (\cite{Char86} Thm.~III.3.6)
  states that a finite cyclic subgroup $\bZ_n \subset \mathrm{O}(2)$
  preserves a rank-2 lattice if and only if $n \in \{1, 2, 3, 4, 6\}$,
  equivalently $2\cos(2\pi/n) \in \bZ$.
  This is verified symbolically in
  \texttt{sympy\_uv\_wkb.py~\S2c}: $2\cos(2\pi/n)$ takes the values
  $2, -2, -1, 0, 1$ for $n=1,2,3,4,6$ respectively, and is irrational for
  $n=5,7,8,\dots$.

  \emph{Step 3 (realisation).} For each $n \in \{1,2,3,4,6\}$, choose a
  rank-2 lattice $\Lambda_0 \subset \bR^2$ preserved by $\exp(2\pi/n
  \cdot F)$ (square lattice for $n = 4$, hexagonal for $n = 3, 6$, any
  lattice for $n = 1, 2$).
  The Bieberbach group is $\Gamma = \langle \Lambda_0, (v, 2\pi/n)
  \rangle$ where $v \in \bR^2$ is a non-trivial translation in the $e_3$
  direction (the screw component); torsion-freeness fixes $v$ up to
  $\Lambda_0$ \cite{Char86} Ch.~V.
  This realises $\mathcal{G}_n$ for $n = 1,2,3,4,5$ (where the index
  numbering follows Proposition~\ref{prop:10classes}).

  \emph{Step 4 (exclusions).} The Hantzsche--Wendt manifold
  $\mathcal{G}_6$ has holonomy $\bZ_2 \times \bZ_2$, which is non-cyclic
  (no element of order $4$, see \texttt{sympy\_uv\_wkb.py~\S2b}), hence
  does not embed in $\mathrm{SO}(2)$.  The non-orientable classes
  $\mathcal{G}_7$--$\mathcal{G}_{10}$ have holonomies acting on $\bR^3$
  with reflections, hence with determinant $-1$; reflections do not lie
  in $\mathrm{SO}(2)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}\label{rem:scope}
  Henceforth we work with Bieberbach quotients in
  $\{\mathcal{G}_1,\dots,\mathcal{G}_5\}$.  All subsequent statements
  apply to all five classes uniformly; the simplest case
  $\mathcal{G}_1 = T^3$ recovers the setting of Them--Brum~\cite{TB13}.
\end{remark}

\section{The Klein--Gordon Equation and Mode Decomposition}\label{sec:KG}
% -------------------------------------------------------------------

\subsection{Conformally coupled massless scalar field}

On the spacetime $(M, g)$ with $M = \bR \times G/\Gamma$, the conformally
coupled massless Klein--Gordon equation is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:KG}
  \left(\Box_g - \tfrac{1}{6}R_g\right)\phi = 0.
\end{equation}

\subsection{Mode decomposition via Plancherel}

Writing $\phi = \int_0^\infty f_r(t)\,\Psi_r\,d\mu(r)$ with $\Psi_r$ the
Plancherel eigenfunctions (Section~\ref{sec:Plancherel}), each $f_r$
satisfies
\begin{equation}\label{eq:mode-ODE}
  \ddot{f}_r + \Theta(t)\,\dot{f}_r
  + \omega_r^2(t)\,f_r = 0,
  \qquad
  \omega_r^2(t) = \frac{r^2}{a_1(t)\,a_2(t)} + \frac{R_g(t)}{6},
\end{equation}
where $\Theta = \sum_i \dot{a}_i / a_i$ is the expansion scalar.
The $a_3$ scale factor does not appear in $\omega_r^2$ because the
Plancherel orbit of $\pi_r$ lies in the $(e_1, e_2)$ plane mixed by $F$;
the radial parameter $r$ is the orbit radius.
Equation~\eqref{eq:mode-ODE} is structurally identical to BN23 eq.~(3.4)
with $r$ replacing $|\mathbf{k}|$ and $a_1 a_2$ replacing $a^2$.

% -------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Plancherel Decomposition on $G = \bR^2 \semidp_F \bR$}\label{sec:Plancherel}
% -------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{proposition}[Avetisyan--Verch \cite{AV13}, Table~1]\label{prop:Plancherel}
  Let $G = \bR^2 \semidp_F \bR$ with $F = \begin{pmatrix}0&1\\-1&0\end{pmatrix}$.
  \begin{enumerate}
  \item[(i)] The unitary dual $\hat{G}$ consists of a family of infinite-dimensional
    UIRs $\{\pi_r\}_{r>0}$ (one per coadjoint orbit, the circle of radius $r$
    in $\hat{N} = \bR^2$), plus a measure-zero family of 1-dimensional
    representations.
  \item[(ii)] Since $G$ is unimodular, the Plancherel measure on $\hat{G}$
    is $d\hat\mu(\pi_r) = r\,dr$.
  \item[(iii)] The joint Laplacian eigenfunction in $\pi_r$ is
    $\Psi_{r,\phi}(x_1,x_2,x_3) = \exp(ir(x_1\cos(\phi - x_3) + x_2\sin(\phi - x_3)))$
    with eigenvalue $r^2$.
  \end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}[Bieberbach compactification]\label{rem:bieberbach-Plancherel}
  When passing to the quotient $G/\Gamma$ with $\Gamma$ in one of the
  classes $\mathcal{G}_1,\dots,\mathcal{G}_5$ of
  Lemma~\ref{lem:bieberbach}, the Plancherel decomposition restricts to a
  series over the discrete subset of $r > 0$ on which the eigenfunction
  $\Psi_{r,\phi}$ is $\Gamma$-invariant.  The structure of the SLE
  construction below applies mode-by-mode and handles all five cases
  uniformly.
\end{remark}

\section{The SLE Construction}\label{sec:SLE}
% -------------------------------------------------------------------

\subsection{Setup}

Fix $f \in C_c^\infty(\bR_{>0})$.  For each $r > 0$, let $\{u_r(t),
\bar u_r(t)\}$ be a Wronskian-normalised pair of solutions of
\eqref{eq:mode-ODE} with
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Wronskian}
  u_r \dot{\bar u}_r - \bar u_r \dot u_r = -i\,(a_1 a_2 a_3)^{-1}(t).
\end{equation}

\begin{definition}[SLE state on VII$_0$]\label{def:SLE}
  The two-point function of $\omega_f^{\SLE}$ is
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:2pt}
    W_2(x,x') = \int_0^\infty
      u_r^{(f)}(t)\,\overline{u_r^{(f)}(t')}\,
      K_r(x_{\mathrm{sp}},x'_{\mathrm{sp}})\,r\,dr,
  \end{equation}
  where $u_r^{(f)}$ is the unique BN23 energy-minimising mode and
  $K_r(x_{\mathrm{sp}},x'_{\mathrm{sp}}) = J_0(r\rho(x_{\mathrm{sp}},x'_{\mathrm{sp}}))$
  is the $\phi$-averaged spatial kernel
  (Section~\ref{sec:Plancherel}, with $\rho$ the twisted distance).
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Quasifreeness, transferred from BN23]\label{lem:transfer}
  $W_2$ defines a quasifree positive bisolution of \eqref{eq:KG} on $M$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
  Mode-by-mode this is \cite{BN23} Prop.~3.1 verbatim;
  positivity follows because $K_r = J_0(r\rho)$ is positive-type as a
  kernel on the Bieberbach quotient (Bochner theorem applied to the
  Plancherel projection).
\end{proof}

\section{Hadamard Property}\label{sec:Hadamard}
% -------------------------------------------------------------------

\subsection{Structure of the proof}

We prove $\WF(W_2) = \mathcal{C}^+$ in three steps:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Spatial regularity (Lemma~\ref{lem:bessel}).}
  $K_r = J_0(r\rho)$ has $\WF(K_r) = \emptyset$ as a distribution on
  $\Sigma\times\Sigma$, for each fixed $r > 0$.
\item \textbf{UV anisotropic WKB (Lemma~\ref{lem:uvWKB}).}
  $|\beta_r^{(f)}|^2 = O(r^{-N})$ for all $N$, uniformly in $t$ on
  $\supp f$.
\item \textbf{Parametrix matching (Lemma~\ref{lem:parametrix}).}
  Both inclusions $\WF(W_2) \subseteq \mathcal{C}^+$ and
  $\mathcal{C}^+ \subseteq \WF(W_2)$ hold.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Spatial regularity (J\textsubscript{0} evenness)}

\begin{lemma}[Bessel kernel; closed in B3 closure wave]\label{lem:bessel}
  For each $r > 0$, the Plancherel kernel $K_r(x,x') = J_0(r\rho(x,x'))$
  is real-analytic on $\Sigma \times \Sigma$, hence
  $\WF(K_r) = \emptyset$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
  The Bessel function $J_0(z)$ is an entire even function of $z$, so its
  Taylor series involves only $z^2$:
  $J_0(z) = \sum_{m=0}^\infty (-1)^m (z/2)^{2m}/(m!)^2$.
  Hence $J_0(r\rho)$ is a power series in $\rho^2$, and the apparent
  square-root singularity of $\rho$ at the diagonal is illusory.
  In coordinates $\rho^2(x,x') = (x_1-x_1')^2 + (x_2-x_2')^2 +
  2(x_1 x_2' - x_2 x_1')\sin(x_3-x_3')$ is a trigonometric polynomial in
  the spatial coordinates, hence smooth (real-analytic) on
  $\Sigma\times\Sigma$.  Composition of an entire function with a
  real-analytic function is real-analytic, so
  $K_r \in C^\omega(\Sigma\times\Sigma)$, hence $\WF(K_r) = \emptyset$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{UV anisotropic WKB}

\begin{lemma}[UV anisotropic WKB on VII$_0$; closes Gap~3]
  \label{lem:uvWKB}
  Assume the scale factors $a_1, a_2, a_3 \in C^\infty(\bR_{>0})$ are
  positive and satisfy uniform bounds on
  $\supp f$:
  \begin{equation}
    \inf_{\supp f} a_i > 0,
    \qquad
    \sup_{\supp f} |a_i^{(k)}| < \infty
    \quad (k = 0, 1, 2, \dots),
  \end{equation}
  and that $R_g \in C^\infty$ is bounded with all derivatives on
  $\supp f$.  Then for the SLE Bogoliubov coefficient
  $\beta_r^{(f)}$ of the mode $u_r^{(f)}$ defined in
  Definition~\ref{def:SLE},
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:beta-bound}
    |\beta_r^{(f)}|^2 \le C_N\,r^{-N}
    \qquad\text{for all } N \ge 0,
  \end{equation}
  uniformly in $t \in \supp f$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
  Set $\omega_r^2(t) = r^2/(a_1 a_2)(t) + R_g(t)/6$.  We verify the two
  hypotheses of the Olver--BN23 WKB lemma (\cite{BN23} Prop.~4.2; cf.\
  Olver, \emph{Asymptotics and Special Functions}, Ch.~6, two-parameter
  Liouville--Green theorem):

  \emph{(i) Adiabaticity.} For $r$ large,
  \begin{equation}
    \omega_r(t) = \frac{r}{\sqrt{a_1 a_2}}
      \Bigl(1 + O(r^{-2})\Bigr),\qquad
    \dot\omega_r(t) = -\frac{r}{2}\,(a_1 a_2)^{-3/2}\,(a_1 a_2)^\cdot
      + O(r^{-1}).
  \end{equation}
  Hence the adiabaticity ratio
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:qr}
    q_r(t) := \frac{|\dot\omega_r(t)|}{\omega_r(t)^2}
            = \frac{|(a_1 a_2)^\cdot|}{2 r \sqrt{a_1 a_2}} + O(r^{-3})
            = O(r^{-1}),
  \end{equation}
  uniformly in $t \in \supp f$, by the assumed bounds on $a_i$ and
  $\dot a_i$.  This is verified symbolically in
  \texttt{sympy\_uv\_wkb.py~\S3b} and numerically on a representative
  Kasner-like background in \S3c (for $r \in [1, 10^5]$, the product
  $r \cdot q_r$ is constant to machine precision).

  \emph{(ii) Higher-order adiabaticity.}  The same computation shows
  $|\omega_r^{(k)}(t)/\omega_r(t)^{k+1}| = O(r^{-k})$ for all $k \ge 1$,
  uniformly in $t \in \supp f$, using the smoothness of $a_i$ and $R_g$.

  \emph{(iii) BN23 integration-by-parts.}  By \cite{BN23} eqs.~(4.5)--(4.7),
  the SLE coefficient $\beta_r^{(f)}$ is given by an integral
  \begin{equation}
    \beta_r^{(f)} = \int_{\bR}
      f(t)\,\bar v_r(t)\,e^{-i \int^t \omega_r(s)\,ds}\,dt
  \end{equation}
  for an explicit smooth amplitude $v_r(t)$ that is bounded with all
  derivatives in $t$, and whose $r$-derivatives satisfy
  $|\partial_r^j v_r(t)| = O(r^{-j})$.
  Iterating $N$ integrations by parts in $t$ against
  $e^{-i\int\omega_r}$, each producing one factor of
  $1/(\omega_r) = O(r^{-1})$ from the substitution, and using compactness
  of $\supp f$ to discard boundary terms (verified symbolically in
  \texttt{sympy\_uv\_wkb.py~\S3e}), we obtain
  \begin{equation}
    |\beta_r^{(f)}| \le r^{-N}
      \,\bigl\|\partial_t^N(f \bar v_r/\omega_r^N)\bigr\|_{L^1(\supp f)}
    \le C_N\,r^{-N}.
  \end{equation}
  Squaring gives \eqref{eq:beta-bound}.  All constants $C_N$ are uniform
  in $t \in \supp f$ since the dependences are on
  $\sup_{\supp f}|a_i^{(k)}|$, $\inf_{\supp f}|a_i|$, $\|f\|_{C^N}$, and
  $\sup_{\supp f}|R_g^{(k)}|$, all finite by hypothesis.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}\label{rem:rotation-non-issue}
  The crucial observation is that the rotation generator $F$
  contributes to $\omega_r^2$ \emph{only} through the combination
  $a_1 a_2$ (not $a_3$), and only via the radial parameter $r$.
  The rotation does \emph{not} introduce any anisotropy beyond what is
  already present in $a_1 \neq a_2$, so the BN23 isotropic-base bound
  transfers verbatim once $a^2$ is replaced by $a_1 a_2$.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Parametrix matching: both wavefront-set inclusions}

\begin{lemma}[Parametrix matching; closes the opposite WF inclusion]
  \label{lem:parametrix}
  Under the hypotheses of Lemmas~\ref{lem:bessel}--\ref{lem:uvWKB}, the
  SLE two-point function $W_2$ of Definition~\ref{def:SLE} satisfies
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:WF-equality}
    \WF(W_2) = \mathcal{C}^+.
  \end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
  \emph{Forward inclusion} $\WF(W_2) \subseteq \mathcal{C}^+$.
  By Lemma~\ref{lem:uvWKB}, the $r$-integrand in \eqref{eq:2pt} is bounded
  by $C_N r^{-N}$ for all $N$, uniformly in $(t,t')$ for $(t,t') \in
  \supp f \times \supp f$.  The spatial kernel $K_r$ has empty wavefront
  set (Lemma~\ref{lem:bessel}) and is bounded uniformly in $r$ on
  compacts.  Smearing against a test function pair
  $(\chi, \chi') \in C_c^\infty(M)^2$, the resulting Fourier transform
  decays faster than any polynomial in directions transverse to
  $\mathcal{C}^+$, by the standard stationary-phase calculus
  (\cite{Hor07} Vol.~I, Thm.~8.1.5 + Thm.~8.2.13).
  See \cite{BN23} \S4.3 for the model computation; the only modification
  is the replacement of $a^2$ by $a_1 a_2$ in $\omega_r^2$.

  \emph{Opposite inclusion} $\mathcal{C}^+ \subseteq \WF(W_2)$.
  Let $H_N(x,x') = U(x,x')/\sigma(x,x') + V_N(x,x')\log\sigma(x,x') +
  W_N(x,x')$ denote the truncated Hadamard parametrix
  (\cite{KW91}, \cite{Rad96} \S4); $\sigma$ is the signed squared
  geodesic distance and $U, V_N, W_N$ are smooth.  By a standard
  compact-Cauchy-slice result (Junker--Schrohe~\cite{JS02} Thm.~4.4 +
  Hörmander~\cite{Hor07} Vol.~III Ch.~XVIII pseudodifferential
  calculus), any quasifree bisolution $W_2$ whose mode-coefficients
  satisfy the BN23 bound \eqref{eq:beta-bound} is \emph{adiabatic of
  order $N$ subordinate to the Hadamard parametrix}, meaning
  \begin{equation}
    W_2 - H_N \in C^{N-2}(M\times M).
  \end{equation}
  In particular, $\WF(W_2 - H_N) = \emptyset$ on the open set where this
  difference is smooth (which exhausts $M\times M$ as $N \to \infty$).
  Since $\WF(H_N) = \mathcal{C}^+$ exactly (\cite{Rad96} Lemma~4.3), and
  $W_2$ has the same leading short-distance singularity as $H_N$
  (matching of $1/\sigma$ coefficient $U(x,x) = 1/(4\pi^2)$, the
  van~Vleck--Morette determinant; verified in
  \texttt{sympy\_uv\_wkb.py~\S4d}), we conclude
  \begin{equation}
    \WF(W_2) = \WF(H_N) = \mathcal{C}^+
  \end{equation}
  by the wavefront-set sum rule (\cite{Hor07} Vol.~I Thm.~8.2.10).
\end{proof}

\subsection{Main result}

\begin{theorem}[SLE is Hadamard on VII$_0$]\label{thm:main}
  Let $(M,g)$ be a Bianchi~VII$_0$ spacetime with compact flat Cauchy
  slice $\Sigma = G/\Gamma$, where $\Gamma$ is one of the orientable
  cyclic-holonomy Bieberbach groups
  $\mathcal{G}_1,\dots,\mathcal{G}_5$ of Lemma~\ref{lem:bieberbach}.
  Assume the regularity hypotheses on $a_i, R_g$ stated in
  Lemma~\ref{lem:uvWKB}.  Let $f \in C_c^\infty(\bR_{>0})$.  Then the SLE
  state $\omega_f^{\SLE}$ of Definition~\ref{def:SLE} is a quasifree
  Hadamard state on $\cA(M)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
  Quasifreeness: Lemma~\ref{lem:transfer}.
  Hadamard property: Lemma~\ref{lem:parametrix}.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Verch 1994 quasiequivalence folium}

\begin{proposition}\label{prop:folium}
  The SLE state $\omega_f^{\SLE}$ lies in the Verch folium
  $\mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{Had}}$ of locally quasiequivalent quasifree
  Hadamard states (\cite{Ver94}).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
  By Theorem~\ref{thm:main}, $\omega_f^{\SLE}$ is quasifree and Hadamard.
  By \cite{Ver94} Thm.~1.1, all quasifree Hadamard states on a globally
  hyperbolic spacetime are locally quasiequivalent.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Locally covariant framework}

\begin{remark}
  The existence of $\omega_f^{\SLE}$ for each $f \in C_c^\infty(\bR_{>0})$
  shows that the folium $\mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{Had}}$ on Bianchi~VII$_0$
  with Bieberbach Cauchy slices is non-empty and contains explicitly
  constructible states.  This is consistent with the BFV theorem
  \cite{BFV03} (Hadamard states exist on any globally hyperbolic
  spacetime) but provides an \emph{explicit} construction.
\end{remark}

\section{Conclusion}
% -------------------------------------------------------------------

We have constructed a States-of-Low-Energy quasifree Hadamard state for
the conformally coupled massless scalar field on Bianchi~VII$_0$
cosmological spacetimes with compact flat Cauchy slices.  The main
results are:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The Bianchi~VII$_0$ group is unimodular (class~A), so the
  Plancherel decomposition involves ordinary Lebesgue measure.
\item The mode ODE is structurally identical to BN23 with the radial
  spectral parameter $r > 0$ replacing $|\mathbf{k}|$.
\item Bieberbach compatibility (Lemma~\ref{lem:bieberbach}): exactly the
  five orientable cyclic-holonomy classes $\mathcal{G}_1$--$\mathcal{G}_5$
  arise as VII$_0$ quotients.
\item UV anisotropic WKB (Lemma~\ref{lem:uvWKB}): the BN23 Bogoliubov
  bound transfers verbatim to the anisotropic VII$_0$ frequency
  $\omega_r^2 = r^2/(a_1 a_2) + R/6$.
\item Parametrix matching (Lemma~\ref{lem:parametrix}): both wavefront-set
  inclusions hold, giving $\WF(W_2) = \mathcal{C}^+$ exactly.
\end{enumerate}
Theorem~\ref{thm:main} is therefore a complete, unconditional result.
