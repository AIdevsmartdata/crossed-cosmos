Dear Editor,

I would like to submit the attached short structural note (5 pages) to Letters in Mathematical Physics.

**Subject and contribution.** The note proves that the type II_∞ von Neumann factor obtained from the (FRW past-light-cone diamond, conformally-coupled massless scalar, Hadamard quasifree state) AQFT setup — established as Theorems 3.5/3.6 of a companion paper [paper/frw_typeII_note/frw_note.tex of the crossed-cosmos repository, arXiv ID forthcoming] — is **hyperfinite, i.e. isomorphic to R_{0,1}**, the unique up-to-isomorphism approximately-finite-dimensional type II_∞ factor.

The proof is a 5-lemma assembly of well-known results in algebraic quantum field theory:

1. The local algebra A_FRW(D) is type III_1 in the Hadamard quasifree representation (Brunetti-Fredenhagen-Verch arXiv:math-ph/0112041 + Hislop-Longo CMP 84:71 (1982) + conformal pullback of Frob arXiv:2308.14797).

2. A_FRW(D) is approximately finite-dimensional (AFD), via Buchholz-D'Antoni-Fredenhagen 1987 CMP 111:123 transferred along the conformal pullback unitary, with the curved-spacetime path D'Antoni-Hollands arXiv:math-ph/0106028 + Fewster split property arXiv:1501.02682 as backup.

3. Connes 1976 (Ann. Math. 104:73) classifies AFD type-III factors: the hyperfinite type-III_1 is unique up to isomorphism.

4. Connes-Takesaki 1977 Tôhoku Math. J. 29:473-575 then implies the modular crossed product of an AFD type-III_1 by R is the unique AFD type II_∞ factor R_{0,1} (provided separability and integrability, both satisfied in our case).

5. The companion FRW Theorem 3.5/3.6 type II_∞ factor satisfies these hypotheses, completing the assembly.
