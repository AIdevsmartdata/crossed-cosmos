\begin{abstract}
We extend the conformal-pullback type II$_\infty$ classification of
\cite{Remondiere2026} (Theorems~3.5/3.6, established for spatially flat
$k=0$ FRW with a comoving doubly-bounded causal diamond and the conformally
coupled massless scalar) to spatially curved Friedmann--Robertson--Walker
backgrounds. The conformal target is no longer Minkowski but the
\emph{Einstein static universe} $(\R\times S^3,g_\ESU)$ for $k=+1$ or the
\emph{Milne chart} $(\R\times H^3,g_\Milne)$ for $k=-1$. The wave operator
identity used in \cite{Remondiere2026} must be replaced by the curvature-corrected
form $(\Box_{\tilde g}-R[\tilde g]/6)(a\varphi)=a^3(\Box_g-R[g]/6)\varphi$
of Wald 1984, App.~D.9; we re-verify this identity symbolically in sympy
for $k\in\{-1,0,+1\}$. Two extensions follow.
For $k=-1$ (open FRW), the Milne chart is isometric to the interior of the
Minkowski future lightcone, so the unitary equivalence and hence the type
II$_\infty$ classification of the modular crossed product is
\emph{unconditional}. For $k=+1$ (closed FRW), the conformal target is the
Einstein static universe; Buchholz--Mund--Summers~\cite{BMS2000}
unconditionally establish the condition of geometric modular action,
modular stability and $SO(4,1)$ covariance for transplanted
de~Sitter wedge nets on RW backgrounds, plus cyclicity on diamonds of
sufficient conformal-time extent (their Theorem~3.1, Corollaries~3.2--3.3),
but do \emph{not} compute either the type or the Connes spectrum of the
ESU spherical-diamond algebra of the conformally coupled scalar. The closed-FRW
type II$_\infty$ statement is therefore conditional on the residual hypothesis
\textbf{(H1$_{+1}$)}: the wedge net of the conformally coupled massless scalar on
ESU in its conformally invariant ground state has type III$_1$ factors with
full Connes spectrum on its spherical-diamond subalgebras. We document that
BMS~\cite{BMS2000} themselves flag the conformally-coupled-scalar question on
RW (and a fortiori on ESU) as explicitly open in their concluding remarks
(p.~16, last paragraph before References).
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

The companion note \cite{Remondiere2026} establishes a conformal-pullback
type II$_\infty$ classification of the gravitationally dressed algebra of a
comoving observer in spatially flat radiation-dominated and matter-dominated
FRW (its Theorems~3.5 and~3.6, henceforth ``the $k=0$ theorem'' or
``Theorem~3.5/3.6'' for definiteness): the conformal isometry of $(g_\FRW,
\eta_{\mu\nu})$ together with conformal invariance of the conformally
coupled $(\xi=1/6)$ massless scalar in $d=4$ lifts to a unitary
$U:\HH_\FRW\to\HH_\Mink$ intertwining the FRW conformal vacuum with the
Minkowski vacuum and the FRW comoving-diamond algebra with the
Hislop--Longo~\cite{HislopLongo1982} Minkowski-diamond algebra. By
Tomita--Takesaki uniqueness and the Connes--Takesaki crossed-product
construction~\cite{Connes1973, CLPW2023}, $\AA(D_\OO)_\FRW
\rtimes_{\sigma^\FRW}\R$ is a type II$_\infty$ factor whenever $a(\eta)$ is
smooth and strictly positive on the closed diamond.

The construction in \cite{Remondiere2026} is restricted to $k=0$ for two
reasons. (a) Conformal flatness of FRW is automatic for $k=0$ in conformal
coordinates; for $k=\pm 1$ the metric is conformally flat as a
\emph{Lorentzian} manifold (it is conformal to a chart of either the
Einstein static universe or the Milne chart of $H^3$), but the conformal
target is no longer Minkowski. (b) The off-shell wave-operator identity
(eq.~(5) of \cite{Remondiere2026})
\(
\Box_{\tilde g}(a\varphi) = a^3(\Box_g - R[g]/6)\varphi
\)
is correct as written only when $R[\tilde g]=0$, i.e.\ when the conformal
target is Ricci-flat; for the Einstein static universe $R[\tilde g]=+6$ and
for the Milne chart $R[\tilde g]=-6$, and an additional curvature term
must be carried.

The present note records the extension of \cite{Remondiere2026} to the two
remaining cases $k=\pm 1$. Section~\ref{sec:prelim} reviews the spatially
curved FRW geometries and the static conformal targets ESU and Milne;
Section~\ref{sec:cc-cov} states and sympy-verifies the curvature-corrected
identity (Wald 1984 App.~D.9 \cite{Wald1984}); Section~\ref{sec:open}
gives the unconditional $k=-1$ theorem; Section~\ref{sec:closed} the
conditional $k=+1$ theorem with the verbatim Buchholz--Mund--Summers
\cite{BMS2000} partial resolution; Section~\ref{sec:openq} the open
questions.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Statements (informal).} Theorem~\ref{thm:open} (open FRW,
$k=-1$): \emph{unconditional}, the modular crossed product is a type
II$_\infty$ factor. Theorem~\ref{thm:closed} (closed FRW, $k=+1$):
\emph{conditional} on the residual hypothesis H1$_{+1}$ (type III$_1$ +
full Connes spectrum on the ESU spherical-diamond algebra of the conformally
coupled scalar); items (1)--(2) (geometric modular action, modular
stability, $SO(4,1)$ covariance, cyclicity on long-enough diamonds) are
\emph{unconditional} via BMS \cite{BMS2000} Thm.~3.1 and Cors.~3.2--3.3,
and only step (3) (crossed-product type) remains open.

\noindent\textbf{Scope and limitations.} As in \cite{Remondiere2026}:
conformal coupling $\xi=1/6$, smooth strict positivity of $a(\eta)$ on the
closed comoving diamond (bounded away from any zero of $a$ and from any
conformal boundary, in particular from Big Bang and Big Crunch); the
wedge case is excluded as in \cite[Rem.~3.3]{Remondiere2026}; massive,
non-conformal $(\xi\ne 1/6)$ and interacting fields are out of scope.

\section{Preliminaries: spatially curved FRW and conformal targets}\label{sec:prelim}

\subsection{Spatially curved FRW in conformal coordinates}\label{sec:metric}

Let $S_k(\chi):=\sin\chi$ for $k=+1$, $S_0(\chi):=\chi$ for $k=0$, and
$S_{-1}(\chi):=\sinh\chi$ for $k=-1$. In conformal time $\eta$ and
hyperspherical comoving coordinates $(\chi,\theta,\phi)$, the spatially
curved FRW metric reads
\begin{equation}\label{eq:frw-curved}
\mathrm{d}s^2_\FRW
= a(\eta)^2\Big[-\mathrm{d}\eta^2 + \mathrm{d}\chi^2
  + S_k(\chi)^2\big(\mathrm{d}\theta^2 + \sin^2\theta\,\mathrm{d}\phi^2\big)\Big].
\end{equation}
The case $k=0$ recovers \cite[eq.~(1)]{Remondiere2026}. The conformal-time
range is $\eta\in(0,\infty)$ for $k=0,-1$ and $\eta\in(0,\eta_*)$ for $k=+1$
with $\eta_*=\pi$ (radiation-dominated) or $2\pi$ (matter-dominated). For
$k=+1$, the Big Bang is at $\eta=0$ and the Big Crunch at $\eta=\eta_*$. For
$k=-1$, the Big Bang is at $\eta=0$ and the conformal-time range extends to
$\eta\to\infty$. Conformal flatness of \eqref{eq:frw-curved} as a Lorentzian
manifold (vanishing Weyl tensor) holds for all $k$ \cite{IihoshiKetov2007,
Wald1994, PenroseRindler1986}.

\subsection{Conformal target spacetimes}\label{sec:targets}

Writing $g_\FRW=a(\eta)^2\,\tilde g_k$, the static conformal target
$\tilde g_k$ is a function of $k$ only:
\begin{align}
\tilde g_0 &= -\mathrm{d}\eta^2 + \mathrm{d}\chi^2 + \chi^2\,\mathrm{d}\Omega^2
            \;=\; \eta_{\mu\nu}\quad (\text{Minkowski}, R[\tilde g]=0),
            \label{eq:target-flat}\\
\tilde g_{+1}
            &= -\mathrm{d}\eta^2 + \mathrm{d}\chi^2 + \sin^2\chi\,\mathrm{d}\Omega^2
            \;=\; g_\ESU\quad
            (\text{Einstein static universe } \R\times S^3,\ R[\tilde g]=+6),
            \label{eq:target-esu}\\
\tilde g_{-1}
            &= -\mathrm{d}\eta^2 + \mathrm{d}\chi^2 + \sinh^2\chi\,\mathrm{d}\Omega^2
            \;=\; g_\Milne\quad
            (\text{Milne chart } \R\times H^3,\ R[\tilde g]=-6).
            \label{eq:target-milne}
\end{align}
Eq.~\eqref{eq:target-flat} is the $k=0$ case used in \cite{Remondiere2026}.
Eq.~\eqref{eq:target-esu} is the conformal-compactification target of any
closed FRW \cite[\S5.4]{Wald1994}, \cite[Ch.~9]{PenroseRindler1986}.
Eq.~\eqref{eq:target-milne} is the conformal target of any open FRW; it is
isometric to the interior of the Minkowski future lightcone $I^+(0)\subset
\R^{1,3}$ via $t=e^\eta\cosh\chi$, $r=e^\eta\sinh\chi$
\cite[\S5.3]{Wald1994}, so $k=-1$ factors through a further conformal
isometry to a subset of $\R^{1,3}$.

\smallskip

\noindent\emph{Comoving worldline in the target.} A comoving observer at
$\chi=0$ in $(g_\FRW,k=\pm 1)$ pulls back, under the rescaling
$\varphi\mapsto a\varphi$ (Section~\ref{sec:cc-cov}), to the timelike
geodesic at $\chi=0$ in $\tilde g_k$: a static observer of ESU for $k=+1$,
and a static observer of Milne (which corresponds to the apex of the
Minkowski lightcone in the further isometric embedding) for $k=-1$.

\section{Curvature-corrected conformal-covariance identity}\label{sec:cc-cov}

The off-shell conformal-covariance identity used in \cite[eq.~(5)]{Remondiere2026},
\(
\Box_{\tilde g}(a\varphi) = a^3(\Box_g-R[g]/6)\varphi,
\)
holds when the conformal target $\tilde g$ is Ricci-flat ($R[\tilde g]=0$),
which is the case for spatially flat FRW. For $k=\pm 1$ the static target
$\tilde g_k$ is \emph{not} Ricci-flat, and the correct off-shell identity
(Wald 1984, Appendix~D, eq.~D.9 \cite{Wald1984}) carries a curvature term:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:cc-curved}
\big(\Box_{\tilde g} - \tfrac{1}{6}R[\tilde g]\big)\,(a\,\varphi)
\;=\; a^3\,\big(\Box_g - \tfrac{1}{6}R[g]\big)\,\varphi.
\end{equation}
For $k=0$, $R[\tilde g]=0$ and \eqref{eq:cc-curved} reduces to the form used
in \cite{Remondiere2026}. For $k=+1$, $R[\tilde g_{+1}]=R[g_\ESU]=+6$; for
$k=-1$, $R[\tilde g_{-1}]=R[g_\Milne]=-6$. (Throughout, we use units in
which the spatial unit-curvature radius is $1$.)

\smallskip

\noindent\emph{Sympy verification.} The script
\texttt{piste\_spatially\_curved.py} (bundled with the source repository,
see also \cite{Remondiere2026}) computes both sides of \eqref{eq:cc-curved}
in $(\eta,\chi,\theta,\phi)$ components for arbitrary smooth $a(\eta)$ and
arbitrary $\varphi(\eta,\chi,\theta,\phi)$, and reports a difference of zero
after \texttt{simplify} for all three values $k\in\{-1,0,+1\}$. The Ricci
scalars (re-derived sympy-symbolically from the Christoffel symbols of
$g_\FRW$ and $\tilde g_k$ separately) match the analytic expressions
$R[g_\FRW] = 6\,a^{-2}\,\big(a''/a + k\big)$ and $R[\tilde g_k]=6k$
exactly.

\smallskip

\noindent\emph{Consequence.} The conformally coupled massless scalar
$(\Box_g-R/6)\varphi=0$ on $g_\FRW$ pulls back via $\varphi\mapsto
\tilde\varphi:=a\varphi$ to \emph{the conformally coupled massless scalar on
the static target} $\tilde g_k$, namely
$(\Box_{\tilde g_k}-R[\tilde g_k]/6)\tilde\varphi=0$, \emph{not} to the free
wave equation (except for $k=0$, where $R[\tilde g_0]=0$ and the conformally
coupled equation coincides with the free wave equation). In particular, the
target theory is the conformally coupled massless scalar on ESU (for $k=+1$)
or on Milne (for $k=-1$).

\smallskip

\noindent\emph{Symplectic form pulls back exactly.} The Klein--Gordon
symplectic form on a constant-$\eta$ Cauchy slice $\Sigma_\eta\subset
D_\OO$ (with the curvature term $-(R/6)\varphi^2$ contributing nothing to
the antisymmetric current, as in
\cite[\S3.1(b)]{Remondiere2026}) reads, for the spatially curved metric
\eqref{eq:frw-curved},
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sigma-curved}
\sigma_\FRW(\varphi_1,\varphi_2)
\;=\; \int_{\Sigma_\eta}\big(\varphi_1\,\partial_\eta\varphi_2
                            -\varphi_2\,\partial_\eta\varphi_1\big)\,
        a(\eta)^2\,S_k(\chi)^2\sin\theta\,
        \mathrm{d}\chi\,\mathrm{d}\theta\,\mathrm{d}\phi,
\end{equation}
with weight $a^2$ from $\sqrt{h}\,n^\eta=a^3\cdot a^{-1}=a^2$, and
$S_k(\chi)^2\sin\theta$ the spatial volume element in $\tilde g_k$.
Substituting $\varphi_i=\tilde\varphi_i/a$ and using
$\partial_\eta(\tilde\varphi/a)=a^{-1}\partial_\eta\tilde\varphi
-a^{-2}(\partial_\eta a)\tilde\varphi$ as in
\cite[\S3.1(b)]{Remondiere2026}, the two
$(\partial_\eta a)/a$ cross terms cancel by antisymmetry of the symplectic
bracket and one recovers $\sigma_\FRW(\varphi_1,\varphi_2)
=\sigma_{\tilde g_k}(\tilde\varphi_1,\tilde\varphi_2)$.

\section{Theorem (open FRW, $k=-1$): unconditional type II$_\infty$}
\label{sec:open}

We now state and prove the unconditional extension of \cite[Thm.~3.5]{Remondiere2026}
to open FRW, by composition with the classical conformal isometry
$\Milne\hookrightarrow I^+(0)\subset\R^{1,3}$.

\begin{proposition}[Conformal unitary, $k=-1$]\label{prop:U-open}
Let $a:[\eta_i,\eta_f]\to\R_+$ be smooth and strictly positive on the
closed conformal-time interval, with $0<\eta_i<\eta_f<\infty$. Let
$D_\OO\subset(\FRW, k=-1)$ be a doubly-bounded causal diamond of a comoving
observer with conformal-time interval contained in $[\eta_i,\eta_f]$, and
let $D_\OO^\Milne$ denote the same coordinate region viewed as a subset of
the Milne chart $\tilde g_{-1}$. Then there exists a unitary
$U_{-1}:\HH_\FRW\to\HH_\Milne$ such that
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $U_{-1}\,\Omega_\FRW = \Omega_\Milne$, where $\Omega_\Milne$ is
the conformally invariant ground state of the conformally coupled massless
scalar on $\tilde g_{-1}$;
\item[(ii)] $U_{-1}\,\varphi_\FRW(f)\,U_{-1}^{-1}
= \tilde\varphi_\Milne(a^3\cdot f)$ for all $f\in C_c^\infty(D_\OO)$, where
$\tilde\varphi_\Milne$ is the rescaled field on Milne;
\item[(iii)] $U_{-1}\,\AA(D_\OO)_\FRW\,U_{-1}^{-1}
= \AA(D_\OO^\Milne)_\Milne$ as von Neumann subalgebras of $B(\HH_\Milne)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
By \eqref{eq:cc-curved}, the field rescaling $\varphi\mapsto\tilde\varphi=a\varphi$
maps the conformally coupled scalar EOM on $g_\FRW$ to the conformally
coupled scalar EOM on $\tilde g_{-1}$. By the symplectic computation of
\eqref{eq:sigma-curved}, the test-function rescaling
$f\mapsto a^3 f$ matches the FRW symplectic form to the Milne symplectic
form on solutions of $D_\OO$ and $D_\OO^\Milne$. Smoothness and strict
positivity of $a$ on $\overline{D_\OO}$ make $f\mapsto a^3 f$ a
$C_c^\infty$-bijection (its inverse $h\mapsto a^{-3} h$ is also
$C_c^\infty$). The induced symplectic transformation lifts to a unitary
$U_{-1}$ on the symmetric Fock spaces with $U_{-1}\Omega_\FRW=\Omega_\Milne$,
exactly as in \cite[Prop.~3.4]{Remondiere2026}. The double-commutant identity
(iii) then follows from (ii) by the same argument.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Open FRW, $k=-1$, unconditional]\label{thm:open}
Let $\varphi$ be the conformally coupled massless scalar field on open FRW
with smooth strictly positive scale factor $a(\eta)$ on the closed
conformal-time interval $[\eta_i,\eta_f]$, $0<\eta_i<\eta_f<\infty$, in the
conformal vacuum $\Omega_\FRW$. Let $D_\OO$ be a doubly-bounded causal
diamond of a comoving observer with conformal-time interval contained in
$[\eta_i,\eta_f]$, and assume the diamond's image $D_\OO^\Milne$ in the
Milne chart, lifted under the isometric embedding
$\Milne\hookrightarrow I^+(0)\subset\R^{1,3}$, is a doubly-bounded causal
diamond $M_\OO\subset\R^{1,3}$ contained strictly inside the Minkowski
future lightcone of the origin (i.e.\ $\overline{M_\OO}\cap\partial I^+(0)
= \emptyset$). Let $\sigma^\FRW$ denote the modular automorphism group of
$(\AA(D_\OO)_\FRW,\Omega_\FRW)$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\sigma^\FRW_t = \Ad U_{-1}^{-1}\circ\sigma^\HL_t\circ\Ad U_{-1}$,
where $\sigma^\HL$ is the Hislop--Longo modular automorphism of the
conformally identified Minkowski diamond $\AA(M_\OO)_\Mink$.
\item $\Gamma(\sigma^\FRW)=\R$.
\item $\AA(D_\OO)_\FRW\rtimes_{\sigma^\FRW}\R$ is a type II$_\infty$
factor.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By Proposition~\ref{prop:U-open}, $U_{-1}$ implements a $W^*$-isomorphism
$\Ad U_{-1}:\AA(D_\OO)_\FRW\xrightarrow{\sim}\AA(D_\OO^\Milne)_\Milne$ with
$U_{-1}\Omega_\FRW=\Omega_\Milne$. The Milne chart is isometric to
$I^+(0)\subset\R^{1,3}$ via $(t,r)=(e^\eta\cosh\chi,e^\eta\sinh\chi)$
\cite[\S5.3]{Wald1994}, and the conformally coupled massless scalar is
conformally invariant; hence
$\AA(D_\OO^\Milne)_\Milne\cong\AA(M_\OO)_\Mink$ via a further unitary
$V_{-1}:\HH_\Milne\to\HH_\Mink$ sending $\Omega_\Milne$ to $\Omega_\Mink$
(the embedding sends the Milne ground state to the restriction of the
Minkowski vacuum to the lightcone interior, in the conformally coupled
sector). The condition $\overline{M_\OO}\cap\partial I^+(0)=\emptyset$
ensures that the Minkowski-vacuum two-point function and its Hadamard
parametrix on $M_\OO$ are uniformly bounded, so the restriction is well-defined
and the unitary $V_{-1}$ exists; this is the Hadamard / Reeh--Schlieder
infrastructure of \cite{HollandsWald2001, Verch1997}. Composing,
$U:=V_{-1}\circ U_{-1}:\HH_\FRW\to\HH_\Mink$ implements
$\AA(D_\OO)_\FRW\xrightarrow{\sim}\AA(M_\OO)_\Mink$ with
$U\Omega_\FRW=\Omega_\Mink$. By Hislop--Longo \cite{HislopLongo1982},
$\AA(M_\OO)_\Mink$ in $\Omega_\Mink$ is a type III$_1$ factor with
M\"obius modular flow having full Connes spectrum $\Gamma(\sigma^\HL)=\R$.
By Tomita--Takesaki uniqueness (Bratteli--Robinson II, \S2.5; Takesaki~II,
Ch.~VIII), modular operators intertwine: $\Delta_\FRW=U^{-1}\Delta^\HL U$,
yielding (1). By W$^*$-isomorphism invariance of the Connes spectrum
(\cite[Prop.~3.4]{Remondiere2026}, equivalent to Connes
\cite[Th\'eor\`eme~1.2.4]{Connes1973}), $\Gamma(\sigma^\FRW)=\Gamma(\sigma^\HL)=\R$,
yielding (2). By Connes--Takesaki, type III$_1$ crossed by its modular
automorphism group with full Connes spectrum $\Gamma=\R$ is type II$_\infty$
\cite[\S2]{CLPW2023, Witten2022, Connes1973}, yielding (3).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}\label{rem:open-folklore}
The doubly-bounded condition $\overline{M_\OO}\cap\partial I^+(0)=\emptyset$
is the natural one for any doubly-bounded comoving diamond not touching the
Big Bang $\eta=0$: under the Milne $\to$ Minkowski embedding, the Big-Bang
hypersurface $\eta=0$ maps onto the future lightcone of the origin, and
$\overline{D_\OO}\cap\{\eta=0\}=\emptyset$ implies
$\overline{M_\OO}\cap\partial I^+(0)=\emptyset$. Hence Theorem~\ref{thm:open}
applies to any doubly-bounded comoving diamond of an open FRW with
$\eta_i>0$, with no additional hypothesis on the algebraic side beyond the
$k=0$ Theorem~3.5/3.6 of \cite{Remondiere2026}. In this sense the $k=-1$
extension is folklore: it follows by direct composition with the classical
isometry of \cite[\S5.3]{Wald1994}.
\end{remark}

\section{Theorem (closed FRW, $k=+1$): conditional type II$_\infty$ + BMS partial resolution}
\label{sec:closed}

For $k=+1$ the conformal target is the Einstein static universe
$\tilde g_{+1}=g_\ESU$, which is \emph{not} conformally embeddable into
Minkowski as a single piece; the conformal compactification of Minkowski
covers only \emph{half} of ESU, and the spherical-diamond modular structure
on full ESU does not reduce to Hislop--Longo on Minkowski by direct
composition.

\subsection{The conformal unitary, $k=+1$}\label{sec:U-closed}

\begin{proposition}[Conformal unitary, $k=+1$]\label{prop:U-closed}
Under the hypotheses of Section~\ref{sec:metric} for $k=+1$ (smooth strictly
positive $a(\eta)$ on a closed conformal-time interval $[\eta_i,\eta_f]
\subset(0,\eta_*)$, bounded away from Big Bang $\eta=0$ and Big Crunch
$\eta=\eta_*$), and for $D_\OO\subset(\FRW,k=+1)$ a doubly-bounded comoving
causal diamond, there exists a unitary $U_{+1}:\HH_\FRW\to\HH_\ESU$ with
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] $U_{+1}\,\Omega_\FRW=\Omega_\ESU$;
\item[(ii)] $U_{+1}\,\varphi_\FRW(f)\,U_{+1}^{-1}=\tilde\varphi_\ESU(a^3\cdot f)$
for all $f\in C_c^\infty(D_\OO)$;
\item[(iii)] $U_{+1}\,\AA(D_\OO)_\FRW\,U_{+1}^{-1}=\AA(D_\OO^\ESU)_\ESU$.
\end{enumerate}
Here $\Omega_\ESU$ denotes the conformally invariant ground state of the
conformally coupled massless scalar on ESU.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Identical to the proof of Proposition~\ref{prop:U-open}, with $\tilde
g_{-1}=g_\Milne$ replaced by $\tilde g_{+1}=g_\ESU$ and using
\eqref{eq:cc-curved} for $k=+1$ (with $R[\tilde g_{+1}]=+6$). Existence of
the conformally invariant ground state $\Omega_\ESU$ of the conformally
coupled scalar on ESU is classical (Dimock \cite{Dimock1980},
L\"uders--Roberts \cite{LudersRoberts1990}, and \cite[\S5.4]{Wald1994}).
\end{proof}

\subsection{Buchholz--Mund--Summers 2000: partial resolution}\label{sec:bms}

The closed-FRW analogue of Hislop--Longo on the spherical-diamond algebra
$\AA(D_\OO^\ESU)_\ESU$ of the conformally coupled scalar in $\Omega_\ESU$
is not in the literature in the form needed here. Buchholz--Mund--Summers
\cite{BMS2000} establish a partial resolution. We quote their setup and
results verbatim.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{BMS setup.} $\mathrm{RW}=(\R\times S^3,
S^2(\tau)(\mathrm{d}\tau^2-\mathrm{d}\sigma^2))$ with
$\tau\in(-\alpha\pi/2,\alpha\pi/2)$, $0<\alpha\le 1$, conformally embedded
in de~Sitter via the diffeomorphism $\Phi(\tau,\chi,\theta,\phi):=
(f(\tau),\chi,\theta,\phi)$, $f(\tau):=
\arcsin(\sin(\alpha\pi/2)\sin\tau)$ (BMS eqs.~15--16). The transplantation
$\Xi:\mathcal W\to\widetilde{\mathcal W}$ is defined on \emph{wedges only}
(not on points), via
$\widetilde{\mathcal A}(\widetilde W):=\mathcal A(W)$ for
$\widetilde W=\Xi(W)$ (BMS eq.~53), and
$\widetilde\omega(A):=\omega(A)$ (BMS eq.~54).

\begin{theorem}[BMS Theorem~3.1, p.~14, verbatim]\label{thm:BMS-31}
With the above definitions, the net $\{\widetilde{\mathcal A}(\widetilde W)\}$
and state $\widetilde\omega$ satisfy the CGMA (Condition of Geometric
Modular Action) and the Modular Stability Condition. The corresponding net
of von Neumann algebras $\{\widetilde{\mathcal R}(\widetilde W)\}$ satisfies
wedge duality and transforms covariantly under $SO(4,1)$ under the adjoint
action of $\mathcal J$. For $\alpha<1$, there exist $J\in\mathcal J$ which
do not implement point transformations of RW.
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}[BMS Corollary~3.2, $\alpha=1$]\label{thm:BMS-32}
For $\alpha=1$, $\mathcal J$ represents the conformal group $SO(4,1)$ of RW
$\simeq$ dS.
\end{corollary}

\begin{corollary}[BMS Corollary~3.3, double-cone non-triviality]\label{thm:BMS-33}
Let $\widetilde O_{x,\varepsilon}\subset\mathrm{RW}$ be a double cone of
conformal-time half-extent $\varepsilon$ centred at $x$.
If $\varepsilon>\frac{\pi}{2}(1-\alpha)$, then $\widetilde\Omega$ is cyclic
for $\widetilde{\mathcal R}(\widetilde O_{x,\varepsilon})$. If
$\varepsilon<\frac{\pi}{2}(1-\alpha)$, then
$\widetilde{\mathcal R}(\widetilde O_{x,\varepsilon})=\C\cdot\mathbf 1$
(trivial).
\end{corollary}

\smallskip

\noindent\textbf{What BMS resolves and what it leaves open.} BMS~\cite{BMS2000}
establish, on wedges of the transplanted RW net: (a) CGMA, (b) cyclic-separating
$\widetilde\Omega$, (c) modular stability $+$ $SO(4,1)$ covariance
(Theorem~\ref{thm:BMS-31}); and (d) cyclic $\widetilde\Omega$ on diamonds of
extent $\varepsilon>\frac\pi2(1-\alpha)$ (Corollary~\ref{thm:BMS-33}). They do
\emph{not} establish (e) type III$_1$ (the string ``III$_1$'' does not appear),
(f) full Connes spectrum $\Gamma(\sigma^{\rm diamond})=\R$, or (g) the
conformally coupled scalar concretely on ESU --- BMS work with \emph{abstract}
CGMA-nets, citing the conformally coupled scalar (\cite[ref.~12]{Dimock1980},
\cite[ref.~22]{LudersRoberts1990}) only as a candidate for future work. The
closing paragraph of \cite{BMS2000} (p.~16, last paragraph before References)
reads, verbatim:
\begin{quote}
``\dots it would also be interesting to determine if the net [12]
[Dimock 1980] and adiabatic vacuum states [22] [L\"uders--Roberts 1990]
\dots\ satisfy the CGMA and the Modular Stability Condition for our choice
of wedges $\widetilde{\mathcal W}$.''
\end{quote}
This is the explicit ``open problem'' flag from Buchholz--Mund--Summers
themselves: even the CGMA / modular stability question for the conformally
coupled scalar on RW is open in their concluding section, let alone the
type-classification and Connes-spectrum questions.

\subsection{Residual hypothesis and conditional theorem}\label{sec:H1plus1}

\begin{hypothesis}[H1$_{+1}$, residual form]\label{hyp:H1plus1}
The wedge net of the conformally coupled massless scalar on the Einstein
static universe $\R\times S^3$, in its conformally invariant ground state
$\Omega_\ESU$, has type III$_1$ factors on its spherical-diamond
subalgebras with full Connes spectrum
$\Gamma(\sigma^{\rm diamond})=\R$. Equivalently, the net of
\cite{Dimock1980} combined with the L\"uders--Roberts /
adiabatic ground state of \cite{LudersRoberts1990} satisfies
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] factoriality and type III$_1$ on doubly-bounded spherical
diamonds, and
\item[(ii)] $\Gamma(\sigma^{\rm diamond})=\R$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{hypothesis}

The hypothesis is \emph{strictly weaker} than ``$\AA(D_\OO^\ESU)_\ESU$
satisfies all of (a)--(f) above'' because (a)--(d) are now \emph{theorems}
(BMS \cite{BMS2000}, Theorem~\ref{thm:BMS-31} + Corollary~\ref{thm:BMS-32}
+ Corollary~\ref{thm:BMS-33}) for the dS-transplanted net, with the $\alpha=1$
limit yielding the conformal RW=dS case unconditionally. Only (e)--(f),
the type-and-spectrum input, remain.

\begin{theorem}[Closed FRW, $k=+1$, conditional on H1$_{+1}$]\label{thm:closed}
Let $\varphi$ be the conformally coupled massless scalar on closed FRW with
smooth strictly positive $a(\eta)$ on the closed conformal-time interval
$[\eta_i,\eta_f]\subset(0,\eta_*)$ (bounded away from Big Bang and Big
Crunch), in the conformal vacuum $\Omega_\FRW$. Let $D_\OO\subset
(\FRW,k=+1)$ be a doubly-bounded comoving causal diamond with conformal-time
extent $\Delta\eta:=\eta_f-\eta_i$. Let $\sigma^\FRW$ denote the modular
automorphism group of $(\AA(D_\OO)_\FRW,\Omega_\FRW)$.

Then there exists a unitary $U_{+1}:\HH_\FRW\to\HH_\ESU$ with
$U_{+1}\Omega_\FRW=\Omega_\ESU$ (Proposition~\ref{prop:U-closed}), and:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{(Wedge level, unconditional via BMS \cite{BMS2000}.)} The
transplanted wedge algebra $\widetilde{\mathcal R}(\widetilde W_\OO)$
associated to $D_\OO$ via the BMS transplantation $\Xi$ satisfies
geometric modular action (CGMA), modular stability and $SO(4,1)$
covariance with cyclic-separating $\widetilde\Omega$. For $\alpha=1$
(conformal RW $\simeq$ dS), $\mathcal J$ implements the full
$SO(4,1)$ conformal group of ESU.
\item \textbf{(Diamond level, unconditional via BMS Cor.~3.3.)} Provided
$\Delta\eta>\frac{\pi}{2}(1-\alpha)$, $\widetilde\Omega$ is cyclic for
the diamond algebra $\widetilde{\mathcal R}(\widetilde D_\OO)$.
\item \textbf{(Crossed-product type, conditional on H1$_{+1}$.)} Conditional
on Hypothesis~\ref{hyp:H1plus1},
$\sigma^\FRW_t=\Ad U_{+1}^{-1}\circ\sigma^\ESU_t\circ\Ad U_{+1}$,
$\Gamma(\sigma^\FRW)=\R$, and $\AA(D_\OO)_\FRW\rtimes_{\sigma^\FRW}\R$ is a
type II$_\infty$ factor.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Items (1)--(2) follow from Proposition~\ref{prop:U-closed} together with the
verbatim statements of Theorem~\ref{thm:BMS-31} and Corollary~\ref{thm:BMS-33}
applied to the transplanted wedge / diamond algebra. Item (3) follows by
the same Tomita--Takesaki + Connes--Takesaki argument as
Theorem~\ref{thm:open}, using $\Omega_\ESU$ in place of $\Omega_\Milne$:
Hypothesis~\ref{hyp:H1plus1} delivers the type III$_1$ classification and
the full Connes spectrum on $\AA(D_\OO^\ESU)_\ESU$ that are needed to apply
the Connes--Takesaki crossed-product type theorem
\cite{Connes1973, CLPW2023}, and the W$^*$-isomorphism invariance of
$\Gamma$ \cite[Prop.~3.4]{Remondiere2026} transports both back to $\AA(D_\OO)_\FRW$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}\label{rem:closed-status}
Items (1)--(2) of Theorem~\ref{thm:closed} are unconditional in the sense
that they require no hypothesis beyond the BMS setup; they upgrade the
``everything is conjectural'' status of the closed-FRW companion theorem
to ``geometric modular action + modular stability + $SO(4,1)$ covariance +
cyclicity on long-enough diamonds are theorems; only the type-classification
of the crossed product remains conditional''. The residual conditional
input H1$_{+1}$ is narrowed from ``the entire algebraic-quantum-field
infrastructure'' (where it stood before BMS) to ``factoriality + type
III$_1$ + Connes spectrum on diamonds'' (where it stands after BMS).
\end{remark}

\section{Open questions}\label{sec:openq}

\begin{question}[Unconditional H1$_{+1}$]\label{q:H1}
Prove or refute Hypothesis~\ref{hyp:H1plus1} unconditionally. Three
strategies suggest themselves: (i) Brunetti--Guido--Longo \cite{BGL1993}
modular structure for diamonds in conformal QFT, together with the conformal
embedding $\R^{1,3}\hookrightarrow\ESU$ which covers exactly half of ESU,
and an $O(2,4)$-conjugation argument transferring the spherical-diamond
modular generator into the Minkowski half (folklore but not in the
literature with the precision needed); (ii) explicit $S^3$
spherical-harmonic mode decomposition adapting Fr\"ob \cite{Frob2023}
from de~Sitter diamonds to ESU; (iii) extension of the BMS transplantation
\cite{BMS2000} from abstract CGMA-nets to the concrete
Dimock--L\"uders--Roberts net \cite{Dimock1980, LudersRoberts1990} of the
conformally coupled scalar.
\end{question}

\begin{question}[Big Bang / Big Crunch double-singularity for $k=+1$]\label{q:bbc}
The closed FRW recollapses at $\eta=\eta_*$ ($=\pi$ for radiation,
$2\pi$ for matter) in addition to the Big Bang at $\eta=0$. Diamonds
touching $\eta=\eta_*$ admit the same three obstructions as diamonds
touching $\eta=0$ (failure of the rescaling bijection, IR singularity of
the conformal-vacuum two-point function, non-Hadamard boundary behaviour;
see \cite[Question~6.4]{Remondiere2026}). The inductive-limit construction
$\AA_0(D_{\rm BC})_\FRW:=\lim_{\eta_f\to\eta_*^-}\AA(D_\OO)_\FRW$ towards
the Big Crunch may admit a PCT-symmetric counterpart of the Big-Bang
conjecture in \cite[Question~6.4]{Remondiere2026}.
\end{question}

\begin{question}[Cosmological no-boundary / Hartle--Hawking states]\label{q:nb}
The conformal vacuum used throughout is the Bunch--Davies /
adiabatic-conformal state. For closed FRW the no-boundary
(Hartle--Hawking) cosmological state and the Linde / Vilenkin tunnelling
candidates yield different ground states on $\AA(D_\OO^\ESU)_\ESU$. The
type classification of the resulting modular crossed product, even
granting H1$_{+1}$ for the conformal-vacuum state, is open --- in
particular whether the no-boundary state delivers type II$_\infty$ or
II$_1$ (by analogy with the Bunch--Davies / static-patch dichotomy of CLPW
\cite{CLPW2023}).
\end{question}
