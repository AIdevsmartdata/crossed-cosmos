\begin{abstract}
We record a geometric--algebraic identity, the
\emph{Krylov--Diameter Correspondence}, on the type~II$_\infty$
crossed product
$\NN := \AA(D_O)_\FRW \rtimes_\sigma \R$ of a doubly-bounded
comoving causal diamond $D_O$ in a conformally flat
Friedmann--Robertson--Walker background, with $\AA(D_O)_\FRW$ the
local algebra of the conformally coupled massless scalar in the
conformal vacuum (Theorem~3.5 of~\cite{Remondiere2026}). On a smooth
seed inside the diamond, the Block~A modular Krylov spread
complexity $C_k$ on $\NN$, lifted from the
Caputa--Mag\'an--Patramanis--Tonni construction~\cite{CMPT24} via
the Connes--Takesaki dual weight, satisfies in cosmic time $t$
along the central worldline
\[
\frac{1}{C_k(t)}\,\frac{\mathrm{d}C_k(t)}{\mathrm{d}t}
\;=\; \frac{1}{\Rprop(\eta_c)}
\;+\; O\!\big(C_k^{-1}\big),
\]
where $\Rprop(\eta_c) = a(\eta_c)\,\Rconf$ is the proper diameter
radius at the central Cauchy slice $\eta_c$. Both sides are
spacetime scalars on the worldline. We tabulate four
era/diamond combinations (radiation, matter, exact de~Sitter; past-light-cone vs. Hubble-horizon diamonds) verified symbolically in
sympy. The Hubble identity $H(t)=(1/C_k)\,\mathrm{d}C_k/\mathrm{d}t$
holds exactly in two of these and fails by an era-dependent $O(1)$
factor in the others; we do \emph{not} claim a universal
Hubble--Krylov identity. The Block~A definition of $C_k$ on a
type~II$_\infty$ crossed product is novel beyond the
type~II$_1$~\cite{AguilarGutierrez2025} and
type~III$_1$~\cite{CMPT24} precedents and remains conjectural at the
level of the universal operator-growth hypothesis on II$_\infty$
KMS states.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

The crossed-product programme of Witten~\cite{Witten2022} and
Chandrasekaran, Longo, Penington and Witten
(CLPW)~\cite{CLPW2023} produces, from a type~III$_1$ local algebra
$\AA(\OO)$ and a one-parameter modular automorphism group~$\sigma$,
a type~II von~Neumann algebra of \emph{gravitationally dressed}
observables. The semifinite trace on the resulting type~II factor
is a quantitative entropy carrier and underlies recent advances in
the generalised second law and entanglement entropy of subregions
in quantum gravity.

In parallel, Caputa, Mag\'an, Patramanis and Tonni
(CMPT)~\cite{CMPT24} introduced \emph{modular Krylov complexity} for
the operator-spread under the modular flow on type~III$_1$ algebras,
and proved a universal late-modular-time slope $\lambda_L^{\mathrm{mod}}
= 2\pi$ (their Eq.~(4.16)/(5.4)). This is the modular analogue of the
universal operator-growth hypothesis~\cite{ParkerEtAl2019}. The slope
$2\pi$ is intrinsic to the modular automorphism $\Delta^{is}$ of
Tomita--Takesaki theory; it is independent of any background-spacetime
time coordinate.

The present note connects these two strands in the simplest
non-stationary cosmological setting where both make sense. Our
starting point is the type~II$_\infty$ classification of the
crossed-product algebra
\(\NN := \AA(D_O)_\FRW \rtimes_\sigma \R\)
on a comoving causal diamond $D_O$ in a conformally flat FRW
background, established for the conformally coupled massless scalar
in the conformal vacuum in~\cite[Thm.~3.5]{Remondiere2026} via a
conformal pullback to the Hislop--Longo modular flow on the
Minkowski diamond~\cite{HislopLongo1982}. On this II$_\infty$
factor we consider the Krylov spread complexity $C_k$ defined by
Lanczos iteration on the lifted modular generator (Block~A
definition, Section~\ref{sec:blockA}); its rate of growth in cosmic
time $t$ along the central worldline is the object of interest.

The result we record is a clean geometric identity
\begin{equation}\label{eq:KDC-intro}
\frac{1}{C_k(t)}\,\frac{\mathrm{d}C_k(t)}{\mathrm{d}t}
\;=\; \frac{1}{\Rprop(\eta_c)}
\;+\; O\!\big(C_k^{-1}\big),
\end{equation}
where $\Rprop(\eta_c) = a(\eta_c)\,\Rconf$ is the proper diameter
radius at the central Cauchy slice. This holds in any conformally
flat FRW background covered by~\cite[Thms.~3.5--3.6, Cor.~3.7]{Remondiere2026},
\emph{independent of the equation of state} and \emph{independent of
the particular convention chosen for the diamond}, provided
$D_O\subset (\eta_i,\eta_f)$ with $a(\eta)$ smooth and strictly
positive on $\overline{D_O}$ (i.e.\ bounded away from any FRW
singularity).

The Hubble identity $(1/C_k)\,\mathrm{d}C_k/\mathrm{d}t = H(t)$ is
an era- and diamond-convention-dependent \emph{corollary} of
\eqref{eq:KDC-intro}, exact for radiation $+$ past-light-cone and
de~Sitter $+$ Hubble-horizon, and \emph{wrong by a factor of $1/2$}
for matter $+$ past-light-cone (Section~\ref{sec:eras}). We make no
universal Hubble--Krylov claim; the substantive content of the note
is the geometric identity~\eqref{eq:KDC-intro}.

\section{Setup: type \texorpdfstring{II$_\infty$}{II-infty} on the FRW comoving diamond}\label{sec:setup}

\subsection{The local algebra and the conformal pullback}

We adopt the framework of~\cite[\S\S2--3]{Remondiere2026}. Let
$(M, g_\FRW)$ be a spatially flat conformally flat
Friedmann--Robertson--Walker spacetime with
\(\mathrm{d}s^2_\FRW = a(\eta)^2\big(-\mathrm{d}\eta^2 + \mathrm{d}\mathbf{x}^2\big)\)
in conformal time $\eta\in I\subset\R$, with $a(\eta)$ smooth and
strictly positive on the open interval $I$. We work with the
conformally coupled massless scalar field $\varphi$ ($\xi = 1/6$) in
the conformal (Bunch--Davies) vacuum $\Omega_\FRW$, and with a
doubly-bounded causal diamond $D_O$ for a comoving observer at
$\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$, of conformal-time interval
$[\eta_i,\eta_f]\subset I$ and central Cauchy slice
$\eta_c := (\eta_i + \eta_f)/2$. The local von~Neumann algebra
$\AA(D_O)_\FRW$ is generated, in the GNS representation
$\pi_{\Omega_\FRW}$, by the smeared fields $\varphi(f)$ for
$f\in C_c^\infty(D_O)$.

\begin{theorem}[{\cite[Thm.~3.5]{Remondiere2026} and \cite[Thm.~3.6, Cor.~3.7]{Remondiere2026}}]\label{thm:typeII}
For any of the three FRW backgrounds $a(\eta) = a_0\eta$
(radiation, $\eta\in(0,\infty)$), $a(\eta) = a_0\eta^2$
(matter, $\eta\in(0,\infty)$), $a(\eta) = -1/(H_{\mathrm{dS}}\,\eta)$
(de~Sitter Poincar\'e patch, $\eta\in(-\infty,0)$), and any
doubly-bounded comoving diamond $D_O$ as above, the modular
automorphism group $\sigma^\FRW$ of the pair
$(\AA(D_O)_\FRW, \Omega_\FRW)$ is unitarily intertwined with the
Hislop--Longo M\"obius flow $\sigma^\HL$ on the conformally
identified Minkowski diamond, has Connes spectrum $\Gamma(\sigma^\FRW)
= \R$, and the crossed product
\[
\NN \;:=\; \AA(D_O)_\FRW \rtimes_{\sigma^\FRW} \R
\]
is a type~II$_\infty$ factor.
\end{theorem}

\subsection{The semifinite trace and standard form}

By Connes--Takesaki, $\NN$ carries a (semifinite, faithful, normal)
canonical trace
\(\mathrm{tr}_\NN := \phi_{\Omega_\FRW}\circ E\)
obtained from the dual operator-valued weight to the modular
flow~\cite{Witten2022, CLPW2023}. Let
$L^2(\NN, \mathrm{tr}_\NN)$ be the standard form Hilbert space of
$\NN$, with cyclic separating vector $\Xi_\omega$ representing the
trace. We write $\Delta_\NN$ for the modular operator of
$(\NN, \Xi_\omega)$; under the Connes--Takesaki dual-weight
construction the modular generator decomposes as
\(K_\NN = -\log\Delta_\NN = K_\FRW \otimes \mathbf{1} + \mathbf{1}\otimes p_t,\)
where $K_\FRW = -\log\Delta_\FRW$ is the lifted modular Hamiltonian
of the original type~III$_1$ algebra $\AA(D_O)_\FRW$ and $p_t$ is
the dual translation generator from the $\R$-factor in the crossed
product. (We follow the sign conventions of~\cite{CLPW2023, Witten2022}.)

\subsection{The proper diameter radius}

The diamond $D_O$ has, on each constant-$\eta$ Cauchy slice, an
\emph{intrinsic comoving radius} $\Rconf$ and an \emph{intrinsic
proper radius}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Rproper}
\Rprop(\eta) \;:=\; a(\eta)\,\Rconf,
\end{equation}
which is a spacetime scalar (a function on $\overline{D_O}$,
gauge-invariant under reparametrisations of $\eta$). At the central
Cauchy slice $\eta = \eta_c$ we write $\Rprop(\eta_c)$. The
prescription for $\Rconf$ in terms of $\eta_i, \eta_f$ depends on
the diamond convention; we discuss two natural choices in
Section~\ref{sec:eras}.

\section{Block~A definition of \texorpdfstring{$C_k$}{C\_k} on a type \texorpdfstring{II$_\infty$}{II-infty} crossed product}\label{sec:blockA}

\subsection{Definition}

Following~\cite{CMPT24} for the type~III$_1$ case, and lifting via
the Connes--Takesaki dual weight to the type~II$_\infty$ standard
form of $\NN$, we define:

\begin{definition}[Block~A modular Krylov spread complexity on a type~II$_\infty$ crossed product]\label{def:Ck}
Fix a smooth seed
\(\lvert\mathrm{seed}\rangle := \pi_{\Omega_\FRW}\!\big(\varphi(f_0)\big)\,\Xi_\omega\)
with $f_0 \in C_c^\infty(D_O)$, viewed as a vector in
$L^2(\NN, \mathrm{tr}_\NN)$. Run the Lanczos algorithm on
$-iK_\NN$: this generates an orthonormal Krylov basis
$\{\lvert O_n\rangle\}_{n\ge 0}$ via the three-term recursion
\(b_{n+1}\,\lvert O_{n+1}\rangle = (-iK_\NN)\,\lvert O_n\rangle - a_n\lvert O_n\rangle - b_n\lvert O_{n-1}\rangle\)
with Lanczos coefficients $\{b_n\}_{n\ge 1}$. Define the
\emph{Krylov spread complexity}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Ck-def}
C_k(s) \;:=\; \sum_{n\ge 0} n\,\big\lvert\langle O_n\,\lvert\, e^{-iK_\NN s}\,\lvert\,\mathrm{seed}\rangle\big\rvert^2.
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

\noindent\emph{Reductions.}
(i) On a finite-trace corner $p\NN p$ with $\mathrm{tr}_\NN(p) <
\infty$, Definition~\ref{def:Ck} reduces to the finite-dimensional
Krylov complexity of Parker et al.~\cite{ParkerEtAl2019}.
(ii) Restriction of the seed to $\AA(D_O)_\FRW \otimes \mathbf{1}$
(``ungauged'' limit, dropping the $\R$-factor) recovers the
type~III$_1$ modular Krylov complexity of~\cite{CMPT24}.
(iii) On a type~II$_1$ algebra (e.g.\ the de~Sitter static-patch
construction of~\cite{CLPW2023}) the same definition reproduces the
DSSYK / boundary-Krylov construction of
Aguilar-Guti\'errez~\cite{AguilarGutierrez2025} after substituting
the type~II$_1$ trace.

\subsection{Status}

\begin{remark}\label{rem:blockA-status}
Definition~\ref{def:Ck} is well-posed under standard hypotheses on
$\NN$ and on the smooth seed (the seed lies in the analytic vectors
of $K_\NN$; the Krylov basis is orthonormal in
$L^2(\NN, \mathrm{tr}_\NN)$; the spread complexity is a normalised
moment of the Lanczos return amplitude as in Vardian~\cite{CMPT24}
and~\cite{AguilarGutierrez2025}). What we do \emph{not} prove here is
the universal operator-growth hypothesis (UOGH) on type~II$_\infty$
KMS states, which would underwrite a $b_n = O(n)$ scaling and the
$e^{2\pi s}$ asymptotic of $C_k(s)$ on $\NN$ analogous to Parker et
al.~\cite{ParkerEtAl2019} and CMPT~\cite{CMPT24}. The asymptotic
slope $\lambda_L^{\mathrm{mod}}=2\pi$ is taken as input from the
type~III$_1$ subalgebra: in the conformal-pullback setting of
Theorem~\ref{thm:typeII}, the seed and the Lanczos sequence in
$L^2(\NN, \mathrm{tr}_\NN)$ project onto the type~III$_1$ Lanczos
sequence of~\cite{CMPT24} on $L^2(\AA(D_O)_\FRW, \Omega_\FRW)$ via
the conditional expectation $E:\NN\to\AA(D_O)_\FRW$, which preserves
the late-modular-time growth rate. A direct II$_\infty$-intrinsic
proof of $\lambda_L^{\mathrm{mod}} = 2\pi$ is open (see
Section~\ref{sec:open}).
\end{remark}

\section{Main theorem: Krylov--Diameter Correspondence}\label{sec:main}

The Bisognano--Wichmann/Hislop--Longo modular Hamiltonian on a
spacelike-symmetric Minkowski diamond of proper radius $\Rprop$
has, at the centre $\mathbf{x}_c$, a local Unruh temperature
\begin{equation}\label{eq:CHM}
T_{\mathrm{loc}}(\mathbf{x}_c) \;=\; \frac{1}{2\pi\,\Rprop},
\qquad \text{equivalently}\qquad
\left.\frac{\mathrm{d}s}{\mathrm{d}\tau_{\mathrm{proper}}}\right|_{\mathbf{x}_c} \;=\; \frac{1}{2\pi\,\Rprop},
\end{equation}
where $s$ is the Tomita--Takesaki modular parameter (the parameter of
$\Delta_\NN^{is}$) and $\tau_{\mathrm{proper}}$ is proper time of an
observer at the centre. This is the Casini--Huerta--Myers
relation~\cite{CHM2008}; here $\Rprop = \Rprop(\eta_c)$ as
defined by~\eqref{eq:Rproper}, since the central Cauchy slice
intersects the centre worldline at proper time corresponding to
$\eta_c$.

\begin{theorem}[Krylov--Diameter Correspondence on the type II$_\infty$ FRW crossed product]\label{thm:KDC}
Let $a(\eta)$ be a smooth strictly positive scale factor on
$\eta\in I$, $D_O$ a doubly-bounded comoving causal diamond with
$\overline{D_O}\subset I$, $\NN = \AA(D_O)_\FRW\rtimes_\sigma\R$ the
type~II$_\infty$ factor of Theorem~\ref{thm:typeII}, $C_k$ the
Block~A modular Krylov spread complexity of Definition~\ref{def:Ck}
on a smooth seed $\lvert\mathrm{seed}\rangle\in L^2(\NN, \mathrm{tr}_\NN)$,
and $t$ the comoving observer's cosmic time at the centre of $D_O$.
Then, in the late-modular-time asymptotic regime under the working
assumption that the universal slope
$\lambda_L^{\mathrm{mod}} = 2\pi$ of CMPT~\cite{CMPT24} carries over
to $\NN$ via Remark~\ref{rem:blockA-status},
\begin{equation}\label{eq:KDC}
\boxed{\;\;
\frac{1}{C_k(t)}\,\frac{\mathrm{d}C_k(t)}{\mathrm{d}t}
\;=\; \frac{1}{\Rprop(\eta_c)}
\;+\; O\!\big(C_k^{-1}\big),
\;\;}
\end{equation}
where $\Rprop(\eta_c) = a(\eta_c)\,\Rconf$ is the proper diameter
radius at the central Cauchy slice. Both sides of~\eqref{eq:KDC} are
spacetime scalars on the central worldline; the identity is
manifestly invariant under reparametrisations
$t \mapsto t'(t)$ of the worldline parameter.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
By~\cite[Eq.~(4.16)/(5.4)]{CMPT24} and Remark~\ref{rem:blockA-status},
the Lanczos coefficients on the type~III$_1$ sub-flow satisfy
$b_n \sim \pi n$ in modular-time units, hence
$C_k(s)\sim e^{2\pi s}$ at late modular time and
\((1/C_k)\,\mathrm{d}C_k/\mathrm{d}s = \lambda_L^{\mathrm{mod}}=2\pi.\)
By~\eqref{eq:CHM}, on the central worldline,
\(\mathrm{d}s/\mathrm{d}t = 1/(2\pi\,\Rprop(\eta_c))\) where
$t = \tau_{\mathrm{proper}}$ for a comoving observer in FRW. The
chain rule then gives
\(\frac{1}{C_k}\,\frac{\mathrm{d}C_k}{\mathrm{d}t} = 2\pi \cdot \frac{1}{2\pi\Rprop(\eta_c)} = \frac{1}{\Rprop(\eta_c)},\)
up to corrections of order $C_k^{-1}$ from the subleading
late-modular-time expansion of the spread complexity.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Gauge invariance]\label{rem:gauge}
The identity~\eqref{eq:KDC} is invariant under reparametrisation of
the worldline parameter (e.g.\ $t\to\eta$ or any other smooth
monotone reparametrisation): both
$(1/C_k)\,\mathrm{d}C_k/\mathrm{d}t$ and $1/\Rprop(\eta_c)$
transform identically by the chain rule, and the second is a
pointwise spacetime scalar. The traversal through cosmic time vs.\
conformal time vs.\ modular time is purely a matter of which
parameter one differentiates with respect to; the underlying
geometric--algebraic equality is the same. We verified this
symbolically (sympy, script
\texttt{piste1\_gauge\_audit.py}); see~\cite{Remondiere2026} for
details.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[What this is \emph{not}]\label{rem:not}
\eqref{eq:KDC} is \emph{not} a universal Hubble--Krylov identity. The
Hubble parameter
$H(t) := (1/a)\,\mathrm{d}a/\mathrm{d}t\big|_{\mathrm{central worldline}}$
agrees with the right-hand side of~\eqref{eq:KDC} only in two of the
four era/diamond combinations tabulated in
Section~\ref{sec:eras}, and \emph{disagrees by a factor of $1/2$}
for matter $+$ past-light-cone diamond. The clean statement is
geometric (proper diameter radius), not Friedmann (Hubble
expansion rate); see also the gauge audit cited in the title
footnote. Conversely, as a consistency
check, the special cases for which $H(t) = 1/\Rprop(\eta_c)$ does
hold (radiation $+$ past-light-cone, de~Sitter $+$ Hubble-horizon)
are recovered as Friedmann arithmetic, not as deeper structural
features of $\NN$.
\end{remark}

\section{Era-by-era special cases}\label{sec:eras}

We tabulate the right-hand side of~\eqref{eq:KDC} for the three
backgrounds covered by~\cite[Thms.~3.5, 3.6, Cor.~3.7]{Remondiere2026},
combined with two natural conventions for the diamond:
(\textbf{PLC}) the past-light-cone diamond
\(\eta_i \to 0^+,\ \eta_f = 2\eta_{\mathrm{obs}},\ \Rconf = \eta_{\mathrm{obs}}\)
(only meaningful when the lower limit is regular; for de~Sitter we
use the analogous past-cone with $\eta_i\to-\infty$);
(\textbf{H}) the Hubble-horizon diamond defined by
\(\Rprop = 1/H(t)\) at the central slice. Both ratios in the
final column are in cosmic time $t$; the entries are sympy-verified
(script \texttt{piste1\_gauge\_audit.py}, see~\cite{Remondiere2026}).

\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.25}
\begin{tabular}{lllll}
\toprule
Era & $a(\eta)$ & $H(t)$ & Diamond & $\Rprop(\eta_c)^{-1}/H(t)$ \\
\midrule
Radiation & $a_0\,\eta$         & $1/(2t)$  & PLC & $1$ (exact) \\
Matter    & $a_0\,\eta^2$        & $2/(3t)$  & PLC & $1/2$ \\
Matter    & $a_0\,\eta^2$        & $2/(3t)$  & H   & $1$ (tautological) \\
de Sitter & $-1/(H_{\mathrm{dS}}\,\eta)$ & $H_{\mathrm{dS}}$ & H   & $1$ (tautological) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\medskip

\noindent The reading of the table is as follows. The Hubble identity
$H(t) = (1/C_k)\,\mathrm{d}C_k/\mathrm{d}t$ holds:
(i) exactly, by Friedmann arithmetic, for radiation $+$ PLC, since
\(\Rprop(\eta_c) = a(\eta_c)\,\eta_{\mathrm{obs}} = a_0\eta_{\mathrm{obs}}^2 = 2t\)
in radiation FRW;
(ii) tautologically for matter $+$ Hubble-horizon and for
de~Sitter $+$ Hubble-horizon, where the diamond is \emph{defined} by
$\Rprop = 1/H$;
and \emph{fails by a factor of $2$} for matter $+$ PLC, where
\(\Rprop(\eta_c) = a(\eta_c)\,\eta_{\mathrm{obs}} = a_0\eta_{\mathrm{obs}}^3 = 3t\)
gives $1/\Rprop(\eta_c) = 1/(3t)$ vs.\ $H(t) = 2/(3t)$.

\begin{remark}[On the matter $+$ PLC factor of $1/2$]\label{rem:matter-half}
The discrepancy in the matter $+$ PLC row is a feature of the
diamond convention, not of the algebra. Different conventions for
$\Rconf$ as a function of $\eta_i, \eta_f$ give different values of
$\Rprop$, hence of the right-hand side of~\eqref{eq:KDC}. The
\emph{rate} on the algebraic side (the left-hand side) is
unambiguously $1/\Rprop(\eta_c)$ for the chosen diamond; the
\emph{rate} on the geometric side, $H(t)$, is intrinsic to the
background but not to the diamond. There is no contradiction; the
identity \eqref{eq:KDC} is not the equation $H(t) = $ Krylov rate,
which is the era-and-diamond-conditional corollary discussed above.
\end{remark}

\section{Comparison with CMPT and the modular Lyapunov \texorpdfstring{$2\pi$}{2pi}}\label{sec:cmpt}

CMPT~\cite{CMPT24} establish, on the type~III$_1$ algebra of a
two-dimensional CFT in a vacuum-like state, the universal late-modular-time
slope
\begin{equation}\label{eq:cmpt-2pi}
\frac{1}{C_k(s)}\,\frac{\mathrm{d}C_k(s)}{\mathrm{d}s} \;=\; \lambda_L^{\mathrm{mod}} \;=\; 2\pi,
\end{equation}
where $s$ is the Tomita--Takesaki modular parameter intrinsic to the
algebra (the parameter of $\Delta^{is}$). The constant $2\pi$ is the
modular analogue of the universal upper bound on operator
growth~\cite{ParkerEtAl2019} and is independent of any
background-spacetime time coordinate.

The present Theorem~\ref{thm:KDC} converts~\eqref{eq:cmpt-2pi} into a
statement about cosmic time $t$ along the comoving worldline of the
diamond's centre. The conversion is the Casini--Huerta--Myers
relation~\eqref{eq:CHM} between modular and proper time at the
centre of a spacelike-symmetric diamond. The factor $2\pi$ in
\eqref{eq:cmpt-2pi} cancels the factor $2\pi$ in~\eqref{eq:CHM},
leaving the clean geometric statement~\eqref{eq:KDC}.
The Krylov complexity in~\eqref{eq:KDC} is on the type~II$_\infty$
crossed product $\NN$, not on the type~III$_1$ subalgebra
$\AA(D_O)_\FRW$; the Block~A definition of Section~\ref{sec:blockA}
ensures that the late-modular-time slope is inherited.

The closest published precedent for a Krylov construction on a
type~II algebra with cosmological motivation is
Aguilar-Guti\'errez~\cite{AguilarGutierrez2025}, where the
type~II$_1$ DSSYK Krylov complexity is computed on the boundary
Hartle--Hawking seed. Theorem~\ref{thm:KDC} is the type~II$_\infty$
extension of that programme adapted to the FRW geometric setting and
specialised to the spread complexity at the diamond centre.

\section{Open problems}\label{sec:open}

\begin{question}[II$_\infty$-intrinsic proof of $\lambda_L^{\mathrm{mod}}=2\pi$]\label{q:lambda}
Prove the universal operator-growth hypothesis on type~II$_\infty$
KMS states with respect to the canonical
trace~$\mathrm{tr}_\NN$, in particular establishing $b_n = O(n)$ on
$L^2(\NN, \mathrm{tr}_\NN)$ for the lifted modular Hamiltonian
$K_\NN$. This would close the gap noted in
Remark~\ref{rem:blockA-status}: the slope $\lambda_L^{\mathrm{mod}}=2\pi$
in Theorem~\ref{thm:KDC} is currently inherited from the type~III$_1$
subalgebra via a conditional-expectation argument; a direct
II$_\infty$ proof would also clarify whether the constant $2\pi$ is
robust under the addition of a non-trivial cocycle (e.g.\ a small
mass perturbation).
\end{question}

\begin{question}[Diamond-convention-independent geometric invariants]\label{q:diamond}
The right-hand side of~\eqref{eq:KDC} depends on $\Rprop(\eta_c)$,
hence on the choice of diamond. Are there \emph{diamond-convention-independent}
combinations of $C_k$ and its derivatives that recover purely
intrinsic FRW invariants (e.g.\ $H(t)$, $\dot H$, $\dot H/H^2$) as
geometric--algebraic identities? A natural candidate would involve
the running of $\Rprop$ across a one-parameter family of nested
diamonds.
\end{question}

\begin{question}[Big Bang regime, $\eta_i \to 0^+$]\label{q:bigbang}
The companion note~\cite{Remondiere2026} (open-questions section,
``Big Bang -- algebraic structure at $\eta_i=0$'') flags three
obstructions to the conformal-pullback construction in $4$D as
$\eta_i\to 0^+$:
(a) failure of the conformal-rescaling bijection
$f\mapsto a^3 f$ at the Big Bang, (b) IR singularity of the
conformal-vacuum two-point function, (c) non-Hadamard boundary
behaviour. In $2$D all three vanish (as verified by the
\texttt{eta\_zero\_2d\_opus.py} script of~\cite{Remondiere2026}); in
$4$D none are addressed by the present argument. The PLC diamond of
the radiation row in Section~\ref{sec:eras} is a borderline case
which heuristically extends to $\eta_i\to 0^+$ but which the present
proof does not establish.
\end{question}

\begin{question}[Beyond the leading order, and non-PLC diamonds]\label{q:allorders}
$1/\Rprop(\eta_c)$ in~\eqref{eq:KDC} is the leading
late-modular-time slope; the $O(C_k^{-1})$ correction is
seed-dependent. An all-orders expansion of $C_k(t)$ in the cosmic
time variable, in terms of geometric invariants of the diamond and
its central worldline, would give a genuine cosmological
observable. A related question: for an arbitrary spacelike-symmetric
diamond inside $D_O$, under what intrinsic conditions on the
diamond does the ratio $1/(\Rprop(\eta_c)\,H(t))$ reduce to a clean
closed form (e.g.\ a function of the equation-of-state parameter
$w$ alone)?
\end{question}
