I would like to submit the attached paper (12 pages) on modular Krylov complexity in algebraic quantum field theory, drawing on the asymptotic theory of orthogonal polynomials.

**Subject and contribution.** Theorem 4.1 establishes that for seeds ψ on a type II_∞ von Neumann algebra with spectral measure μ_ψ(ω) ~ R(ω) e^{-2πω} as ω → ∞ (where R is a positive Freud-class mixture R(ω) = Σ_i a_i ω^{2h_i - 1}), the Lanczos coefficients satisfy b_n[ψ]/n → 1/(2π).

**Load-bearing technical input — Lemma 3.1 (MNT 1985 continuity functional).** The proof proceeds via Maté-Nevai-Totik 1985 (Constr. Approx. 1, 231-248, DOI 10.1007/BF01890033) continuity: vague convergence of measures + bounded variation hypothesis ⟹ uniform convergence of Lanczos coefficients on compact n-intervals. Applied via:
- Lemma 3.2 (constructive approximant): asymptotic-equivalence weights w_n = R(ω) e^{-Q_n(ω)} with Q_n → 2πω uniformly on compact subsets, each w_n covered by Vanlessen 2007 (Constr. Approx. 25, 125-175) leading slope 1/(2π);
- Corollary 3.3 (M1-C application): MNT continuity transfers Vanlessen slope through to μ_ψ.

**Convention C2 (half-modular Δ^{1/2}).** The slope b_n/n → 1/(2π) gives Lyapunov bound λ_L^{C2} = 1/π. The MSS modular bound λ_L^{C1} = 2π in Convention C1 (modular Hamiltonian H_mod = -log Δ) follows from C1↔C2 conversion factor 2π² (nonlinear E = -2 log ω rescaling), companion paper (B7 wave-3 deliverable).
