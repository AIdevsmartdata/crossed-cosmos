Dear Editor,

I would like to submit the attached short note for consideration as a Note (or Comment) in J. Stat. Phys.

**Summary.** The Belinski-Khalatnikov-Lifshitz (BKL) era-return map on Mixmaster cosmology is conjugate to the Gauss continued-fraction map T(x) = {1/x} via x = 1/u. This is well-known. The pushforward Gauss measure dν(x) = dx/((1+x) ln 2) becomes dμ_HU(u) = du/(u(u+1) ln 2) on u ∈ (1, ∞), the unique absolutely continuous invariant measure of the BKL era-return map.

The attached note shows in five elementary lines that

  ⟨p_3⟩_{μ_HU} = ∫_1^∞ p_3(u) du/(u(u+1) ln 2) = √3π/(9 ln 2) ≈ 0.872253116

(closed form for the time-averaged dominant Kasner exponent at era-start). The proof uses completing the square in 1+u+u² and the standard arctan integral. We also provide closed forms for ⟨p_1⟩ and ⟨p_2⟩ verifying ⟨p_1⟩ + ⟨p_2⟩ + ⟨p_3⟩ = 1 to 200 digits at mpmath precision.

The note also provides a remark distinguishing the Heinzle-Uggla era-return measure (this work) from the BKL-step-weighted measure (Khinchin-divergent denominator), to prevent a common conflation of measures that we have observed in numerical work.

**Length and scope.** 3 pages of LaTeX. Includes 8 references; the key Khalatnikov-Lifshitz-Khanin-Shchur-Sinai 1985 J. Stat. Phys. paper [doi:10.1007/BF01017851] published in this journal is cited as the foundational era-statistics reference. The note is a clean closed-form companion result; it does not extend the underlying ergodic theory.

**Reproducibility.** A short Python/sympy/mpmath script is attached. All three closed forms agree with direct numerical evaluation to absolute error 0 at 200-digit precision.
