**Subject and contribution.** The paper establishes a conditional spectral type equivalence between the modular flow generator on the type II_∞ crossed-product algebra of the BFV folium of Bianchi IX cosmology, and the Euler-restricted Connes-Consani-Moscovici (CCM) zeta spectral triple operator on L²([λ⁻¹, λ], du/u). The key algebraic input is the Hartnoll-Yang 2025 (arXiv:2502.02661, JHEP) BKL automorphic dilation operator D_HY = i(x ∂_x + Δ) and the CCM 2025 (arXiv:2511.22755) zeta spectral triple D_CCM = -i u ∂_u, which we show are unitarily equivalent (both have continuous spectrum ℝ via Mellin/Plancherel, isomorphic as type-I von Neumann algebra generators).

**Framing as spectral type equivalence (NOT pointwise identity).** A previous attempt to identify D_HY = -D_CCM + iΔ · id pointwise under u = e^x is incorrect (sympy verifies the difference is i(x-1)∂_x, vanishing only at x=1). The correct framing — establishing only spectral type equivalence at the algebra-level — is what the paper demonstrates. We are explicit about this distinction in §6 to prevent any over-interpretation as a Riemann Hypothesis claim.

**Conditional theorem.** The spectral correspondence is established conditional on:
- H1: Bianchi IX BKL-pathwise Hadamard quasifree state existence on full Lebesgue measure trajectories (open research program, ~2-4 mo separate paper extending Banerjee-Niedermaier 2023 to S³ topology + Mixmaster chaos).
- H2: CCM-BFV-Eisenstein conformal pullback identifying CCM Euler product cutoff p ≤ λ² with BFV folium coarse-graining scale (no published framework; the deepest gap, ~6 mo work).
