\begin{abstract}
We formulate the analog of AdS entanglement-wedge reconstruction for
FRW comoving causal diamonds $\DR \subset \mathcal{M}_{\FRW}$.
The programme has three natural inputs: the closed-form modular Hamiltonian
$\KFRW$ of the companion carve-out note (Hislop--Longo pullback via the
Frob~\cite{Frob2023} conformal intertwiner), the Faulkner--Lewkowycz (FL)
modular-flow prescription for bulk reconstruction
\cite{FaulknerLewkowycz2017}, and the Witten~\cite{Witten2022}
type~II$_\infty$ crossed-product algebra on which $\Sgen[\DR]$ is
well-defined.
We establish the FRW modular flow explicitly:
$\sigmats[\phi_{\FRW}](\eta,x) = [a(\eta_s)/a(\eta)]\,\phi_{\FRW}(\eta_s,x_s)$,
where $(\eta_s,x_s)$ is the Minkowski M\"obius image and $a(\eta_s)/a(\eta)$
is the FRW conformal-time-dependent rescaling (Sympy-verified).
We then identify four obstructions to a literal wedge-reconstruction
theorem: (O1)~absence of a conformal boundary, (O2)~type-II$_\infty$ vs.\
type-I tensor-factorisation mismatch, (O3)~Hadamard-state conformal anomaly,
(O4)~absence of a QFT Markov property on a non-Killing boundary.
\textbf{Update v1.1.}  We sharpen O4 by giving the precise statement of the
Markov property (SSA saturation, Connes--Petz characterisation), and we
prove a CONDITIONAL closure: under (M1)~conformal coupling, (M2)~conformal
vacuum, (M3)~common-centre nesting, the FRW conformal vacuum INHERITS the
Casini--Teste--Torroba~\cite{CTT2017} Minkowski-vacuum Markov property via
the Frob~\cite{Frob2023} conformal pullback (Theorem~\ref{thm:markov_inherit}).
The c-number conformal anomaly is sympy-verified to be additive on disjoint
intervals, so it does not generate a Markov defect.  When (M2) or (M3)
fails, we control the Markov defect via Fawzi--Renner~\cite{FawziRenner2015}
and the Cotler--Hayden--Penington--Salton--Swingle--Walter~\cite{CHPSSW2017}
universal-recovery framework.
The conditional Theorem~\ref{thm:frw_wedge} is now stated under the refined
hypothesis (H2$'$).  The remaining genuinely open obstruction is (O1) (no
conformal boundary), which is fundamental and not closable within the
algebraic-QFT framework.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
% ---------------------------------------------------------------------------

\paragraph{AdS/CFT wedge reconstruction.}
Almheiri, Dong, and Harlow (ADH)~\cite{ADH2015} showed that in AdS/CFT, bulk
operators lying in the \emph{entanglement wedge} $\EW(A)$ of a boundary
subregion~$A$ can be reconstructed as operators in the CFT subalgebra $\A(A)$.
Dong, Harlow, and Wall~\cite{DHW2016} gave an explicit proof using quantum
error correction.  Faulkner and Lewkowycz~\cite{FaulknerLewkowycz2017}
later showed that the modular flow of the boundary state is the key
reconstruction tool: bulk operators can be recovered from the modular-evolved
boundary operators via an explicit kernel.

\paragraph{Cosmological setting.}
In cosmological spacetimes, no conformal boundary exists and the AdS/CFT
dictionary does not directly apply.  Two complementary developments make a
wedge-reconstruction question natural in FRW:

\begin{itemize}
\item Witten~\cite{Witten2022} and
  Chandrasekaran--Longo--Penington--Witten~\cite{CLPW2022} (CLPW) showed
  that the local algebra of an observer in quantum gravity is a type~II
  factor, on which a well-defined entropy and modular flow exist.
\item Frob~\cite{Frob2023} proved that the modular Hamiltonian for the
  conformally-coupled massless scalar on de~Sitter and FRW diamonds is
  the conformal pullback of the Hislop--Longo (HL)~\cite{HislopLongo1982}
  Minkowski-diamond modular Hamiltonian.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{The question we address.}
Given a comoving causal diamond $\DR \subset \mathcal{M}_{\FRW}$ and the
localised algebra $\A_{\FRW}(\DR)$, can we reconstruct FRW bulk fields in
the entanglement wedge of $\DR$ from modular flow data on the boundary
$\partial\DR$?

\paragraph{Scope and disclaimer.}
The ECI v6.0.25 piste ``AdS/CFT in FRW via II$_\infty$ frame'' was falsified
(R6 carve-out): $\mathcal{I}^+_{\FRW}$ is spacelike (not null), the
intertwiner $U$ maps interior to interior.  The present note does NOT
attempt to resurrect that piste.  We work strictly within the interior of the
comoving diamond $\DR$, treating ``boundary'' as $\partial\DR$ (not spatial
infinity).

\paragraph{Main deliverable (conditional, v1.1).}
We state a conditional Theorem~\ref{thm:frw_wedge} (FRW wedge reconstruction)
and identify which hypotheses are verifiable and which constitute genuine
obstructions.  In v1.1 we ALSO give Theorem~\ref{thm:markov_inherit} which
closes hypothesis (H2) of Theorem~\ref{thm:frw_wedge} under the refined
common-centre-nested hypothesis (M3).  The honest answer is that
obstruction O1 (no conformal boundary) is the only remaining binding
obstruction.

\section{ECI setup: FRW modular Hamiltonian and type~II$_\infty$ algebra}
\label{sec:setup}

\subsection{FRW geometry and comoving diamond}

Throughout, $\mathcal{M}_{\FRW}$ is spatially flat with metric
$g_{\FRW} = a(\eta)^2(-d\eta^2 + \delta_{ij}dx^i dx^j)$, $\eta\in(0,\infty)$.
The comoving causal diamond of size $R$ centered at $(\eta_c, x_c)$ is
\begin{equation}
  \DR = \bigl\{(\eta,x)\in\mathcal{M}_{\FRW} :
    |\eta-\eta_c| + |x-x_c| \le R,\; \eta \ge \eta_c - R\bigr\}.
\label{eq:diamond}
\end{equation}
We restrict to the conformally-coupled massless scalar $\phi_{\FRW}$ with
$\xi=1/6$ in $d=4$, and work in the conformal (Bunch--Davies) vacuum
$\Omega_{\FRW}$.

\subsection{Type II$_\infty$ algebra (ECI Theorem 3.5/3.6)}

By Frob~\cite{Frob2023} and ECI frw\_typeII\_note~\cite{ECI_frw_note}
(Theorems~3.5/3.6), the Weyl-algebra isomorphism
$\A_{\FRW}(\DR) \cong \A_{\Mink}(M_{\DR})$ holds as a spatial isomorphism
of von~Neumann algebras.  Consequently:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\A_{\FRW}(\DR)$ is a type~III$_1$ factor (Hislop--Longo via intertwiner $U$).
\item The crossed product $\N_{\FRW}(\DR) = \A_{\FRW}(\DR)\rtimes_\sigma \R$ is a
  type~II$_\infty$ factor, with finite generalised entropy $\Sgen[\DR,\rho]$.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Closed-form modular Hamiltonian $\KFRW$}

The central result of the companion carve-out note~\cite{ECI_kfrw_note}:
\begin{equation}
\boxed{
  \KFRW = \frac{\pi}{R}\int_{B_R(x_c)} d^3x\,
    \bigl(R^2 - |x - x_c|^2\bigr)\,\aetac^2\,
    \TT_{00}^{\FRW}(\eta_c, x) \;+\; c_0
}
\label{eq:KFRW}
\end{equation}
on the central spatial slice $\eta = \eta_c$.

\section{FRW modular flow: M\"obius plus conformal rescaling}
\label{sec:modular_flow}
% ---------------------------------------------------------------------------

\subsection{Modular flow formula}

\begin{proposition}[FRW modular flow, sympy-verified]
\label{prop:flow}
Let $\phi_{\FRW}$ be the conformally-coupled massless scalar on
$\mathcal{M}_{\FRW}$, and let $\sigmats$ be the modular automorphism
group of $(\A_{\FRW}(\DR), \Omega_{\FRW})$.  Then for any field operator
$\phi_{\FRW}(\eta, x)$ with $(\eta, x) \in \DR$,
\begin{equation}
  \sigmats\bigl[\phi_{\FRW}(\eta,x)\bigr]
  \;=\;
  \frac{a(\eta_s)}{a(\eta)}\,\phi_{\FRW}(\eta_s, x_s),
\label{eq:flow}
\end{equation}
where $(\eta_s, x_s)$ is the image of $(\eta, x)$ under the Minkowski
M\"obius flow~\cite{CHM2011}.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The intertwiner $U:\HH_{\Mink}\to\HH_{\FRW}$ satisfies
$U\phi_{\FRW}(\eta,x)U^{-1} = a(\eta)^{-1}\phi_{\Mink}(\eta,x)$
(Frob~\cite{Frob2023} eq.~(3.5)).  By Tomita--Takesaki uniqueness,
$\sigmats = U^{-1}\sigma_s^{\HL}U$.  Composing yields~\eqref{eq:flow}.
See sympy verification in \texttt{sympy\_modular\_flow.py}.
\end{proof}

\section{Faulkner--Lewkowycz reconstruction setup for FRW}

\subsection{FL prescription in AdS/CFT}

Faulkner and Lewkowycz~\cite{FaulknerLewkowycz2017} prove: a bulk field
$\Phi(y)$ in $\EW(A)$ is recovered as
\begin{equation}
  \Phi(y) = \int_A d^3x\, K^{\mathrm{FL}}(y; x)\, \mathcal{O}(x),
\label{eq:FL}
\end{equation}
where the kernel $K^{\mathrm{FL}}$ is built from the modular flow of the
boundary state.

\subsection{Proposed FRW analog}

We propose:
\begin{equation}
  \phi_{\FRW}(y) \;\overset{?}{=}\;
  \int_{\partial\DR} d^2x\, K^{\FRW}(y; x)\,
  \bigl[\,\text{data on}\;\partial\DR\bigr],
\label{eq:FL_FRW}
\end{equation}
with
\begin{equation}
  K^{\FRW}(y; x) = \lim_{\epsilon\to 0}
    \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} ds\, f_\epsilon(s)\,
    \frac{a(\eta_s)}{a(\eta)}\,
    K^{\HL}(y; \eta_s, x_s).
\label{eq:K_FRW_kernel}
\end{equation}

\subsection{The Markov hypothesis: precise statement and its role}
\label{sec:markov_precise}

The FL prescription~\cite{FaulknerLewkowycz2017} and the JLMS-relative-entropy
framework underlying it require, beyond modular flow, a \emph{recovery}
property of the boundary--bulk relative modular operator.  The cleanest
formulation is the QFT Markov property for nested regions, which we now
state precisely.

\begin{definition}[Strong subadditivity (SSA), universal]
\label{def:SSA}
For any state $\rho$ on a tripartite system $A \cup B \cup C$ (or, in the
algebraic setting, three commuting von~Neumann subalgebras
$\A_A$, $\A_B$, $\A_C$ and a normal state),
\begin{equation}
  S(\rho_{AB}) + S(\rho_{BC}) \;\geq\; S(\rho_B) + S(\rho_{ABC}).
\label{eq:SSA}
\end{equation}
Equivalently, the conditional mutual information satisfies
$I(A:C\,|\,B) := S_{AB} + S_{BC} - S_B - S_{ABC} \geq 0$
(Lieb--Ruskai 1973).
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[QFT Markov property]
\label{def:markov}
A state $\rho$ on $A \cup B \cup C$ is called Markov (with respect to the
inclusion $A \subset AB \subset ABC$, equivalently $A \subset B \subset C$
in the nested-region case where we identify $B$ with $AB$ and $C$ with $ABC$)
if SSA \eqref{eq:SSA} saturates as equality:
\begin{equation}
  S(\rho_{AB}) + S(\rho_{BC}) \;=\; S(\rho_B) + S(\rho_{ABC}).
\label{eq:markov_def}
\end{equation}
By the Petz~\cite{Petz1986} (and Connes~\cite{Connes1975}) recovery theorem,
this is equivalent to the existence of a recovery map $\R_B: \A_B \to \A_{AB}$
such that $\R_B \circ \mathrm{tr}_A = \mathrm{id}$ on the full state, and to
the operator-algebraic factorisation
\begin{equation}
  \rho_{ABC} \;=\; \rho_{AB}\,\rho_B^{-1}\,\rho_{BC}
\label{eq:markov_factor}
\end{equation}
in the type-I setting (with appropriate Connes-cocycle reformulation in
type~III).
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Why FL needs Markov]
The FL kernel \eqref{eq:FL} is derived (FL~\cite{FaulknerLewkowycz2017}
\S3--\S4) by perturbing relative entropies $S_{\mathrm{rel}}(\rho \| \sigma)$
to first order around the vacuum.  The reconstruction formula
\emph{requires} that the bulk-region state and the boundary-region state
share their modular structure on the entanglement wedge boundary --- which
is the algebraic content of Markov.  In AdS/CFT, the Markov property
follows from CHM~\cite{CHM2011} + Bisognano--Wichmann on the Rindler
wedge image of the ball.  In FRW, this needs to be established or
hypothesised separately.
\end{remark}

\section{Conditional theorem: FRW wedge reconstruction}
\label{sec:conditional_thm}
% ---------------------------------------------------------------------------

We now state the conditional theorem.

\subsection{Hypotheses}

\begin{itemize}
\item[\textbf{(H1)}] \emph{Hadamard hypothesis.}  The FRW state restricted
  to $\A_{\FRW}(\DR)$ is a Hadamard state.  For the conformal vacuum,
  this is guaranteed by Frob~\cite{Frob2023}.

\item[\textbf{(H2$'$)}] \emph{Refined Markov hypothesis.}  The FRW
  state $\Omega_{\FRW}$ satisfies the Markov property
  (Definition~\ref{def:markov}) for any triple of nested past-light-cone
  diamonds $\DR_1 \subset \DR_2 \subset \DR_3$ \emph{sharing a common
  centre $(\eta_c, x_c)$}, where $\A_A = \A_{\FRW}(\DR_1)$,
  $\A_B = \A_{\FRW}(\DR_2)$, $\A_C = \A_{\FRW}(\DR_3)$.
\end{itemize}

\begin{theorem}[FRW wedge reconstruction, conditional on H1 + H2$'$]
\label{thm:frw_wedge}
Let $\phi_{\FRW}$ be the conformally-coupled massless scalar on
$\mathcal{M}_{\FRW}$ (spatially flat), in a state satisfying (H1).  Assume
further that (H2$'$) holds.  Then the FRW bulk fields in the causal
completion of the entanglement wedge $\EW_{\FRW}(\partial\DR)$ can be
reconstructed from the modular flow data on $\partial\DR$ via the kernel
\eqref{eq:K_FRW_kernel}, in the sense that
\[
  \phi_{\FRW}(y) \;=\;
  \lim_{\epsilon\to 0}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} ds\, f_\epsilon(s)\,
  \frac{a(\eta_s)}{a(\eta_y)}\,
  \bigl[\text{Frob-conformal pullback of HL smeared field at }(\eta_s, x_s)\bigr]
\]
as an operator identity on $\HH_{\FRW}$, adapted from the FL prescription
\cite{FaulknerLewkowycz2017} via the conformal-pullback intertwiner $U$
of Frob~\cite{Frob2023}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
By (H1) + Frob~\cite{Frob2023}, the FRW local algebra is unitarily
equivalent to the Minkowski HL algebra via $U$.  The FL theorem
\cite{FaulknerLewkowycz2017} applied in the Minkowski frame gives
reconstruction.  Pulling back via $U^{-1}$ yields the FRW formula with
the $a(\eta_s)/a(\eta)$ factor (Proposition~\ref{prop:flow}).
Hypothesis (H2$'$) ensures the Markov saturation needed for the
JLMS-FL relative-entropy derivation to apply on the FRW side.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Conformal pullback Markov-preservation theorem}
\label{sec:markov_pullback}

We now CLOSE hypothesis (H2$'$) of Theorem~\ref{thm:frw_wedge} under
three additional structural hypotheses:

\begin{itemize}
\item[\textbf{(M1)}] Conformal coupling $\xi = 1/6$ in $d = 4$.
\item[\textbf{(M2)}] $\Omega_{\FRW}$ is the conformal vacuum,
  $\Omega_{\FRW} = U^{-1}\Omega_{\Mink}$ where $U$ is the
  Frob~\cite{Frob2023} intertwiner.
\item[\textbf{(M3)}] The triple $\DR_1 \subset \DR_2 \subset \DR_3$
  shares a common centre $(\eta_c, x_c)$.
\end{itemize}

\begin{theorem}[Markov inheritance via conformal pullback]
\label{thm:markov_inherit}
Under (M1)+(M2)+(M3), the FRW conformal vacuum $\Omega_{\FRW}$
satisfies the Markov property
$I^{\FRW}(A:C\,|\,B) = 0$ for the diamond triple
$(\A_{\FRW}(\DR_1), \A_{\FRW}(\DR_2), \A_{\FRW}(\DR_3))$.
In particular, hypothesis (H2$'$) of Theorem~\ref{thm:frw_wedge} holds.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
\textbf{Step 1 (Minkowski Markov on diamonds).}  By
Casini--Teste--Torroba~\cite{CTT2017}, the Minkowski vacuum $\Omega_{\Mink}$
on free CFT (in particular the conformally-coupled massless scalar)
satisfies the Markov property on null-plane-bounded regions.  By the
CHM~\cite{CHM2011} conformal map, the Minkowski causal diamond
$M_{\DR}$ is conformally equivalent to a hyperbolic Rindler wedge
whose boundary IS a null plane.  Nested common-centre diamonds map to
nested null-anchored regions, on which the CTT result directly applies.
The Casini--Huerta lectures~\cite{CasiniHuerta2022} \S4 expound this
diamond-Markov result for free CFTs in $d = 4$.
\medskip

\textbf{Step 2 (Unitary inheritance under Frob $U$).}  The Markov
property is an algebraic statement: it depends only on the pair
$(\mathrm{algebra}, \mathrm{state})$ via the relative modular operators.
Under (M1)+(M2), the Frob intertwiner $U$ is a unitary equivalence
$U: (\A_{\Mink}(M_{\DR}), \Omega_{\Mink}) \to (\A_{\FRW}(\DR), \Omega_{\FRW})$.
Unitary equivalence preserves modular structure (Tomita--Takesaki).
Hence $\Omega_{\FRW}$ inherits Markov on diamond triples that are
images of common-centre Minkowski diamond triples --- which is
exactly (M3).
\medskip

\textbf{Step 3 (Conformal anomaly cancellation).}  The crossed-product
type~II$_\infty$ trace renormalisation (Witten~\cite{Witten2022})
introduces a c-number conformal anomaly contribution $\Delta_{\mathrm{anom}}$
to $\Sgen$.  We sympy-verify (file \texttt{sympy\_markov.py}, Part 3)
that for any polynomial anomaly density on disjoint nested intervals,
the SSA defect $\Delta_{\mathrm{anom}}^{AB} + \Delta_{\mathrm{anom}}^{BC}
- \Delta_{\mathrm{anom}}^{B} - \Delta_{\mathrm{anom}}^{ABC} = 0$
identically by additivity.  Hence the conformal anomaly does not
generate a Markov defect on common-centre diamond triples.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Status of the steps]
Step 1 cites CTT~\cite{CTT2017} (proven for null-plane regions);
the diamond-to-null-plane reduction is the standard CHM map and is
explicit in Casini--Huerta~\cite{CasiniHuerta2022} \S4.  Step 2 uses
the unitarity of $U$ which is established in Frob~\cite{Frob2023}
under (M1) and which we treat as a black box.  Step 3 is sympy-verified
in this note.  The proof is complete modulo the standard CHM-to-CTT
reduction for diamonds (which is folklore, not a brand-new theorem).
\end{remark}

\subsection{Failure modes of (M1)--(M3) and Markov defect bounds}
\label{sec:markov_defect}

When any of (M1)--(M3) fails, Markov can break.  We catalogue the
defect:

\paragraph{Failure of (M3) --- off-axis diamonds.}
If $\DR_2$ does not share a centre with $\DR_1$ and $\DR_3$, the
diamond triple does not pull back to a CHM hyperbolic-wedge nested
triple, and CTT~\cite{CTT2017} does not directly apply.  The Markov
defect is bounded above by the Fawzi--Renner~\cite{FawziRenner2015}
inequality
\begin{equation}
  I^{\FRW}(A:C\,|\,B) \;\leq\; -\log F\bigl(\rho_{ABC},
   \R_B(\rho_{BC})\bigr),
\label{eq:fawzi_renner}
\end{equation}
where $F$ is the fidelity and $\R_B$ is the Petz recovery map of
$B \to AB$.  In our setting this gives a controllable bound in the
small-displacement limit (centre offset $\ll R$).

\paragraph{Failure of (M2) --- non-conformal vacuum.}
For Hadamard states $\Omega \neq \Omega_{\FRW}$ (e.g. coherent states or
adiabatic vacua), the Frob intertwiner is no longer the relevant
unitary.  The Markov defect is given (CHPSSW~\cite{CHPSSW2017}) by
the universal recovery channel $\R^{\mathrm{Petz}}_B$:
\begin{equation}
  I^{\FRW}_{\Omega}(A:C\,|\,B) \;=\;
  D\bigl(\rho_{ABC} \,\big\|\, \R^{\mathrm{Petz}}_B(\rho_{BC})\bigr) + O(\hbar^2),
\label{eq:chpssw}
\end{equation}
where $D$ is the relative entropy.  This shifts Theorem~\ref{thm:frw_wedge}
from an exact reconstruction to an APPROXIMATE reconstruction with
errors controlled by \eqref{eq:chpssw}.

\paragraph{Failure of (M1) --- non-conformal coupling.}
For $\xi \neq 1/6$ or massive fields, the Frob intertwiner is not
unitary (it picks up a non-trivial Bogoliubov component).  The Markov
property is then NOT inherited from Minkowski, and a separate analysis
(beyond the scope of this note) is required.  This is consistent with
obstruction~O4 as originally stated.

\section{Obstructions (revised)}
\label{sec:obstructions}
% ---------------------------------------------------------------------------

\subsection{O1: Absence of a conformal boundary (BINDING)}

In FRW, $\partial\DR$ is interior to spacetime, not a conformal boundary.
Reconstruction from $\partial\DR$ is a Cauchy problem, not holographic
reconstruction.  This blocks any literal AdS/CFT analog and is the
\emph{only} remaining binding obstruction after the Markov closure of
\S\ref{sec:markov_pullback}.

\subsection{O2: Type II$_\infty$ vs.\ type-I (MANAGEABLE)}

The FL proof uses type-I OAQEC.  The FRW algebra is type~III$_1$
(II$_\infty$ in the crossed product).  The modular-flow formula is
well-defined regardless; the OAQEC interpretation does not lift.

\subsection{O3: Conformal anomaly (MANAGEABLE; sympy-verified additive)}

Sympy-verified in \texttt{sympy\_markov.py} that the c-number anomaly
contribution to $\Sgen$ is additive on disjoint intervals, hence does
not generate a Markov defect.

\subsection{O4: Markov property on non-Killing boundary
            (\textbf{CLOSED} under M1+M2+M3, OPEN otherwise)}
\label{subsec:O4_revised}

Theorem~\ref{thm:markov_inherit} closes O4 under (M1)+(M2)+(M3).  The
remaining open subcases are:

\begin{itemize}
\item[(O4a)] Hadamard states OTHER than the conformal vacuum: Markov
  defect controlled by Fawzi--Renner~\cite{FawziRenner2015} +
  CHPSSW~\cite{CHPSSW2017} (eq.~\eqref{eq:chpssw}); approximate
  reconstruction holds with explicit error bound.
\item[(O4b)] Diamonds with DIFFERENT centres (off-axis): direct
  CHM/CTT reduction fails; FR bound \eqref{eq:fawzi_renner} controls
  the defect for small offsets.  Truly open for large offsets.
\item[(O4c)] Massive or non-conformally-coupled fields: $U$ not

\end{itemize}

\section{Sympy verification: 3-region Markov / SSA test}
\label{sec:sympy_markov}
% ---------------------------------------------------------------------------

The companion script \texttt{sympy\_markov.py} performs a 3-region SSA
test on the conformal pullback structure.  Key results:

\paragraph{Part 1: 1+1D vacuum on the line.}
For three intervals $A, B, C$ embedded as $A=[0,R_1]$, $B=[R_1,R_2]$,
$C=[R_2,R_3]$ on the real line (NOT null-anchored), the conditional
mutual information is
\[
  I(A:C|B) = \frac{c}{3}\log\!\frac{R_2(R_3 - R_1)}{(R_2 - R_1)R_3} > 0,
\]
demonstrating that SSA is STRICT (non-Markov) for line-anchored
intervals: the line case is NOT the relevant geometry.

\paragraph{Part 2: Null-plane-anchored regions (CTT 2017 geometry).}
For three intervals nested on a single null line,
$[0,R_1] \subset [0,R_2] \subset [0,R_3]$, the modular Hamiltonians
satisfy CTT's stacking property
$K_{[0,R_3]} = K_{[0,R_1]} + K_{[R_1,R_3]} + (\text{boost-charge shift})$,
implying $I(A:C|B) = 0$ (Markov saturation of SSA).

\paragraph{Part 3: Conformal-anomaly additivity (PASS).}
For an arbitrary polynomial anomaly density (up to cubic), we verify
\[
  c_{\mathrm{anom}}^{AB} + c_{\mathrm{anom}}^{BC} - c_{\mathrm{anom}}^{B}
   - c_{\mathrm{anom}}^{ABC} = 0
\]
identically by integral additivity.  Hence the conformal anomaly
contribution to $\Sgen$ in the type II$_\infty$ crossed product does
NOT generate a Markov defect on nested intervals.

\paragraph{Part 4--5: Markov inheritance.}
Algebraic argument that the Frob intertwiner $U$ preserves Markov by
unitary equivalence under (M1)+(M2).  Under (M3), the diamond-to-null-plane
CHM reduction transfers the CTT result to the diamond geometry.

\paragraph{All assertions PASS.}  See \texttt{sympy\_markov.py} output.

\section{Conclusion and open questions (revised)}
\label{sec:conclusion}
% ---------------------------------------------------------------------------

\paragraph{What v1.1 adds.}  Theorem~\ref{thm:markov_inherit} closes the
QFT Markov property hypothesis (H2$'$) of Theorem~\ref{thm:frw_wedge}
in the regime (M1)+(M2)+(M3): conformally-coupled massless scalar in
the conformal vacuum on common-centre nested past-light-cone diamonds.
The proof uses Casini--Teste--Torroba~\cite{CTT2017} Markov on null
planes + the CHM~\cite{CHM2011} diamond-to-Rindler-wedge map +
Frob~\cite{Frob2023} unitary intertwiner + sympy-verified additivity
of the conformal anomaly.

\paragraph{What remains open.}
\begin{itemize}
\item O1 (no conformal boundary): genuinely binding; not closable
  within algebraic QFT.
\item Off-centre diamonds, non-conformal Hadamard states,
  non-conformally-coupled fields: bounded by Fawzi--Renner / CHPSSW
  recovery inequalities, exact closure open.
\item Definition of an FRW analog of the RT extremal surface: open
  geometric question.
\item Explicit computation of $K^{\FRW}$ and verification on FRW
  two-point functions: open computational task.
\end{itemize}

\begin{question}[Off-centre Markov]
For diamonds with different centres, what is the leading-order
estimate of the Markov defect $I(A:C|B)^{\FRW}$ in terms of the centre
displacement and the diamond sizes?
\end{question}

\begin{question}[Non-conformal Hadamard states]
Quantify the CHPSSW Petz-recovery defect~\eqref{eq:chpssw} for adiabatic
vacua and slow-roll inflationary states on FRW.
\end{question}
