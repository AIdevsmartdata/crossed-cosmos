Title: Modular flow generates generalized entropy in type-II_inf crossed-product gravity:
       an Araki-cocycle derivation, with a no-go for HPS-DEHK Stinespring intertwining

Authors: Kévin Remondière (Independent researcher, Tarbes, France)

Abstract: (use verbatim abstract from erepr_araki_consistency_LMP.tex)
Working in the type-II_inf crossed-product algebra attached to a static-patch observer
(CLPW + de Vuyst-Eccles-Höhn-Kirklin), we use the Araki relative modular operator and
its cocycle identity to derive the algebraic identity d/dtau_R S_gen = <K_R>_rho, where
K_R is the dressed modular Hamiltonian. The derivation is rigorous in the type-II_inf
setting via Pusz-Woronowicz applied to nsf weights. A no-go theorem is established for
a Stinespring intertwining between the Haag-dual algebra and the DEHK chord algebra:
the Krylov spectra are non-isometric as measured operators. The surviving bridge is a
thermodynamic identity log Z_chord = log Z_DEHK + O(1/N), reproducing the Bekenstein-
Hawking dS entropy. Scope: AdS/CFT static patch only; extension to dynamical spacetimes
remains open (Vardian 2026 arXiv:2602.02675 covers the dS Krylov side).
