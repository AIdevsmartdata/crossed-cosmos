Title: A modular Lyapunov bound for finite-rank type-II_inf crossed-product algebras
       (with saturation conjecture and analog-Hawking falsifier; v2.5)

Authors: Kévin Remondière (Independent researcher, Tarbes, France)

Abstract: (use verbatim abstract from modular_shadow_LMP_v2.5.tex)
Let M_R ⊗ B(H_n) be a finite-rank truncation of the type-II_inf crossed-product
von Neumann algebra associated with an observer's causal wedge at Bisognano-Wichmann
KMS inverse temperature beta=2pi. We prove that the modular Krylov (Lanczos) complexity
C_K^mod(s) satisfies the bound lambda_K^mod <= 2pi/beta = 1, saturating the modular
chaos bound. The proof is a three-step construction: (i) Lanczos cosh upper bound,
(ii) Källén-Lehmann spectral decomposition for nsf weights, (iii) inverse-Lanczos
UOGH argument. A saturation conjecture (Krylov = 2pi T) is proposed for maximally
chaotic type-III_1 sub-algebras, with falsifier: DSSYK chord-to-operator complexity
ratio at finite N. An extension to BEC analog-gravity systems is given in §6, assuming
the BEC exterior phonon algebra admits finite-rank type-II_inf truncation.
