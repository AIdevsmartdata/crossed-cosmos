Title: Proton-decay branching ratio from modular S'_4 SU(5) at the tau=i
       CM-anchored attractor: parameter-free prediction testable at Hyper-K/DUNE 2030+

Authors: K. Remondiere (Independent researcher, Tarbes, France)

Abstract: REVISED per M33 — must disclose B-ratio range and fine-tuning before submission.
Suggested abstract revision:
We compute the branching ratio B(p→e+pi0)/B(p→K+nu-bar) in a modular S'_4 SU(5) GUT
with 5_H + 45_H Higgs sector at the CM-anchored attractor tau_* ~= i. The Higgs-only
ratio B_Higgs = f^{uu}(tau_*)/f^{uc}(tau_*) ~= 1.077 is a modular-geometry prediction
independent of the Yukawa coupling kappa_u. Including coherent gauge X,Y exchange at
M_GUT yields a window-dependent ratio in [0.98, 5.54] depending on the gauge-Higgs
interference phase; in the constructive-leaning phase at M_{T45} ~= 10^14 GeV we obtain
a central forecast of ~= 2.06. We disclose that lifting B_Higgs ~= 1.077 to ~= 2.06
requires a Y_5-Y_{45} interference fine-tuning of order 1/4000 at M_{T45}, which we
label 'modular naturalness' but do not derive from first principles. The primary
falsifier is Hyper-Kamiokande (2030+) and DUNE Phase II (2035+). The B-ratio window
[0.98, 5.54] is the honest theoretical range prior to phase measurement.
