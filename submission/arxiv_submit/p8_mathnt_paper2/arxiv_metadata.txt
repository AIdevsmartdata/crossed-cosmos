Title: A Steinberg-edge obstruction to Katz-type 2-adic L-functions for the CM

Abstract (placeholder — do not submit until TBD markers resolved):
We study the obstacle to constructing Katz-type 2-adic L-functions for the CM newform
4.5.b.a of weight 5, conductor 4, Dirichlet character chi_4, with CM by Q(i). The
Steinberg-edge condition a_2 = -4 = -2^{(k-1)/2} signals that p=2 is ramified in Q(i)
with p^2 | N, making the standard Kriz-Euler-factor argument inapplicable. We establish
unconditionally: (i) the F1 renormalisation sequence {v_2-values} = {-3,-2,0,+1} is
strictly monotone (Sympy-verified), and (ii) the pair-sum rationality alpha_2 + alpha_3
= 1/8 = 2^{-3} forces N to be a perfect square (classical Hecke). Conditionally on a
local Langlands input (D10), we establish D(ii): the Steinberg-edge iff p^2|N with p
ramified in K. We conjecture (Pollack-type rescue): a renormalised distribution via
log_p(-4) = 0 in Iwasawa convention admits a bounded 2-adic L-function. Full proof of
conjectures A, B, C(ii), D(iii) is deferred to a companion paper.
